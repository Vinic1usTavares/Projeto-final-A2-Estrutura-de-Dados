lord lonsdale challenge belt commonly known as lonsdale belt is oldest championship belt in british professional boxing hugh lowther 5th earl of lonsdale introduced prize on behalf of national sporting club nsc intending it to be awarded to british boxing champions arthur frederick bettinson manager of nsc introduced terms and conditions regarding holding of belt which ensured its lasting prestige freddie welsh won first lonsdale belt in 1909 after winning nsc british lightweight title heavyweight henry cooper was first and only boxer to win three lonsdale belts during his 17 year professional career in 1929 british boxing board of control bbbofc assumed responsibility for awarding belt which continues to be awarded to british champions since then only six boxers have won two lonsdale belts each outright since 1934 which led to bbbofc introducing more stringent rules of attainment in 1980s and 1990s last winner of two belts was clinton mckenzie in 1987 lonsdale belt is a coveted prize with great monetary and sentimental value finding homes in private collections museums and has been auctioned for large sums of money belts have been stolen on numerous occasions none of which have ever been found since 1909 only 161 boxers have won a lonsdale belt outright across all weights in 2013 bbbofc in a move to further acknowledge esteem held for outright lonsdale belt
