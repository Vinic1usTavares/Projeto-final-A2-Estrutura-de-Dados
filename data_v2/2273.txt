mae carol jemison born october 17 1956 is an american engineer physician and former nasa astronaut she became first black woman to travel in space when she served as a mission specialist aboard space shuttle endeavour jemison joined nasa s astronaut corps in 1987 and was selected to serve for sts 47 mission during which she orbited earth for nearly eight days on september 12 20 1992 born in alabama and raised in chicago jemison graduated from stanford university with degrees in chemical engineering as well as african and african american studies she then earned her medical degree from cornell university jemison was a doctor for peace corps in liberia and sierra leone from 1983 until 1985 and worked as a general practitioner in pursuit of becoming an astronaut she applied to nasa jemison left nasa in 1993 and founded a technology research company she later formed a non profit educational foundation and through foundation is principal of 100 year starship project funded by darpa jemison also wrote several books for children and appeared on television several times including in an episode of star trek next generation she holds several honorary doctorates and has been inducted into national women s hall of fame and international space hall of fame early life and education mae carol jemison was born in decatur alabama on october 17 1956 youngest of three children of ludwig van beethoven elementary school in chicago illinois family first lived in woodlawn and later morgan park neighborhoods jemison knew from a young age that she wanted to study science and someday go into space television show star trek and in particular african american actress nichelle nichols portrayal of lieutenant uhura further stoked her interest in space jemison enjoyed studying nature
