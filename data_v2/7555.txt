christopher greenup c 1750 april 27 1818 was an american politician who served as a u s representative and third governor of kentucky little is known about his early life first reliable records about him are documents recording his service in revolutionary war where he served as a lieutenant in continental army and a colonel in virginia militia after his service in war greenup helped settle trans appalachian regions of virginia he became involved in politics and played an active role in three of ten statehood conventions that secured separation of kentucky from virginia in 1792 he became one of state s first representatives and served in kentucky general assembly before being elected governor in a race where due to his immense popularity he ran unopposed greenup s term in office was marred by accusations that he had participated in burr conspiracy to align kentucky with spain prior to former s separation from virginia but he vigorously and successfully rebutted these charges following his term as governor he became less active in political arena he died on april 27 1818 greenup county kentucky and its county seat were both named in his honor early life in virginia christopher greenup was most likely born in fairfax county virginia around 1750 harrison p 388 his parents were john and elizabeth witten greenup encyclopedia of kentucky p 72 his early education was attained at local schools of area he learned surveying and studied law under colonel charles binns at charles city county virginia nga bio during revolutionary war he first served as a lieutenant on continental line and later attained rank of colonel in virginia militia trowbridge kentucky s military governors in 1781 greenup helped settle area now known as lincoln county kentucky where he spent time as a surveyor and a land speculator harrison pp 388 389 he was admitted to practice law in county court in 1782 hopkins p 12 following virginia s creation of kentucky county in 1783 he was admitted to bar of district court of harrodsburg and served as clerk from 1785 to 1792 in 1783 he became one of original trustees of transylvania seminary later to become transylvania university he purchased two lots of land in lexington and served as clerk of town s trustees in 1785 greenup was elected to represent fayette county for a single term in virginia house of delegates during his service he was appointed to a committee with benjamin logan and james garrard to make recommendations on ways to further divide area that would become kentucky everman p 4 committee was also responsible for revising acts and surveys related to land and water surveys in area committee ultimately recommended creation of three new counties bourbon madison and mercer when mercer county was created later that year greenup was appointed a justice there during this time greenup continued to practice law in fayette county and pursued various other interests he was a founding member of danville political club and in 1787 he joined kentucky society for promoting useful knowledge future kentucky governors isaac shelby and james garrard as well as future supreme court justice thomas todd were also members of society everman p 20 in 1789 he helped organize kentucky manufacturing society later he was appointed to kentucky river company a group dedicated to improving infrastructure on kentucky river on july 9 1787 during a brief return to virginia greenup married mary catherine cathy pope of hanover county virginia couple had two children nancy and william political career in kentucky greenup served as clerk of first kentucky statehood convention in danville in 1784 he was elected as a delegate to second and sixth statehood conventions in 1785 and 1788 respectively and was a trustee of city of danville in 1787 powell p 18 h e everman biographer of fellow delegate james garrard noted that despite greenup s excellent legal background and legislative experience his lack of oratorical skills prevented him from taking more of a leadership role in conventions everman p 26 when kentucky was admitted to union in 1792 greenup moved to frankfort where he was rewarded for his efforts on behalf of state by being chosen as an elector for state s senators and governor he also served in first kentucky senate hopkins p 13 following this he was appointed to court of oyer and terminer but resigned immediately to accept a seat in u s house of representatives harrison p 389 he was one of kentucky s first two representatives in house and was elected to three successive terms serving from november 9 1792 to march 3 1797 in 1798 he was elected to kentucky house of representatives representing mercer county he also served as clerk of state senate from 1799 to 1802 greenup was a candidate for governor of kentucky in 1800 but was runner up to james garrard in a four man race that also included benjamin logan and thomas todd powell p 16 greenup garnered a majority of vote garrard nominated greenup everman p 76 greenup however intended to make another run at governorship and at his request garrard withdrew nomination days later greenup resigned his circuit judgeship on june 5 1804 to make another run for governor immensely popular he ran unopposed and served as governor from september 4 1804 to september 1 1808 during greenup s administration state chartered bank of kentucky and ohio canal company greenup became a director of former in 1807 despite his popularity however he was unable to pass much of his proposed agenda which included provision of public education and reforms to militia courts revenue system and penal system a partisan frankfort newspaper implicated greenup in burr conspiracy but he successfully defended himself and preserved his reputation he deployed kentucky militia along ohio river to defend state from any threat that might result from burr conspiracy but that threat had largely dissipated by 1807 on october 22 1807
