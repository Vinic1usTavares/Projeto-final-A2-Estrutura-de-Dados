chiming fountain also known as cupid s fountain john staehli fountain portland s city park fountain and washington park fountain is an outdoor cast iron fountain and sculpture built in 1891 by john hans staehli it is installed in washington park in portland oregon united states fountain s name derives from sound made when water drips from upper basin staehli designed fountain to serve as a watering trough for horses pulling carriages into park based on a renaissance fountain it was originally painted white and included a statuette of a boy possibly depicting cupid though figure was damaged and permanently removed from sculpture before or during 1940s fountain was restored in 1960 but no longer functioned its condition was deemed treatment urgent by smithsonian institution s save outdoor sculpture program in february 1994 since then its water pumping function has also been restored chiming fountain has been included in published biking and walking tours of portland and has been mentioned as a highlight of washington park in guides recommending family friendly activities in city description and history decorative fountain is located at junction of southwest sacajawea boulevard and southwest sherwood boulevard in main circle of washington park it was designed by john hans staehli a woodcarver from switzerland who immigrated to portland and was known for his decorative carvings of churches fountain was commissioned by city in 1891 for 400 and completed by staehli later that year becoming city s second piece of public art it served as a watering trough for horses pulling carriages into park avery cook fountain has been given many different names but is most commonly referred to as chiming fountain in reference to sound made by water dripping from upper basin thumb left upright detail of horned gargoyle like figures fountain is made of cast iron and measures approximately tall with a diameter it has a pedestal and two round bronze basins separated by a narrow post and stands in an octagonal concrete base which served as a retaining pool pedestal has four horned gargoyle like figures on bottom of pedestal s west side is an unsigned inscription which reads j staehli portland org 64 second st staehli s design is based on a renaissance fountain and was originally painted white central post originally held a figure of a boy carrying a staff or possibly a fish or a torch from which water spouted some sources suggest figure depicted cupid hence one of fountain s nicknames cupid s fountain according to portland parks recreation figure was last recorded in 1912 in a photograph of willis mcelroy s band in nearby bandstand however in 1920s freezing weather expanded water in fountain damaging figure boy statuette was removed from fountain and discarded by 1940s and it was not replaced thumb right fountain topped by a statuette of a boy in a 1901 photograph city planned to remove fountain in 1960 due to disrepair however mayor terry instructed parks bureau to begin a restoration after francis j murnane a local longshoreman and advocate for historic preservation appealed its removal much of its original decoration was absent so replicas of existing pieces were constructed at a cost of 450 restoration cost around 1 775 including assembly and installation despite its restoration fountain was no longer functional its condition
