dona marcela marino agoncillo marino y coronel june 24 1859 may 30 1946 was filipina renowned as principal seamstress of first and official flag of philippines gaining her title of mother of philippine flag marcela coronel marino was daughter of don francisco diokno marino and dona eugenia coronel marino a rich family in her hometown of taal batangas she finished her studies at santa catalina college marcela acquired her learning in music and feminine crafts at age of 30 marcela coronel marino married felipe encarnacion agoncillo a filipino lawyer and a jurist and gave birth to six children her marriage led an important role in philippine history when her husband was exiled in hong kong during outbreak of philippine revolution marcela marino agoncillo and rest of family joined him and temporarily resided there to avoid anti filipino hostilities of occupying spain while in hong kong general emilio aguinaldo requested her to sew flag that would represent republic of philippines dona marcela marino de agoncillo with her eldest daughter lorenza and a friend delfina herbosa natividad niece of dr jose rizal manually sewed flag in accordance with general emilio aguinaldo s design which later became official flag of republic of philippines while flag itself is perpetual legacy of dona marcela marino de agoncillo she is also commemorated through museums and monuments like marker in hong kong where her family temporarily sojourned at her ancestral home in taal batangas which has been turned into a museum in paintings by notable painters as well as through other visual arts early life marcela coronel marino was born on june 24 1859 in taal batangas philippines to don francisco diokno marino and dona eugenia coronel marino she grew up in her ancestral marino house in taal batangas built in 1770s by her grandparents don andres marino and dona eugenia diokno marino as a daughter of a rich and religious family marcela coronel marino was referred to in their town as which means a virgin enthroned in town church stories told in area related that people kept waiting patiently by church patio for her appearance in morning to attend mass accompanied either by a maid or an elder relative marcela coronel marino was sent to a convent after her education in manila convent she was studying in was santa catalina college of dominican nuns an exclusive school for girls established in walled city of intramuros where she finished her elementary and secondary education in college she learned spanish music feminine crafts and social graces she spent her girlhood partly in their hometown and partly in convent accordingly marcela was skilled in needlework thumb ancestral house of marcela marino de agoncillo in taal batangas marriage and family marcela in response to message written by general emilio aguinaldo dona marcela marino de agoncillo wrote following statement when she was interviewed post exile and death dona marcela marino de agoncillo and her daughters stayed in hong kong from 1895 to 1906 she took care of their house which became an asylum their funds had run out because of heavy expenses incurred by don felipe for his diplomatic activities in france and in united states she once had to sell children s and their jewels to support her family and to pay for their voyage back to manila other money was also used to help boost revolutionary funds their support for revolution made them an impoverished family however they gained it back when felipe resumed his legal practice after fall of first philippine republic and establishment of american regime dona marcela marino de agoncillo and her family ended their exile and went back to manila as soon as they were fetched by don felipe after his diplomatic activities abroad had ended agoncillos settled in their family house in malate after death of don felipe agoncillo s remaining family suffered from starvation due to their meager supply of food water and other needs japanese conquerors also contributed to their anguish during period of japanese invasion taking this all in stride marcela remained pragmatic and a source of inspiration after their house was incinerated during japanese occupation all she said to her remaining daughters was we will then have to go to taal though she endured 1945 battle of manila health of dona marcela marino de agoncillo who was alternatively called marcela and lola during her old age was steadily deteriorating she continued to mourn her deceased husband to such an extent that her daughters found it necessary to hide all his remaining photographs on may 30 1946 she quietly died in taal at age of 86 her mortal remains were brought from taal to manila and interred alongside her husband in catholic cemetery of la
