depicted in a ghent manuscript tewkesbury medieval festival is a medieval fair held over second weekend of every july near town of tewkesbury united kingdom its main feature is re enactment of battle of tewkesbury which was fought in 1471 located on parts of ground where original battle was fought festival also features a medieval camp in which traders ply their wares and visitors are entertained by musicians and acrobats largest medieval fair in united kingdom tewkesbury medieval festival was listed in footprint england as one of ten most bizarre festivals in country background tewkesbury medieval festival started in 1984 by len clatworthy as a simple fair with 10 stalls a beer tent and a small scale re enactment of battle of tewkesbury which was originally fought in 1471 near town of tewkesbury united kingdom festival became a regular celebration held over second weekend of every july on parts of original battlefield in 2005 organisers a group of local re enactors named companions of black bear registered event as a company to facilitate application of grants and their protection besides re enactment and camp festival offers guided walks of battlefield since its start event has expanded to become largest medieval fair in united kingdom attendance of festival is open to all but participation as a re is by invitation only sight of hundreds of men dressed as medieval soldiers and fighting in a battle led footprint england to list event as one of ten most bizarre festivals in country re enactment of battle of tewkesbury festival s main feature is its re enactment of battle of tewkesbury which was fought on 4 may 1471 between houses of york and lancaster engagement was a decisive victory for yorkists and their leader king edward iv forces of house of lancaster were decimated and their leaders killed or captured leaving edward as unchallenged ruler of england several lancastrians fled battlefield and sought sanctuary at tewkesbury abbey yorkists stormed abbey captured their foes and executed them re enactment at first festival was a simple affair compared to later years approximately 100 local enthusiasts kitted out in crude imitations of medieval gear fought each other on fields of tewkesbury they wore woollen chainmail or armour made of fibreglass their swords and pole arms were made from wood arrows were rolled up wallpaper as festival became more popular over years scale and quality of re enactment changed re enactors from other parts of united kingdom joined event as well as those from poland germany and other european countries armour and weapons used in later years were faithful steel reproductions that could cost thousands of pounds sterling ps by 2002 battle of tewkesbury was re enacted by approximately 2 000 men and women and british broadcasting corporation called next year s re enactment largest in europe aside from battle storming of tewkesbury abbey is also re enacted during festival subsequent trial and execution of prisoners are acted out in a mock fashion medieval camp since 2002 festival has been more than a small setup of stalls it has grown to be a camp of up to 120 stalls where life in medieval times is enacted by participants of battle and their families beer tent is a regular fixture and stalls peddle herbs sweets and food entertainers such as acrobats fire eaters and jugglers wander area performing their arts musicians and dancers also entertain visitors re enactors demonstrate pottery yarn spinning and other medieval craft these enthusiasts come from foreign countries such as belarus and hungary german rock band has performed several times at festival short bouts of simulated duels are performed in camp and a jousting tournament was also featured festival has attracted visitors from as far as new zealand and west coast of united states local tabloid quoted an estimate of 25 000 visitors to festival in 2003 town of tewkesbury although town council of tewkesbury plays no part in organising festival it has supported event by giving it grants amount for 2005 and 2008 was ps1 000 each most of money for organising festival is recouped from tithes paid by stall holders which at times proved insufficient to cover expenses event was operating in red for 2002 2004 under encouragement from companions of black bear and chamber of commerce town s traders support event by decorating their shops and dressing up in medieval period costumes pubs and restaurants serve medieval style fare as well a free bus service connects town and festival see also references external
