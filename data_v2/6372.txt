hms havock was an h class destroyer built for british royal navy in mid 1930s during spanish civil war of 1936 1939 ship enforced arms blockade imposed by britain and france on both sides as part of mediterranean fleet during first few months of second world war havock searched for german commerce raiders in atlantic ocean and participated in first battle of narvik during norwegian campaign of april june 1940 before she was transferred back to mediterranean fleet in may where she escorted a number of convoys to malta ship took part in battle of cape spada in july 1940 battle of cape matapan in march 1941 and evacuation of greece in april 1941 she was damaged during battle of crete following month but participated in syria lebanon campaign in june havock began escorting supply convoys in june to tobruk libya until ship was damaged in october she was repaired in time to escort a convoy to malta during first battle of sirte in december and was badly damaged by italian battleship littorio whilst protecting another convoy during second battle of sirte in march 1942 repairs were attempted in malta but ship was further damaged in an air raid in early april admiralty decided that further attempts to repair her at malta were pointless and ordered her to gibraltar for permanent repairs on 6 april while on passage to gibraltar havock ran aground near cape bon tunisia and her crew
