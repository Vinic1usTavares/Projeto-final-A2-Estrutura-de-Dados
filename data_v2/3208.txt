air vice marshal francis hubert frank mcnamara 4 april 1894 2 november 1961 was an australian recipient of victoria cross highest decoration for valour in face of enemy that can be awarded to a member of british and commonwealth forces serving with australian flying corps he was honoured for his actions on 20 march 1917 when he rescued a fellow pilot who had been forced down behind enemy lines mcnamara was first australian aviator and only one in world war i to receive victoria cross he later became a senior commander in royal australian air force raaf born and educated in victoria mcnamara was a teacher when he joined militia prior to world war i in 1915 he was selected for pilot training at central flying school point cook and transferred to australian flying corps following year he was based in middle eastern theatre with no 1 squadron when he earned victoria cross in 1921 mcnamara enlisted as a flying officer in newly formed raaf rising to rank of air vice marshal by 1942 he held senior posts in england and aden during world war ii retiring from air force in 1946 mcnamara continued to live in britain until his death from heart failure in 1961 early life born in rushworth
