robert white march 29 1759 march 9 1831 was a distinguished early american military officer lawyer judge and politician in u s state of virginia white represented frederick county in virginia house of delegates 1789 1792 and served as a judge of general court of virginia 1793 1831 prior to his political and law careers white served in virginia militia and continental army during american revolutionary war white was grandson of virginia pioneer settler and physician dr robert white 1688 1752 and thus a member of prominent white political family of virginia and west virginia he was nephew of united states house representative alexander white 1738 1804 and brother of united states house representative francis white 1761 1826 early life and education robert white was born on march 29 1759 in winchester virginia he was a son of john white one of justices serving on first bench of magistrates of frederick county court and his wife ann patton white white was also a grandson of dr robert white an early physician and pioneer settler of frederick county white received his primary education at a grammar school near marsh creek in pennsylvania under direction of reverend craighead a presbyterian minister at age of 16 white undertook a hiatus from his education to serve in american revolutionary war military career in 1775 white enlisted as a private in virginia militia company under command of captain hugh stephenson or stevenson which had been organized in berkeley county virginia present day jefferson county west virginia stephenson s company departed for boston campaign a few days before daniel morgan s company departed from winchester white along with stephenson s company departed on june 20 1775 from morgan s spring near shepherdstown and marched to boston to reinforce commander in chief of continental army george washington s forces who had besieged british army forces there while in boston white s chivalric bearing received attention of washington who saw in boy germ of that remarkable decision of character on march 17 1776 british forces withdrew from boston thus ending boston campaign in summer of 1776 white was elevated to rank of ensign white then fought as a lieutenant at battle of germantown on october 4 1777 under major william darke of berkeley county throughout spring of 1778 white was engaged in attacking british army detachments later in 1778 at short hills new jersey white suffered a fractured femur caused by a musket ball and received a wound to head from stock of a british army grenadier s musket following engagement at short hills white was taken as a prisoner of war by british forces white had fallen unconscious and was taken to tent of an amiable and accomplished british officer who had rescued him from death he was later exchanged and in autumn of 1778 white returned to winchester by slow and painful efforts exceedingly lame weak and emaciated by acute and protracted suffering his wounds had not yet healed by time of his return to winchester but following removal of bone fragments wound began to close white remained permanently physically disabled as a result of his injuries while not fully recovered from his wounds white received commission of a captain of cavalry in 1779 he commenced recruitment and training of american troops in philadelphia to fight in war but due to severity of his injuries he retired from military service at age of 20 and returned to winchester white was inducted as an original member of society of cincinnati for his military service during war law and political careers following his military service and return to winchester in 1779 white studied jurisprudence under his uncle alexander white one of most prominent lawyers practicing in shenandoah valley during course of his four years of law studies white read legal treatises of william blackstone and edward coke among others while lying on his back or propped up on a couch recovering from his injuries white was admitted to practice law at winchester in december 1782 after which he engaged in practice of law for eleven years white s health continued to improve and his law practice was an extensive and profitable one in a may 1837 biographical sketch of white published in southern literary messenger white was described as an able lawyer clear and cogent in argument but not eloquent his voice rather harsh and shrill and in impetuosity of debate his enunciation was sometimes affected even to stammering white maintained a lofty eminence within frederick county bar for over a decade during this time white served as a member of virginia house of delegates from 1789 to 1793 representing multi member district of frederick county white ran for election against matthew page and joseph holmes for seat in 1791 and won alongside page with 310 votes to page s 335 votes white ran for election to his seat in 1793 against page thomas buck and james singleton white received largest total of votes with a result of 388 judicial career and later life white was appointed as first judge serving virginia s tenth judicial district which was composed of five counties including hampshire county winchester was chosen by an act of virginia general assembly as center point of this judicial district where all judicial records were kept his appointment as a judge of tenth judicial district paid a meager compensation of 1 600 dollars per annum white was then appointed a judge of general court of virginia on november 16 1793 and he continued to hold that office until his death in 1831 in that period white served as president of general court of virginia for several years before his death serving on general court of virginia required white to travel to richmond in june and november of each year until 1825 white served as both a judge on general court of virginia and a judge of virginia s tenth judicial district during war of 1812 white took a dislike to encroachment of military power as winchester began to attract a sizable number of recruits going off to fight in war white refused to permit military officers to appear before him in his courtroom with their swords by their sides several of white s judicial opinions became well known as powerful specimens of sound learning and extensive research including case in which defendant was tried for murder and preston case in which there was a legal question regarding estoppel from his office white conducted teaching of jurisprudence to john buchanan a notable maryland jurist who later served in maryland house of delegates and as an associate justice of maryland court of appeals in spring of 1825 white was en route to serve on court of loudoun county and stayed night at a tavern along shenandoah river he was discovered by proprietor following morning suffering with paralysis white remained stricken with paralysis for several weeks and returned to his home in winchester on a litter his position as judge of tenth judicial district was assumed by two judges william and john scott white remained paralyzed for remainder of his life on july 1 1825 white gave power of attorney to his son john baker white for management of his affairs white later died at his residence in winchester on march 9 1831 personal life and family white married arabella baker daughter of john baker and his wife judith wood baker
