leslie halasz sabo jr hungarian halasz szabo laszlo 22 february 1948 10 may 1970 was a soldier in united states army during vietnam war he received highest military decoration medal of honor for his actions during cambodian campaign in 1970 born in kufstein austria sabo s family immigrated to united states when he was young and moved to ellwood city pennsylvania sabo dropped out of college and was drafted into u s army in 1969 becoming a member of 506th infantry regiment 101st airborne division on 10 may 1970 sabo s unit was on an interdiction mission near se san cambodia when they were ambushed from all sides by vietnam people s army sabo repeatedly exposed himself to north vietnamese fire protecting other soldiers from a grenade blast and providing covering fire for american helicopters until he was killed sabo was nominated for medal of honor shortly after his death but records were lost in 1999 a fellow vietnam war veteran came across records and began process of reopening sabo s nomination following several delays sabo s widow received medal of honor from president barack obama on 16 may 2012 42 years after his death biography early life sabo was born in kufstein austria on 22 february 1948 to elizabeth and leslie sabo sr who had been members of an upper class hungarian family leslie jr had one brother george who was born in 1944 as well as a second brother who had been killed in world war ii bombings at age of one with post world war ii occupation of hungary by soviet union sabo s family lost their fortune in war and upon realizing communism would be installed in hungary long term they left country permanently sabo family moved to united states in 1950 just after sabo turned two years old leslie sr who had previously worked as a lawyer attended evening classes to become an engineer in united states family moved to youngstown ohio and lived there for a short time before moving to ellwood city pennsylvania as leslie sr followed a job at knox corp growing up sabo s father stressed discipline and patriotism sabo graduated from lincoln high school in 1966 and briefly attended youngstown state university before dropping out and working at a steel mill for a short time he was described by friends and family as an affectionate and kind hearted hometown boy who was easygoing and always in good humor he enjoyed billiards and bowling military career thumb left sabo in 1969 holding an m 60 machine gun sabo was drafted into united states army april 1969 and sent to fort benning georgia for basic combat training while on leave he married rose sabo brown nee daughter of a world war ii veteran and silver star medal recipient whom he had met in 1967 he attended advanced individual training in september and october of that year followed by a honeymoon trip to new york city new york sabo was assigned to bravo company of 3rd battalion 506th infantry regiment u s 101st airborne division and was known to enjoy his time in military preferring environment of discipline and camaraderie in january 1970 sabo and his unit departed for vietnam to fight in vietnam war and he began corresponding with his wife regularly via letter unit came into contact with north vietnamese troops frequently for first several months of its deployment but most of these were small hit and run attacks on 5 may 1970 sabo s platoon was attached to u s 4th infantry division for a secret mission into cambodia and dropped into country on a uh 1 huey helicopter they were to conduct a series of interdiction missions against ho chi minh trail with assistance of heavy air support for five days they came into constant heavy contact with north vietnamese forces that were often of superior size medal of honor action on 10 may 1970 sabo s platoon was part of a force of two platoons from bravo company on a mission to se san cambodia they were to engage a force of north vietnamese army nva troops that had used area as a staging ground for tet offensive and other attacks there they were ambushed by a force of 150 nva troops hidden in jungle and trees which had caught american force in open and unprepared this battle became known as mother s day ambush sabo who was at column s end repeatedly repulsed efforts by north vietnamese to surround and overrun americans as battle continued a north vietnamese soldier threw a grenade near a wounded american soldier lying in open sabo ran out from a small tree that had been providing him cover and draped himself over his wounded comrade as grenade exploded then after absorbing multiple wounds from grenade blast sabo attacked enemy trench killing two soldiers with a grenade of his own and helped his injured ally to shelter of a nearby treeline later with americans running out of ammunition sabo again exposed himself to retrieve rounds from americans killed earlier in day sabo then began redistributing ammunition to other members of platoon including stripping ammunition from wounded and dead comrades as night fell north vietnamese refocused their efforts from wiping out american force to harassing helicopters that were carrying more than two dozen wounded soldiers as that was occurring remaining platoon from bravo company broke through north vietnamese lines and relieved other two platoons while first medical helicopter arrived and loaded two wounded soldiers under heavy fire sabo again stepped out into open and provided covering fire for helicopter until his ammunition was exhausted he received several serious wounds under heavy fire by north vietnamese while trying to reload although mortally wounded sabo crawled forward toward enemy emplacement pulled pin of a grenade and threw it at last possible second toward an enemy bunker resulting explosion silenced enemy bunker at cost of sabo s life in all seven other members of platoon were killed in this ambush and another 28 were wounded north vietnamese forces lost 49 subsequent recognition thumb
