stephen jones chamberlin 23 december 1889 23 october 1971 was a lieutenant general in united states army who served during world war ii as general of army douglas macarthur s assistant chief of staff g 3 staff officer in charge of plans and operations born in spring hill kansas on 23 december 1889 he was a 1912 graduate of united states military academy at west point new york during world war i he was aide de camp to major general david c shanks new york port of embarkation commander at hoboken new jersey for which he was one of twelve army officers who received navy cross after war he attended command and general staff school at fort leavenworth kansas and served on staff of chief of infantry in war department he attended army war college and was posted to staff of army s hawaiian division at fort shafter hawaii as assistant chief of staff g 3 in 1938 he became assistant chief of construction branch in g 4 division of war department general staff he became involved in vast construction program of arsenals depots airbases and coastal defenses as united states rearmed prior to its entry into world war ii in january 1942 he was sent to australia where he became assistant chief of staff g 3 at general macarthur s general headquarters ghq southwest pacific area in this role he was responsible for planning and overseeing execution of macarthur s major operations including new guinea philippines and borneo campaigns chamberlin was director of intelligence division g 2 on war department general staff from 1946 to 1948 when he became commander of fifth army he retired in september 1951 and was then employed as chief of security for us air force s arnold engineering development center at arnold air force base tennessee he died on 23 october 1971 early life stephen jones chamberlin was born in spring hill kansas on 23 december 1889 he was appointed to united states military academy at west point new york which he entered on 2 march 1908 and from which he graduated on 12 june 1912 chamberlin was commissioned a second lieutenant in 16th infantry which was then stationed at presidio of san francisco in may 1914 regiment moved to el paso texas he transferred to 8th infantry on 1 february 1915 and served at fort william mckinley in philippines he was promoted to first lieutenant on 1 july 1916 becoming a battalion adjutant on 14 october he was assistant to post quartermaster from 31 october 1916 to 1 january 1917 and then post exchange officer from 20 december 1916 to 9 june 1917 he was promoted to captain on 15 may 1917 and was acting regimental adjutant from 9 june to 1 september 1917 world war i with united states now involved in world war i chamberlin became aide de camp to major general david c shanks new york port of embarkation commander at hoboken new jersey chamberlin was also officer in charge of troop movements on 2 march 1918 chamberlin married shank s daughter sarah chapman at st bartholomew s episcopal church new york on corner of madison avenue and east 44th street in a simple ceremony attended only by sarah s sister katherine and captain maxwell sullivan as best man chamberlin was promoted to major on 7 june 1918 for his distinguished service in line of his profession as dispatch officer at port of embarkation hoboken new jersey he became one of only twelve army officers to receive navy cross during world war i he was also awarded distinguished service medal his citation read in september 1918 shanks was appointed commander of 16th division at camp kearny california and chamberlin was appointed division s assistant chief of staff to prepare for role he attended a course at army war college following armistice with germany he was sent on a tour of battlefields in france and belgium between wars in aftermath of world war i chamberlin was reduced in rank to captain on 9 february 1919 but was promoted to major again on 1 july 1920 a year later he was posted to panama canal zone initially as transportation officer and then with 33rd infantry on returning to united states in january 1922 he joined staff of 19th infantry brigade at fort mcpherson georgia he was transferred to 22nd infantry at fort benning georgia on 17 february 1923 from 1924 to 1925 he attended command and general staff school at fort leavenworth kansas graduating as an honor graduate duty then followed with third corps area from 30 june to 5 july 1925 with national guard at staunton virginia from 5 july 1925 to 1 july 1926 in office chief of infantry at washington d c and at camp perry ohio as publicity officer he served on staff of chief of infantry in war department from 1926 to 1930 and then commanded a battalion of 22nd infantry from 1930 to 1932 chamberlin attended army war college from july 1932 to june 1933 upon graduation he was posted to staff of army s hawaiian division at fort shafter hawaii serving as assistant chief of staff g 3 after more than 15 years as a major he was finally promoted to lieutenant colonel on 1 august 1935 on returning to united states in july 1936 he became assistant professor of military science and tactics for high schools of los angeles world war ii thumb right lieutenant chamberlin became assistant chief of construction branch in g 4 division of war department general staff at this time united states was embarking on a military buildup in response to a worsening international situation which culminated in outbreak of world war ii on 1 september 1939 a major component of this build up was a vast construction program of arsenals depots airbases and coastal defenses some 175 million was allocated to construction under expansion program as it became known on 7 may 1940 assistant chief of staff g 4 major general richard c moore g 4 of war department general staff asked for an estimate of cost to house an additional 1 200 000 men and balked at estimate he received of 800 per head to save on cost of cantonments moore decided not to paint them chamberlin disagreed on grounds that paint would reduce maintenance costs president franklin roosevelt intervened and directed that buildings be painted as a result an order was placed for of paint resulting in an 11 million budget shortfall chamberlin was promoted to colonel on 14 february 1941 commenting after war on construction program he wrote in january 1942 chamberlin was appointed assistant chief of staff g 4 of us army forces in australia arriving by air from washington d c on 9 january he soon became chief of staff of us army forces in australia first under major general julian f barnes and then under his successor lieutenant general george h brett chamberlin was promoted to brigadier general on 15 february 1942 general douglas macarthur arrived in australia on 17 march to become supreme commander of newly established southwest pacific area swpa which now included us army forces in australia on 19 april macarthur formally established his general headquarters ghq and chamberlin was appointed its assistant chief of staff g 3 as g 3 chamberlin was one of most highly rated members of ghq staff although not being part of bataan gang group of officers who had escaped with macarthur from philippines made him something of an outsider at ghq chamberlin was responsible for planning and overseeing execution of macarthur s major operations including new guinea philippines and borneo campaigns one member of staff later recalled chamberlin jealously guarded his position in late 1943 chamberlin differed with one of his planners brigadier general bonner fellers over a proposed landing at hansa bay fellers thought that hansa bay could be bypassed but chamberlin felt that this would be too risky while fellers was a newcomer to ghq he had known macarthur for many years and fellers took his proposal directly to macarthur who approved it a furious chamberlin had fellers fired from g 3 macarthur made him his military secretary one of chamberlin s challenges was working with australians their decentralized mode of planning was entirely different from top down approach used by ghq and chamberlin found this a source of frustration as it was difficult to extract information from them nonetheless he established a good working relationship with australian deputy chief of general staff lieutenant general frank berryman when macarthur began looking for a new chief of staff to replace lieutenant general richard k sutherland in 1945 he considered but rejected giving post to chamberlin chamberlin became deputy chief of staff in february 1946 and was briefly acting as chief of staff from 2 may to 10 june 1946 for his services in southwest pacific and occupation of japan chamberlin was awarded three army distinguished service medals and silver star in september 1946 he was one of five american major generals who was made an honorary commander of order of british empire in military division for his work with us army forces in australia and ghq swpa other four major generals were hugh j casey leif sverdrup lester whitlock and charles willoughby later life from june 1946 to october 1948 chamberlin was director of intelligence division g 2 on war department general staff he commanded fifth army from 1948 to 1951 receiving promotion to lieutenant general on 24 january 1948 in 1949 he was chairman of a general officer committee which researched role of race in army and produced a report favoring continuation of segregation and maintenance of a quota limiting number of african americans who could serve in uniform he retired in september 1951 and was then employed as chief of security for us air force s arnold engineering development center at arnold air force base tennessee he died at hoag memorial hospital in newport beach orange california on 23 october 1971 he was buried in section 3 site 1968 a wh of arlington national cemetery his wife sarah was subsequently interred with him in 1975 his papers are in us army
