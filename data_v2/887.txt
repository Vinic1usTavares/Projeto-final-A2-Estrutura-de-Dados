tintinhull garden located in tintinhull near yeovil in english county of somerset is a small 20th century arts and crafts garden surrounding a 17th century grade i listed house property is in ownership of national trust it is visited by around 25 000 people per year house started as a small farmhouse in 1630 but was enlarged into its current form in 18th century house was property of napper family for centuries it was given to national trust in 1954 arts and crafts style garden is modeled on that at hidcote manor garden in gloucestershire it was originally laid out by phyllis reiss and developed by penelope hobhouse house original farmhouse which forms basis of current tintinhull house was built of hamstone 1630 it was reshaped and enlarged around 1722 when west facade was added house was property of napper family who acquired manor after dissolution of monasteries and also owned tintinhull court and was passed down in family until they sold it sometime after 1814 let it to pitt family until death of john napper in 1791 it passed through several hands until 1835 when it was bought by jeremiah penny in 1898 then owner arthur cobbett added a single storey extension to east front before selling it to his tenant botanist dr s j m price in 1933 it was bought by phyllis reiss and her husband capt f e reiss house includes several paintings from national trust s collection including a painting of front of house by john s goodall house is a grade i listed building and can be booked for holiday lets from national trust garden thumb left part of garden garden is laid out into areas separated by walls and hedges garden layout was developed in early 20th century by dr price including laying down triangular and diamond shaped flagstone paths ornamental domes of box were planted in 1920s early landscaping was expanded and planted starting in 1933 by phyllis reiss in an arts and crafts hidcote style garden is separated into rooms by yew hedges and walls different areas include eagle court former courtyard middle garden fountain garden and pool garden pool garden is site of a former tennis court in 1954 reiss gave house and garden to national trust but continued to live in house and care for garden until her death in 1961 from then on trust let house to a variety of tenants including garden designer and writer penelope hobhouse and her husband prof john malins from 1980 to 1993 gardens are included in national register of historic parks and gardens and include small pools and an garden see also list of grade i listed buildings in south
