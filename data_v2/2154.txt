rochdale cenotaph is a first world war memorial on esplanade in rochdale greater manchester in north west of england designed by sir edwin lutyens it is one of seven memorials in england based on his cenotaph on whitehall in london and one of his more ambitious designs memorial was unveiled in 1922 and consists of a raised platform bearing lutyens characteristic stone of remembrance next to a pylon topped by an effigy of a recumbent soldier a set of painted stone flags surrounds pylon a public meeting in february 1919 established a consensus to create a monument and a fund for families of wounded servicemen meeting agreed to commission lutyens to design monument his design for a bridge over river roch was abandoned after a local dignitary purchased a plot of land adjacent to rochdale town hall and donated it for site of memorial lutyens revised his design and edward stanley 17th earl of derby unveiled memorial on 26 november 1922 it is a grade i listed structure having been upgraded in 2015 when lutyens war memorials were declared a national collection background in aftermath of first world war and its unprecedented casualties thousands of war memorials were built across britain almost all towns and cities erected some form of memorial to commemorate their fallen mayor of rochdale called a public meeting on 10 february 1919 three months after armistice to discuss proposals for town s commemorations consensus was that town should have a monument and a fund to provide for wounded servicemen their families and families of 2 000 war dead from rochdale public
