zenas randall bliss april 17 1835 january 2 1900 was an officer and general in united states army and a recipient of medal of honor he formed first unit of seminole negro indian scouts and his detailed memoirs chronicled life on texas frontier he was father of rhode island lieutenant governor zenas work bliss bliss was a native of rhode island and graduated from west point in 1854 he served most of his thirty seven year career on texas frontier and served in union army during american civil war during civil war he was captured by confederate forces and held as a prisoner of war bliss received medal of honor for his actions while leading his regiment at battle of fredericksburg early life and military career bliss was born april 17 1835 in johnston rhode island to an upper middle class family his parents were zenas and phebe waterman randall bliss bliss p xiv he received an appointment to united states military academy at west point new york in july 1850 when he was only fifteen years old he graduated from west point in 1854 and served next six years in texas he was stationed at fort davis and fort quitman but his first assignment was as a brevet second lieutenant in 1st u s infantry regiment at fort duncan he was promoted to full rank of second lieutenant in 8th u s infantry on march 3 1855 and subsequently promoted to first lieutenant on october 17 1860 following outbreak of civil war he was promoted to captain on may 14 1861 civil war service when american civil war began on april 12 1861 bliss immediately saw action he was captured by confederate forces and spent eleven months as a prisoner of war first in san antonio texas and later in richmond virginia he was finally exchanged in april 1862 and sent back to union lines where he was commissioned as colonel of tenth rhode island infantry following month 10th rhode island served for only 90 days and was used in defenses of washington d c after 10th rhode island was discharged bliss assumed command of seventh rhode island infantry on august 21 1862 in october 1862 seventh rhode island joined first brigade second division ninth corps army of potomac war of rebellion i vol 19 pt 2 pp 368 369 regiment saw action in december 1862 at battle of fredericksburg during which bliss performed actions that earned him medal of honor several decades later when ix corps was sent west it participated in siege of vicksburg seventh served under major general william t sherman in capture of jackson mississippi in april 1864 seventh rejoined army of potomac bliss became commander of first brigade second division ninth corps his brigade fought in battle of wilderness bliss was badly injured by a horse at spotsylvania but he returned to lead his brigade in siege of petersburg and battle of crater court of inquiry following fiasco at crater censured bliss but he remained on duty during civil war bliss received a brevet honorary promotion to major dated december 13 1862 for gallantry and meritorious service in battle of fredericksburg he was later breveted to lieutenant colonel on may 7 1864 for gallantry and meritorious service in battle of wilderness bliss was mustered out of volunteer service on june 9 1865 and reverted to his regular army rank of captain he was awarded medal of honor on december 3 1898 for valor during battle of fredericksburg postbellum career bliss remained in regular army after civil war and was promoted to major of 39th infantry regiment on august 6 1867 he was promoted to lieutenant colonel of 19th infantry on march 4 1879 and to colonel of 24th infantry a unit with black soldiers and white officers on april 20 1886 bliss was promoted to brigadier general in regular army april 24 1895 and to major general on may 14 1897 he retired from army eight days later on may 22 including his four years at west point bliss had served 46 years 10 months and 22 days in uniform when he retired bliss was a member of district of columbia commandery of loyal legion and was assigned insignia number 2601 he was also a member of grand army of republic society of army of potomac and order of indian wars zenas bliss died in washington d c january 2 1900 at age 64 he is buried alongside his wife at arlington national cemetery at arlington virginia his grave can be found in section 1 lot 8 b bliss and his wife had four children two of whom lived to adulthood bliss pp xiv xv one of his sons was zenas work bliss 1867 1957 who served as lieutenant governor of rhode island from 1910 to 1913 there is a life sized portrait of general bliss at
