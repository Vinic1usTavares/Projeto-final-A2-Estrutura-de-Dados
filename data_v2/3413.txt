blas fajardo ople february 3 1927 december 14 2003 was a filipino journalist and politician who held several high ranking positions in executive and legislative branches of philippine government including as senate president from 1999 to 2000 and as secretary of foreign affairs from 2002 until his death perceived as a leftist nationalist at onset of his career in public service ople was in his final years a vocal supporter for allowing a limited united states military presence in philippines and for american initiatives in war on terror including 2003 u s invasion of iraq ople s most enduring role was his nineteen years as secretary later minister of labor and employment during administration of president ferdinand marcos when philippine labor laws were overhauled through enactment of labor code of philippines that he had helped author early life and career ople was born in hagonoy bulacan on february 3 1927 to felix antonio ople a craftsman who repaired boats and his wife fajardo he graduated valedictorian of his grade school class at hagonoy elementary school in 1941 upon invasion of philippines by japan during world war ii he also had been to hagonoy institute during his secondary schooling teenage ople joined guerilla movement and fought under del pilar regiment and buenavista regiment of bulacan military area founded by santos in 1948 he finished his high school studies at far eastern university high school in manila he worked towards a degree in liberal arts at educational center of asia formerly quezon college in manila after graduation ople pursued a career in journalism he became a desk editor at daily mirror and author of its jeepney tales column still in his twenties ople was one of youngest newspaper columnists of that era ople also established a public relations consulting firm he soon became known for his nationalist views he co founded kilusang makabansa national progress movement an organization which frequently spoke out on issues of nationalism and social justice in 1950s in 1953 he joined magsaysay for president movement a volunteer group supporting presidential campaign of ramon magsaysay heading its executive planning committee and working as a speechwriter for candidates of nacionalista party after magsaysay s election he joined government as special assistant to secretary of labor and technical assistant on labor and agrarian affairs secretary of labor in 1965 ople was appointed as social security commissioner by president ferdinand e marcos in 1967 he was appointed secretary of labor and employment in 1978 position was renamed minister of labor and employment he resigned briefly in 1971 to run an unsuccessful campaign for election to philippine senate but was re appointed to his post in 1972 retaining position until 1986 at time of his appointment ople was perceived as a leftist nationalist his leftist credentials were enhanced when he co founded in 1972 philippine soviet friendship society jr p 387 as labor secretary ople was instrumental in framing of labor code of philippines which codified labor laws of country and introduced innovations such as prohibiting termination of workers without legal cause ople instituted labor policies institutionalizing technical education of workers in 1976 ople initiated a program for overseas employment of filipino workers it was during his tenure at labor that philippine overseas employment administration and overseas workers welfare administration were created ople obtained recognition from international labour organization during his stint as labor minister in 1975 he was elected president of 60th international labour conference of ilo first filipino to hold that post in 1983 that organization awarded ople a gold medal of appreciation he was a close adviser of president marcos though he was not later to be associated with corruption of marcos government and was perceived as not corrupt he created international headlines in december 1984 when he admitted to press that lupus stricken marcos was incapacitated to point of being unable to take major initiatives and that president s illness had placed philippines in a kind of interregnum marcos responded a few days later by baring his chest to his cabinet before television cameras to dispel rumors that he was seriously ill or had undergone surgery in 1978 ople was elected an assemblyman of interim batasang pambansa representing central luzon and reelected in 1984 during 1986 presidential elections ople served as a political campaign manager of president marcos who was running against corazon aquino shortly before outbreak of 1986 people power revolution marcos dispatched ople to washington d c to lobby american government on behalf of president ople was in washington d c upon outbreak of revolt and was advised by u s secretary of state george p shultz to call on marcos to resign ople publicly reiterated his support for marcos in american media in such fora as on this week with david brinkley 1986 constitutional commission member following success of people power revolution and installation to presidency of corazon aquino ople was relieved of his cabinet post ople returned to philippines and immediately attempted to position himself as leader of political opposition against aquino nonetheless in may 1986 ople accepted an offer by president aquino to serve in constitutional commission that drafted a new philippine constitution in 1987 congressional elections ople ran a second time for philippine senate under banner of nationalist led grand alliance for democracy coalition he was defeated in this attempt and returned to private life serving as chairman of institute for public policy a policy research institute senator of philippines thumb blas ople in 1992 he ran again for senate under laban ng demokratikong pilipino he was elected to a six year term in senate ople served as chairman of foreign relations committee and on commission of appointments he became senate president pro tempore in 1998 ople won a re election for senate in 1998 under laban ng pilipino in 1999 upon resignation of terminally ill marcelo ople became president of senate in that capacity he was a key proponent of 1999 visiting forces agreement between philippines and united states which allowed american forces to enter philippines for short term training exercises he yielded senate presidency in 2000 to franklin drilon later that year he sat as one of senator judges in impeachment trial of his ally president joseph estrada he was one of eleven votes during trial that successfully voted to block opening of an envelope that was believed to contain proof of corruption charges against estrada public anger over senate vote triggered edsa revolution of 2001 leading to ouster of estrada and accession of vice president gloria macapagal arroyo to presidency secretary of foreign affairs in july 2002 president gloria macapagal arroyo appointed ople a member of political opposition in senate as secretary of foreign affairs in her cabinet appointment was with some controversy weeks earlier arroyo s hand picked vice president had resigned as foreign affairs secretary after voicing disagreement with plan of philippine and united states governments to allow american troops to help combat islamic terrorist groups such as abu sayyaf as part of post 9 11 war on terror ople who had earlier been a vocal supporter of 1999 visiting forces agreement was perceived to be more amenable to plan in addition left wing labor activists denounced appointment of ople citing his marcos era role in promoting overseas employment of filipino workers which they said had resulted in abuses inflicted on filipino workers abroad during his stint as secretary of foreign affairs ople was at forefront of negotiations that led to deployment of american military forces inside philippines though he insisted that american troops would not participate in combat missions under his watch american and filipino governments signed an agreement that provided immunity to each other s citizens facing charges before international tribunals such as international criminal court ople was also a vocal supporter of iraq war and pushed for deployment of a small filipino contingent in iraq he predicted in november 2003 baghdad will be transformed from a symbol of brutal despotism to a new shining symbol of human freedom sacrifices invested in liberation of iraq to which filipinos made a significant contribution will be fully vindicated and
