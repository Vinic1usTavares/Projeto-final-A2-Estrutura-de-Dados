david john shannon dso bar dfc bar 27 may 1922 8 april 1993 was an australian bomber pilot of world war ii known for his part in dambusters raid on night of 16 17 may 1943 born in south australia shannon joined royal australian air force raaf in 1941 and learned to fly under empire air training scheme after further training in united kingdom he was posted to no 106 squadron raf operating avro lancaster heavy bombers and was awarded distinguished flying cross dfc in january 1943 in march 1943 shannon was selected by no 106 squadron s commanding officer wing commander guy gibson to join newly formed no 617 squadron for operation chastise attack on dams of ruhr valley awarded distinguished service order dso following raid shannon continued to fly with no 617 squadron until october 1944 during which time he earned bars to his dso and dfc he was then assigned to transport duties first with no 511 squadron and then in march 1945 with no 246 squadron ranked squadron leader shannon was demobilised after war and remained in england becoming an executive with shell he died in south london in 1993 aged seventy early life david john shannon was born on 27 may 1922 at unley park
