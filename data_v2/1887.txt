encyclopedia of central intelligence agency is a 2003 book by w thomas smith jr it is an encyclopedic work on central intelligence agency cia only independent agency of united states federal government that is tasked with intelligence gathering work chronicles history of agency from its founding in 1947 through war on terror which began after september 11 2001 encyclopedia s chronology ends in 2003 it provides approximately 550 entries across 282 pages on topics including notable contributors intelligence operations historical events and depictions of cia in fictional media encyclopedia was praised by school library journal for its reference value and comprehensiveness booklist recommended encyclopedia be placed in academic public and high school libraries as a helpful resource publishers weekly was critical of author s objectivity suggesting that encyclopedia was biased in favor of agency s intelligence operations but concluded that book was still a useful reference tool background encyclopedia of central intelligence agency is written by w thomas smith jr prior to authoring work he served in united states marine corps as a paratrooper and leader within infantry smith worked as a journalist for star reporter in columbia south carolina and subsequently taught journalism at university of south carolina smith gave an interview to columbia newspaper state about his book and his interest in researching history of central intelligence agency he explained that he was long interested in researching military history and was initially asked by american society of journalists and authors to put together a monograph on 1944 invasion of normandy by western allied forces during operation overlord in world war ii his literary representative later telephoned him to ask if he would instead be interested in writing an encyclopedia detailing history of cia smith agreed as he felt cia was highly relevant to military history publication history
