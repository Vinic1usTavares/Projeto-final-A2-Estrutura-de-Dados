five days at memorial life and death in a storm ravaged hospital is a 2013 non fiction book by american journalist sheri fink book details aftermath of hurricane katrina at memorial medical center in new orleans in august 2005 and is an expansion of a pulitzer prize winning article written by fink and published in new york times magazine in 2009 it describes events that took place at memorial medical center over five days as thousands of people were trapped in hospital without power triage system put into effect critically ill patients for evacuation and it was later alleged that a number of these patients were euthanized by medical and nursing staff shortly before entire hospital was evacuated on fifth day of crisis fink examines legal and political consequences of decision to euthanize patients and ethical issues surrounding euthanasia and health care in disaster scenarios book was well received by most critics and won three awards including a national book critics circle award for non fiction book was set to be basis of third season of fx anthology true crime series american crime story before being scrapped http deadline com 2017 08 ryan murphy katrina based on five days at memorial book scott rudin producer sarah paulson star background five days at memorial originated as a 13 000 word magazine article titled deadly choices at memorial published by new york times magazine in august 2009 fourth anniversary of hurricane katrina story focused on events that unfolded in new orleans memorial medical center now baptist medical center when hospital was flooded and its generators failed in aftermath of katrina drawing particular attention to euthanasia of numerous patients by medical and nursing staff fink was drawn to subject matter because of her experience as a doctor working in areas of conflict and as a journalist reporting on hospitals in war zones article which was a joint assignment for propublica and new york times magazine drew on two years worth of research and interviews with 140 people and won 2009 pulitzer prize for investigative reporting while she was writing deadly choices at memorial fink decided to expand article into a book since she kept finding out new facts and trying to fit them into story because they seemed essential she was encouraged by her editor to save extra material to publish in a book expanding on her original research fink conducted over 500 interviews with people who were at hospital during disaster families of dead patients hospital executives law enforcement officials and ethicists she wished to interview dr anna pou one of principal characters of story about allegations of euthanasia but pou refused based on her lawyer s advice fink said that while some of medical and nursing professionals were observing a code of silence she was impressed by openness of several staff members including two doctors who talked freely of their decision to euthanize their patients fink also reviewed photographs videos emails and diary entries produced at time and consulted weather reports and hospital s floor plans content book is divided into two parts first part titled deadly choices focuses on events that occurred at memorial medical center over five days referred to in book s title august 28 september 1 2005 during these five days memorial s emergency plans proved inadequate as hospital lost power and its back up generators had failed leaving it without lights air conditioning sewer systems and essential medical equipment thousands of staff patients and evacuees were trapped by floodwaters inside building awaiting evacuation by boat or helicopter fink describes unconventional method of triage adopted by medical staff whereby ambulatory patients were prioritized for evacuation and those with do not resuscitate orders were placed last in list patient evacuation began on third day and progressed slowly until fifth day when some medical staff decided to hasten deaths of critically ill patients believing they would not survive with lethal injections of morphine story is described from perspective of several participants pieced together from interviews emails and phone logs thumb left memorial medical center in january 2006 still closed after hurricane katrina second part titled reckoning discusses legal and political ramifications of memorial s response to crisis and especially decisions to euthanize patients in total 45 patients died before hospital was evacuated and 23 were identified as having concentrations of morphine and other drugs in their tissues fink focuses largely on investigation into actions of dr anna pou and two intensive care nurses cheri landry and lori budo all three of whom were charged with second degree murder following allegations that they had administered lethal doses of morphine to some patients public s sympathy lay largely with three accused memorial staff charges against landry and budo were eventually dropped and a grand jury chose not to indict pou in 2007 fink discusses ethical issues surrounding events at memorial as well as those involved in disaster settings and euthanasia in general a brief epilogue critiques health care protocols in disasters and uses example of hurricane sandy in 2012 to illustrate lack of change undertaken by hospitals in response to disaster caused by katrina and a failure of us government to enforce standards for emergency preparedness reception critical reviews of five days at memorial were mostly positive jason berry of new york times commended shimmering intelligence
