civil service rifles war memorial is a first world war memorial located on riverside terrace at somerset house in central london england designed by sir edwin lutyens and unveiled in 1924 memorial commemorates 1 240 members of prince of wales own civil service rifles regiment who were killed in first world war they were territorial force reservists drawn largely from british civil service which at that time had many staff based at somerset house both battalions of expanded civil service rifles were disbanded shortly after war regiment amalgamated with queen s westminster rifles but former members established an old comrades association to keep regiment s traditions alive association began raising funds for a war memorial in 1920 and prince of wales unveiled memorial on 27 january 1924 it takes form of a single rectangular column surmounted by a sculpture of an urn and flanked by painted stone flags union flag on one side and regimental colour on other base on which column stands is inscribed with regiment s battle honours while an inscription on column denotes that a scroll containing names of fallen was placed inside memorial first stood in quadrangle of somerset house which civil service rifles had used as a parade ground but civil service began to vacate somerset house towards end of 20th century as building and its courtyard were re purposed memorial was moved to riverside terrace in late 1990s members of regiment continued to attend remembrance sunday ceremonies until at least late 1980s by which time many former members were in their nineties last known surviving member of regiment attended a rededication ceremony in 2002 memorial was designated a grade ii listed building in 1987 which was upgraded to grade ii in november 2015 when it became part of a national collection of lutyens war memorials background in aftermath of first world war and its unprecedented casualties thousands of war memorials were built across britain amongst most prominent designers of memorials was sir edwin lutyens described by historic england as leading english architect of his generation lutyens established his reputation designing country houses for wealthy clients but war had a profound effect on him and following it he devoted much of his time to memorialising its casualties he became a public figure through his design for cenotaph on whitehall which became britain s national war memorial this along with his work for imperial war graves commission led to commissions for war memorials across britain and empire as well as memorials for towns and cities lutyens was commissioned to design memorials for several private companies and regimental associations including civil service rifles these were among least controversial of lutyens war
