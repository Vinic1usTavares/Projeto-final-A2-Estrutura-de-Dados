robert kweku atta gardiner 29 september 1914 13 april 1994 was a ghanaian civil servant university professor and economist who served as executive secretary of united nations economic commission for africa from january 1962 until october 1975 and as commissioner for economic planning of ghana from october 1975 until may 1978 gardiner was born in 1914 in kumasi gold coast into a family of successful merchants after attending adisadel college he received his education in sierra leone and in united kingdom following completion of his studies gardiner became a professor of economics at fourah bay college from 1946 until 1948 he served as an area specialist for un trusteeship council in 1949 he was hired as first director of extramural studies at university college in ibadan nigeria four years later he returned to gold coast to serve in higher levels of civil service following country s independence as ghana in 1957 gardiner was appointed by prime minister kwame nkrumah to be establishment secretary two had a tenuous working relationship as nkrumah s idealism conflicted with gardiner s practical style of administration following his dismissal in 1959 gardiner accepted his nomination as deputy executive secretary of un economic commission for africa uneca in 1960 civil war erupted in newly independent republic of congo and gardiner became heavily involved in un efforts to resolve situation serving as a mediator between various factions and as a close adviser to un secretary general u thant on matter in january 1962 gardiner was appointed executive secretary of uneca in that capacity he oversaw training of new african experts to staff organisation and promoted economic development throughout africa following his departure from un in 1975 gardiner returned to ghana to serve as commissioner for economic planning he resigned in 1978 his reputation as an economist damaged by instability of ghanaian government gardiner then engaged himself in various academic capacities until his declining health forced him to retire he died in 1994 biography early life and education robert kweku atta gardiner was born on 29 september 1914 in kumasi gold coast into fante tribe his double middle name translated from fante dialect means male twin born on wednesday he was one of eight children of phillip h d gardiner and nancy torraine ferguson both successful merchants his father died when he was two years old and one of his siblings died before reaching adulthood gardiner studied at st anglican school in kumasi before attending adisadel college in cape coast where in 1934 he was head prefect that year he won two school prizes for essays he had written he also attended fourah bay college in freetown sierra leone and university of cambridge s selwyn college graduating from latter in 1941 gardiner then studied at university of london and oxford university s new college at latter he worked under margery perham while examining constitutional development in gold coast during this time he became involved with west african students union and led its study group in march 1942 he delivered a lecture entitled west africa and war before royal institute of international affairs discussing relevance of world war ii to african continent that year royal african society appointed gardiner to international institute of african languages and cultures he also served on church of england s anglo african committee he proceeded to united states to tour american colleges by march 1943 he had visited 30 institutions on 24 july of that year he married linda charlotte edwards a jamaican painter they had two daughters charlotte and roberta and a son george early career after completing his studies gardiner secured a position as an economics professor at fourah bay college and helped to establish its department of commercial and social studies in late 1946 he assisted a british delegation at an international conference on education in paris later that year he was hired by ralph bunche to work as an area specialist for united nations trusteeship council with a focus on west africa in section of territorial research and analysis bunche also placed him on staff of special palestine commission he served as an area specialist until 1948 in october 1949 he was made first director of extramural studies at university college in ibadan nigeria making him only african department head at institution under his tenure department employed experimental methods to promote and develop its programs and by 1952 it was determined to be a success he resigned on 30 september 1953 gardiner subsequently joined gold coast civil service as director of department of social welfare and community becoming third african to be entrusted with a department in colony in that capacity he sought to establish more vocational schools throughout country following year he was made chairman of kumasi college of technology council in 1955 he was made permanent secretary of ministry of housing following gold coast s independence as ghana in 1957 prime minister kwame nkrumah appointed gardiner establishment secretary making him head of ghana civil service during gardiner s tenure two frequently clashed according to observers disagreements between them stemmed from their differing personalities gardiner was pragmatic while nkrumah was idealistic gardiner was often sought by lower level civil servants to shield them from political pressure he worked to balance demands for africanisation with professional competence in bureaucracy in his capacity as establishment secretary he drafted a protocol that allowed ghana to manage its own assets housed in british west african organisations until relevant neighboring colonies could achieve independence nevertheless nkrumah withdrew ghana s capital from groups with stated purpose of ensuring complete monetary independence from united kingdom in 1958 gardiner was serving as prime minister s chief of staff nkrumah considered dispatching him as high commissioner to pretoria south africa but idea was ultimately discarded in part due to difficulties in finding gardiner s children means to earn an education in region late in his tenure he dismissed his sister from her nursing position for an extended leave of absence straining his family ties he later said i had no choice her action was clearly contrary to regulations in 1959 gardiner was dismissed by nkrumah for unknown reasons in may he accepted a nomination to post of deputy executive secretary of un economic commission for africa uneca by taking job his political position in ghana was made neutral but his overall reputation in african continent was diminished as others saw his un role as an external imposition several african states that had close ties with nkrumah were also unenthusiastic about his discontent with prime minister gardiner moved to uneca s headquarters in addis ababa on 17 may but following outbreak of civil war in newly independent republic of congo he was appointed consultant in public administration to chief of civilian mission of un operation in congo known under its french acronym as onuc in august 1960 and tasked with helping rebuild congolese civil service he faced difficulty in his work because his ghanaian nationality aroused suspicions of congolese president joseph kasa vubu ghanaian government had offered enthusiastic support to kasa vubu s rival prime minister patrice lumumba following lumumba s dismissal in september gardiner asked to be replaced in november he resumed his work at uneca in march 1961 he returned to congo at behest of un secretary general dag hammarskjold to lead a secretariat mission in negotiating a deal concerning reorganisation of congolese army on 26 july gardiner was appointed director of public administration division at un department of economic and social affairs making him highest ranking african in un s staff while he worked in new york he also acted as foremost congo adviser to hammarskjold s successor u thant he played a key role in mediating negotiations that resulted in reconvening of congolese parliament and a ceasefire between un peacekeepers and armed forces of secessionist state of katanga on 26 january 1962 gardiner was appointed officer in charge of onuc and tasked with managing approximately 400 un civilian staff he flew into congo on 10 february to assume control of mission and subsequently garnered significant worldwide media attention in role situation in congo in january 1963 and on 1 may he ended his service executive secretary of uneca at same time as his appointment to onuc gardiner was named executive secretary of uneca but while he was in congo he did not assume any of responsibilities of post upon his return to addis ababa he worked to guard organisation from political controversies and operate it in a non partisan manner mindful of criticism of his predecessor abbas for being too passive gardiner promised that under his management uneca would take an active role in economic planning and development projects during his 12 year tenure in post he advocated for africanisation of uneca s staff and methods it utilised as he had few resources his first objectives upon entering office was to improve commission s data gathering abilities and to acquire qualified personnel latter goal was furthered by his establishment of three training paths for african personnel fellowship awards seminars and workshops and education at institutions program proved highly successful and by 1968 a total of 1 600 professionals had been trained as a result many countries were able to their statistical and demographic services he also used commission to promote smaller regional and subregional economic research training banking organisations following foundation of organisation of african unity oau in 1963 gardiner appealed to community for cooperation with uneca in furthering economic growth nevertheless gardiner s conservative approach and modest outlook for african development ran contrary to socialist leaning idealism of oau leadership and resulting rivalry between uneca and oau undermined inter organisational coordination and technical capability of latter for years in april may 1967 gardiner attended accra conference that resulted in eventual foundation of economic community of west african states he presented articles of association to conference for signature and advised that smaller west african states should attempt to integrate sections of their markets to open themselves up to a wider array of economic activities gardiner strongly endorsed construction of a lagos mombasa highway and on 1 july 1971 he established trans african highway bureau to oversee development of a continental road network he also played a key role in convincing states to sign african economic charter in 1973 partly due to his efforts to promote regional economic organisations ghana mollified its resistance towards west african integration in 1970s after being approached by british broadcasting corporation gardiner agreed to become first african to deliver reith lectures entitled a world of peoples his six broadcasts in 1965 discussed race relations following year he published a book under same name he also delivered gilbert murray memorial lectures in 1969 j b memorial lectures in 1970 and aggrey fraser memorial lectures in 1972 in 1969 ghanaian prime minister kofi busia offered him office of secretary of presidential commission and council of state gardiner subsequently wrote a letter of resignation to thant and made preparations to return to accra meanwhile members of busia s cabinet led by foreign minister victor owusu insisted that gardiner not be given post objecting to busia s ostensible intentions to groom him to be next president of ghana busia relented and sent a letter to thant expressing his government s wish that gardiner remain at un when gardiner was informed of decision he was deeply embarrassed and kept his post in 1972 he was dispatched to uganda where he attempted in vain to convince president idi amin to halt expulsion of asian minorities from country while at uneca he served as a mentor to fellow ghanaian and future un secretary general kofi annan concurrent academic and advisory activities following unanimous approval of governments of commonwealth of nations gardiner was appointed chairman of commonwealth foundation serving from 1970 until 1973 he was a visiting professor of economics at university of strathclyde from 1970 until 1975 he was also a visiting professor of economics and senior consultant to newly established centre for development studies at university of cape coast from 1974 until 1977 and chairman of board of directors of post and telecommunications corporation later he was a fellow of selwyn college ghana academy of arts and sciences and ghana institute of management and public administration between 1970 and 1975 gardiner was variously a member of advisory committee of council on world tensions council for scientific and industrial research of ghana third world foundation and un development planning committee gardiner also served as trustee or board member for dag hammarskjold foundation international institute of tropical agriculture and third world prize selection committee and sponsored minority rights group international return to ghana in october 1975 gardiner left uneca and returned to ghana on 14 october he was appointed commissioner for economic planning for supreme military council two years later government dispatched him to nairobi in an attempt to improve relations between kenya tanzania and uganda and salvage financially troubled east african railways and harbours corporation he also personally involved himself in anti corruption efforts in port of takoradi he resigned in may 1978 citing concern for his health according to africa confidential there were rumours that gardiner had resigned in an attempt to distance himself from ignatius acheampong s economically unstable regime his reputation as an economist was nevertheless tarnished afterwards he served a term as chairman of council of university of science and technology successor to kumasi college of technology and edited african development bank s 20th anniversary book african development bank 1964 1984 until his declining health forced him into retirement gardiner died early in morning on 13 april 1994 in accra ghana survived by his wife and children beliefs and personality in comparison to his ghanaian contemporaries gardiner was a conservative he believed that political and economic issues of transformation could be separated and strongly supported a capitalist model of development he thought increased productivity and higher wages would boost ghana s economy and believed that persistence of a trade deficit would contribute to inflation and national financial difficulties he expressed that improved transport and communication systems and rural education and health services as well as land redistribution participation in global trade and expanded job opportunity would contribute to development in africa gardiner also thought that african aid models were too reliant on foreign assistance and that such assistance should only be used to augment local development efforts he advocated for normalisation of relations between african states and white minority government of south africa believing former could benefit from economic strength of latter his successor at uneca secretariat adedeji felt gardiner lacked a creative outlook saying of him robert gardiner was a conservative economist who didn t see anything wrong with extant development paradigm gardiner was quiet and reserved in nature his two favorite sayings were complacency breeds arrogance and one s job can never be finished he enjoyed walking golfing reading and listening to music honours gardiner was awarded nine honorary degrees from universities in united states united kingdom and ghana over course of his life donald robinson included him in his 1970 book 100 most important people in world today at opening session of international conference for study of problem of developing countries in 1975 he was awarded a gold medal by italian president giovanni leone in recognition for his contributions to africa in march 1978 gardiner was inducted into order of volta with companion insignia for his service as commissioner for economic planning a collection
