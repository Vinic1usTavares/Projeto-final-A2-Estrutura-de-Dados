portman road is a football stadium in ipswich suffolk england which has been home ground of ipswich town f c since 1884 stadium has also hosted many england youth international matches and one senior england friendly international match against croatia in 2003 it staged several other sporting events including athletics meetings and international hockey matches musical concerts and christian events stadium underwent significant redevelopments in early 2000s which increased capacity from 22 600 to a current figure of 30 311 making it largest capacity football ground in east anglia its four stands have since been converted to all seater following recommendations of taylor report history ipswich played their early matches at broomhill park but in 1884 club moved to portman road and have played there ever since ground was also used as a cricket pitch during summer by east suffolk cricket club who had played there since 1855 cricket club had erected a pavilion first fixed building at ground more substantial elements of ground development did not begin for a further 11 years though ipswich became one of first clubs to implement use of goal nets in 1890 at this time ipswich were an amateur
