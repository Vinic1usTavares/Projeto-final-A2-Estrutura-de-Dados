alexander white 1738 september 19 1804 was a distinguished early american lawyer and politician in present day u s states of virginia and west virginia white served as an elected member of house of burgesses representing hampshire county and as a member of virginia house of delegates representing berkeley and frederick counties during american revolutionary war he facilitated release of quaker and hessian civilian prisoners held by patriots in 1788 white participated in virginia ratifying convention in which virginia ratified united states constitution he later served as inaugural member to represent virginia s 1st congressional district in united states house of representatives from 1789 to 1793 white was appointed by united states president george washington to serve as a commissioner on a board responsible for planning and construction of washington d c white served on board from 1795 to 1802 white was son of virginia pioneer settler and physician dr robert white 1688 1752 thus he was a member of prominent white political family of virginia and west virginia he was uncle of virginia judge robert white 1759 1831 uncle of united states house representative francis white 1761 1826 and brother in law of virginia governor james wood 1741 1813 early life and education alexander white was born in 1738 to dr robert white 1688 1752 and his wife margaret hoge at white hall near present day hayfield in orange county later part of frederick county following its creation that same year through his father white was of scottish descent and was raised in presbyterian faith white s father dr robert white had served as a surgeon in royal navy of kingdom of great britain dr white relocated to present day frederick county between 1732 and 1735 as a pioneer settler where he was one of two practicing physicians white was sent by his father to scotland to receive his education where he studied jurisprudence at university of edinburgh white continued his law studies in london england where he was admitted to inner temple on january 15 1762 and matriculated at gray s inn on january 22 1763 white completed his law studies during french and indian war political career white returned to virginia in 1765 where he began practicing law and became a prominent lawyer in shenandoah valley region white served as deputy king s attorney for frederick county in 1772 he engaged in a distinguished career as a lawyer with a national reputation house of burgesses white was elected as a member of house of burgesses in williamsburg from 1772 to 1773 representing hampshire county white served with patrick henry henry reportedly never cast his vote without first consulting with white white was an eloquent public speaker and due to his scottish presbyterian background he was strongly opposed to colonial government s preferential support of church of england in virginia because of this stance white presented a resolution before house of burgesses regarding separation of church and state according to historians hu maxwell and howard llewellyn swisher white is first man in what is now united states to present a resolution to a legislature regarding freedom of religion during his tenure in house of burgesses white served alongside james mercer who was also representing hampshire county following his term white was succeeded by joseph neville upon convening of first court of berkeley county on may 19 1772 white served as king s attorney for county white served one term in house of burgesses he was then appointed deputy king s attorney for colony of virginia in 1773 american revolutionary war while white did not engage in military service during american revolutionary war he remained active in practice of law in winchester throughout war s duration according to release upon condition that they affirm that they would henceforth live by their creed and be at peace with all men virginia house of delegates from 1782 to 1786 white was elected annually and served house of representatives white served two terms as inaugural member to represent virginia s 1st congressional district in united states house of representatives during 1st united states congress and 2nd united states congress march 4 1789 march 3 1793 white was first person residing in west virginia to occupy a seat in united states congress during his tenure white s congressional district spanned from harpers ferry to ohio river as he was previously known in house of burgesses white was reportedly one of more eloquently spoken members in first congress and was known for his remarkable punctuality white voted in favor of residence act united states federal law that settled question of locating capital of united states along potomac river in maryland and virginia three days after residence act became law united states house of representatives agreed upon funding act as part of compromise of 1790 to address issue of funding domestic debt on august 9 1790 funding act became law white voted in support of funding act however according to thomas jefferson white s vote was result of an argument that occurred at a dinner hosted by jefferson after which white and richard bland lee changed their votes in favor of funding act jefferson stated that white reluctantly supported bill with a revulsion of stomach almost convulsive votes of both white and lee carried funding act measure making enactment of residence act possible white was re elected to his seat in 1791 after defeating his opponents william darke and general james wood according to from may 1789 to march 1793 white missed 26 of 211 recorded votes or 12 3 white s voting participation was higher than median of 14 6 among lifetime records of representatives serving in march 1793 following adjournment of 2nd united to potomac river and improved its navigability for commerce later life and death white continued to practice law throughout his political career white died on october 9 1804 at his woodville estate in frederick county virginia he died without children thus leaving no descendants white was interred at wood family s glen burnie estate in winchester virginia his will was drafted on may 26 1804 and
