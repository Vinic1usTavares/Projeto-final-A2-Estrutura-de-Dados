right thumb old court new court controversy dominated term of kentucky governor joseph desha old court new court controversy was a 19th century political controversy in u s state of kentucky in which kentucky general assembly abolished kentucky court of appeals and replaced it with a new court justices of old court refused to recognize action as valid and for a time two separate courts operated as court of last resort for state controversy began when financial panic of 1819 left many kentuckians in debt and unable to meet their financial obligations a debt relief movement began in state and pro relief candidates won majorities in general assembly in 1820 assembly passed a law of replevin that was extremely favorable to debtors disgruntled creditors challenged constitutionality of law appealing their case to court of appeals court opined in favor of creditors attempts to remove anti relief justices failed pro relief legislature passed a measure abolishing court of appeals and replacing it with a new court to which pro relief governor joseph desha appointed pro relief justices who upheld replevin law as economic situation in state improved in second half of 1820s old court supporters regained control of both houses of general assembly they abolished new court and restored old court to power in an 1829 case court nullified decisions rendered by new court in a 1935 case court struck all new court cases from kentucky common law background a period of national prosperity followed end of war of 1812 in kentucky rapid population growth and strong demand for state s goods led to land speculation becoming a popular enterprise charter of kentucky insurance company in 1802 and bank of kentucky in 1806 made currency for loans plentiful establishment of branches of second bank of united states in louisville and lexington further augmented availability of credit in 1818 general assembly chartered 40 more state banks and later added six more klotter p 84 in late 1818 however demand for u s goods fell sharply in europe land values also began to fall touching off panic of 1819 many persons in state were unable to repay their loans a struggle began between creditors seeking to collect money owed to them and debtors seeking relief from obligations they could not meet harrison p 109 a debt relief party sprang up in state which had long been a single party bastion in 1819 governor gabriel slaughter agreed to repeal charters of 46 banks now known as forty thieves established by general assembly he concurred when general assembly abolished damages on disputed bills of exchange hopkins p 25 beginning of controversy in 1820 debt relief party gained majorities in both houses of general assembly metzmeier p 6 on february 11 1820 assembly passed a law of replevin or stay law that prevented creditors from seeking court order for payment of a debt for a period of one year they hoped that this would provide time for an economic recovery which would allow debtors to save their investments if creditor would not accept bank notes issued by bank of kentucky he was forced to wait an additional year to collect debt on november 29 1820 assembly chartered bank of commonwealth another source from which debtors could obtain money harrison p 110 creditors did not want to accept payment from either bank of kentucky or bank of commonwealth notes of former were depreciated due to a lack of capital and latter had no capital and no guarantee of state credit marburg p 331 in december 1820 assembly modified replevin law to state that creditors who would accept payment in notes from bank of commonwealth but not bank of kentucky would be forced to wait three months to collect on a debt wait was one year if creditor accepted only notes from bank of kentucky and it remained two years for creditors who would not accept notes from either by 1821 relief party had successfully ended practice of debt imprisonment in kentucky in december 1822 party became so dissatisfied with sound money practices of bank of kentucky that they revoked its charter williams v blair and lapsley v brashear forced to choose between accepting depreciated money in payment for outstanding debts or waiting long times to collect debts creditors turned to courts for relief in 1822 bourbon county circuit court judge james clark ruled in case of williams v blair that replevin law violated state and federal constitutions this ruling was so unpopular with relief party that they attempted to remove him from office but 59 35 vote fell just short of needed two thirds majority fayette county circuit court judge francis p blair issued a similar ruling in case of lapsley v brashear both cases were appealed to kentucky court of appeals then highest court in kentucky in 1823 in case of blair debt relief position was argued by george m bibb while anti relief position was represented by robert wickliffe in lapsley wickliffe joined george robertson and ben hardin to represent anti relief position while relief position was argued by john rowan and william t barry chief justice john boyle wrote majority opinion in blair on october 8 1823 and associate justice william owsley issued court s opinion in lapsley three days later in both cases anti relief position was upheld vanburkleo p 693 formation of new court assembly passed resolutions against all three justices on court of appeals but did not possess two thirds majority to remove them governor john adair a relief party supporter urged resistance framing issue as court impeding right of people to self govern his efforts drew a resolution against him from anti relief minority on november 8 1823 left thumb william t barry served as chief justice of new court frustrated by their defeats in judiciary relief party turned its attention to gubernatorial election of 1824 where they backed general joseph desha desha s election by overwhelming vote of 38 378 22 499 was seen by party as a mandate to pursue relief agenda an early proposal to reduce salaries of court of appeals justices to 25 cents per year was discarded without a vote but house of representatives mustered votes to remove offending justices marburg p 332 justices were spared removal when senate failed to pass measure with a two thirds majority vote was 23 12 on december 9 1824 senate voted to repeal law that had established court of appeals and to establish a new court of appeals with four justices measure came to house floor on december 23 during debate governor desha personally lobbied legislators to support measure a blatant violation of rules of house next day measure passed in house by a vote of 54 43 governor desha appointed pro relief stalwart william t barry as chief justice and three associate justices who were also relief party supporters harrison pp 110 111 existing court and anti relief party refused to recognize new court as valid achilles sneed clerk of old court refused a legislative mandate to turn over his records to new court by january 1825 francis blair new court clerk assembled a group that broke into sneed s office and took what records they could find harrison p 111 sneed was charged with contempt of court and fined for refusing to turn over records old court continued to meet in a church in frankfort with two supreme courts possibility of civil war in kentucky loomed resolution of controversy in elections of 1825 old court supporters won control of kentucky house of representatives when general assembly s session opened in november 1825 old court partisans immediately formed a committee to make recommendations related to court of appeals on november 23 old court supporters introduced a bill to repeal reorganization act measure passed house but failed in senate where old court and new court supporters were equal in number deciding vote was cast by lieutenant governor robert b mcafee aligned with new court advocates in december assembly s committee concluded that old court justices were constitutional judges and consequently legislature did not have power to abolish their positions best old court supporters were able to do in 1825 was to pass a non binding resolution condemning reorganization act by 1826 economic prosperity was beginning to return to state metzmeier p 7 old court party augmented their majority in house and gained a majority in senate one legislator s proposition to resolve controversy was to call for resignation of governor and lieutenant governor entire general assembly as well as justices from both old and new courts essentially allowing citizens to reset their entire government this extreme measure was rejected instead on december 29 1826 general assembly repealed reorganization act and overrode governor desha s veto of measure vanburkleo pp 693 694 they also repealed replevin law that had touched off controversy on january 1 1827 francis blair returned court records in his possession to old court old court chief justice john boyle resigned to accept a federal judgeship general assembly decided to speed reconciliation of two sides of controversy by naming new court partisan george bibb as boyle s replacement vanburkleo p 694 old court justices mills and owsley resigned hoping to clarify situation further they were immediately reappointed but senate refused to confirm their appointments
