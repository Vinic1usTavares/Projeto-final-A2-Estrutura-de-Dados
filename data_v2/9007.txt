new york state route 18f ny 18f is a long state highway in northwestern niagara county new york in united states southern terminus of route is at an interchange with ny 104 and niagara scenic parkway just east of village of lewiston northern terminus is at an intersection with ny 18 near four mile creek state park in porter ny 18f parallels ny 18 for most of its alignment taking a more westerly course than its parent ny 18f is only remaining suffixed route of ny 18 portion of modern ny 18f south of youngstown was originally designated as part of ny 34 in 1924 however ny 34 was absorbed into ny 18 as part of 1930 renumbering of state highways in new york change was part of a larger extension of ny 18 east to rochester ny 18 was realigned in 1949 to follow its modern alignment between lewiston and lake ontario at which time its former routing alongside niagara river became ny 18f route description thumb left erroneous ny 18f signage on ny 104 officially ny 18f begins at eastern 34 in 1924 in 1930 renumbering of state highways in new york ny 34 became part of ny 18 which was extended north to youngstown and east to rochester as part of renumbering ny 18 was originally state maintained from village of lewiston to youngstown however state of new york relinquished ownership and maintenance of ny 18 between center street in lewiston and village of youngstown on july 24 1947 section within village of lewiston was transferred to village control while remainder of route was given to niagara county in december 1947 lewiston petitioned county to also assume maintenance of portion of ny 18 within village request was eventually granted on january 1 1949 ny 18 was realigned to follow a more inland routing between village of lewiston and what is now four mile creek state park former riverside and lakeside routing of ny 18 between two locations was redesignated as ny 18f at time ny 18f was one of five spur routes of ny 18 which at time extended southward to
