battle of constantinople was fought in june 922 at outskirts of capital of byzantine empire constantinople between forces of first bulgarian empire and byzantines during byzantine bulgarian war of 913 927 in summer byzantine emperor romanos i lekapenos sent troops under commander saktikios to repel another bulgarian raid at outskirts of byzantine capital byzantines stormed bulgarian camp but were defeated when they confronted main bulgarian forces during his flight from battlefield saktikios was mortally wounded and died following night bulgarians who by 922 were in control of most of balkans continued to ravage byzantine countryside virtually unopposed however they lacked maritime power to conduct a successful siege of constantinople subsequent attempts to negotiate a bulgarian arab alliance for a joint assault of constantinople were discovered by byzantines and successfully countered strategic situation in balkans remained unchanged until both sides signed a peace treaty in 927 which recognized imperial title of bulgarian monarchs and complete independence of bulgarian orthodox church as an autocephalous patriarchate primary sources for battle are continuation of george hamartolos chronicle and john skylitzes synopsis of histories background during his short reign byzantine emperor alexander r 912 913 provoked a conflict with bulgarian monarch simeon i r 893 927 simeon i who had long harboured ambitions to claim an imperial title for himself took opportunity to wage war with byzantine empire in disarray following alexander s death in june 913 bulgarians reached constantinople unopposed and forced regency of infant constantine vii r 913 959 to recognize simeon i as emperor in bulgarian tsar following a palace coup in 914 new byzantine regency revoked concessions to bulgarians and summoned whole army including troops in asia minor to deal with bulgarian threat once and all in decisive battle of achelous in 917 byzantine forces were completely annihilated leaving bulgarians in charge of balkans their annual campaigns reached walls of constantinople and isthmus of corinth all subsequent attempts to confront bulgarian army at katasyrtai aquae and pegae ended in defeat despite his military supremacy over land simeon i was aware that he needed naval assistance in order to seize constantinople in 922 he clandestinely sent envoys to fatimid caliph ubayd allah al mahdi billah in mahdia to negotiate assistance of powerful arab navy simeon i proposed to divide equally all spoils bulgarians were to keep constantinople and fatimids would gain
