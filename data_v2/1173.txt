on 29 september 1940 a mid air collision occurred over brocklesby new south wales australia accident was unusual in that aircraft involved two avro ansons of no 2 service flying training school raaf remained locked together after colliding and then landed safely collision stopped engines of upper anson but those of machine underneath continued to run allowing aircraft to keep flying both navigators and pilot of lower anson bailed out pilot of upper anson found that he was able to control interlocked aircraft with his ailerons and flaps and made an emergency landing in a nearby paddock all four crewmen survived incident and upper anson was repaired and returned to flight service training school and flight details thumb left 260px ansons of no 2 sfts in formation alt five twin menzies sinclair 27 from glen innes as navigator tail number l9162 was piloted by leading aircraftman jack inglis hewson 19 from newcastle with leading aircraftman hugh gavin fraser 27 from melbourne as navigator their planned route was expected to take them first to corowa then to narrandera then back to forest hill collision and emergency landing ansons were at an altitude of over township of brocklesby near albury when they made a banking turn fuller lost sight of hewson s aircraft beneath him and two ansons collided amid what fuller later described as a grinding crash and a bang as roaring struck each other and bit into engine cowlings aircraft remained jammed together lower anson s turret wedged into other s port wing root and its fin and rudder balancing upper anson s port tailplane both of upper aircraft s engines had been knocked out in collision but those of one below continued to turn at full power as interlocked ansons began to slowly circle fuller described freak combination as lumping along like a brick he nevertheless found that he was able to control pair of aircraft with his ailerons and flaps and began searching for a place to land two navigators sinclair and fraser bailed out followed soon after by lower anson s pilot hewson whose back had been injured when spinning blades of other aircraft sliced through his fuselage fuller travelled five miles eight kilometres after collision then successfully made an emergency pancake landing in a large paddock south west of brocklesby locked aircraft slid across grass before coming to rest as far as fuller was concerned touchdown was better than any he had made when practising circuits and bumps at forest hill airfield previous day his acting commanding officer squadron leader cooper declared choice of improvised runway perfect and landing itself as a wonderful effort raaf s inspector of air accidents group captain arthur spud murphy flew straight to scene from air force headquarters in melbourne accompanied by his deputy henry fuller told murphy aftermath freak accident garnered news coverage around world and cast a spotlight on small town of brocklesby in preventing destruction of ansons fuller was credited not only with avoiding possible damage to brocklesby but with saving approximately ps40 000 worth of military hardware both ansons were repaired top aircraft n4876 returned to flight service and lower l9162 was used as an instructional airframe hewson was treated for his back injury at albury district hospital and returned to active duty he graduated from no 2 sfts in october 1940 he was discharged from air force as a flight lieutenant in 1946 sinclair was discharged in 1945 also a flight lieutenant fraser was posted to britain and flew as a pilot officer with no 206 squadron raf based in aldergrove northern ireland he and his crew of three died on 1 january 1942 during a routine training flight when their lockheed hudson collided with a tree fuller was promoted to sergeant after his successful landing but also confined to barracks for fourteen days and docked seven days pay for speaking about incident to newspapers without authorisation he graduated from no 2 sfts in october 1940 and received a commendation from australian air board for his presence of mind courage and determination in landing locked ansons without serious damage to aircraft under difficult conditions fuller saw active service first in middle east and then in europe with no 37 squadron raf he earned distinguished flying medal for his actions over palermo in march 1942 commissioned later that year fuller was posted back to australia as a flying officer and became an instructor at no 1 operational training unit in sale victoria he died near sale on 18 march 1944 when
