george gosse gc 16 february 1912 31 december 1964 was an australian recipient of george cross highest award for heroism or courage not in face of enemy that could be awarded to a member of australian armed forces at time gosse served in royal australian navy between 1926 and 1933 reaching rank of sub lieutenant and receiving training and experience with royal navy in 1940 he joined royal australian naval volunteer reserve ranvr for service in world war ii quickly sent back to united kingdom he served on several shore establishments before being sent to british india as a naval mine clearance specialist he returned to uk in late 1944 and in april 1945 he was given command of a naval party responsible for mine clearance in recently captured bremen harbour in germany he displayed courage in defusing three mines under very difficult conditions between 8 may and 19 may 1945 which resulted in him being awarded george cross gosse continued to serve in ranvr after war reaching rank of lieutenant commander before retiring in 1958 and died of a heart condition in 1964 his medal set is displayed in hall of valour at australian war memorial early life and career george gosse was born on 16 february 1912 at harvey western australia eldest child of william hay gosse a farmer and his wife muriel davidson he was a grandson of explorer william gosse and a nephew of businessman sir james hay gosse his father had served in 2nd south australian mounted rifles in second boer war and joined british army as an artillery officer in world war i he was awarded military cross for gallantry and was killed in action in 1918 muriel died in 1920 george and his younger sister were then cared for by their paternal grandmother gosse was schooled at st peter s college adelaide south australia and entered royal australian naval college at jervis bay in 1926 according to a family member he was so like his father gay feckless fearless and gregarious while at ran college he excelled at field hockey and upon graduation received prize for engineering theory beginning in january 1930 he served aboard both australian county class heavy cruisers first then he was promoted to midshipman in may of that year in july 1931 he sailed for united kingdom for further training with royal navy his first assignment was to mediterranean fleet aboard super dreadnought battleship he also attended an air course on aircraft carrier and was familiarised with employment of destroyers during a stint aboard in september 1932 he was promoted to acting sub lieutenant and entered royal naval college greenwich social and sporting temptations of london beckoned and gosse s studies suffered after he failed examination for lieutenant he was returned to australia and his naval career ended on 30 october 1933 gosse then worked at odd jobs for a few years and in 1938 he married diana skottowe at his old school chapel couple had two daughters world war ii on 21 october 1940 gosse enlisted as an ordinary seaman in royal australian naval volunteer reserve he initially underwent training at shore establishments hmas torrens and hmas cerberus before sailing for uk in december in april 1941 he was commissioned as a sub lieutenant while posted to shore establishment hms king alfred in december of that year he was transferred to royal indian navy shore establishment hmis hooghly in calcutta british india as a naval mine disposal officer in august of that year he was transferred to hms lanka this was followed by a posting to shore establishment hms braganza in bombay in october 1942 his annual reports indicate that he was reliable and keen and displayed ingenuity when faced with difficulties he was always cheerful and was a daring character who was very interested in mines in february 1942 he had been promoted to provisional lieutenant thumb left george gosse defuses a german mine at bremen transferred back to uk in november 1944 gosse was posted to shore establishment hms vernon at brixham devon which was european port clearance diving base for royal navy he brought a japanese mine back with him to uk as he considered it would be of use at hms vernon he was a bit of a law unto himself in this period but was fascinated with mechanical devices and exhibited inventiveness he qualified as a shallow water diver in january 1945 following capture of bremen germany in april gosse led naval party 1571 to port to clear mines laid by retreating germans in after arriving in bremen gosse risked his life many times in defusing mines when his divers reported a sighting of what appeared to be a new form of mine on 8 may gosse dived himself and verified that it was a d type mine with additional fittings known as an oyster this mine was pressure operated and its detonation train included magnetic and acoustic elements about 18 00 next day gosse examined mine by touch as visibility was so poor that his waterproof torch was of no use in order to maintain his depth he had to tether himself to mine marker buoy rope using tools he had improvised gosse interrupted detonation train by removing primer release and primer which had to be extracted about down a wide tube having made mine safe gosse was releasing his tether when there was a small explosion later examination of mine showed that water had entered primer tube and actuated a water pressure trigger set to go off if mine was raised gosse personally defused two more oyster mines at bremen between 9 and 19 may he was promoted to acting lieutenant commander on 30 september 1945 and was demobilised on 20 march 1946 for his service in world war ii gosse was awarded 1939 1945 star burma star france and germany star defence medal war medal 1939 1945 and australia service medal 1939 willoughby norrie he continued to serve in ranvr and was substantively promoted to lieutenant commander on 30 june 1955 before retiring in 1958 he remained inventive creating many useful domestic gadgets and fittings but his interest waned once a challenge had been met his work was mostly unspectacular he was president of sporting car club of south australia from 1946 to 1948 during 1950 gosse was part of an armed forces recruiting campaign throughout south australia before collapsing from nervous strain at a rally in in 1953 he was part of contingent sent to uk for coronation of queen elizabeth ii and was awarded queen elizabeth ii coronation medal gosse died of a coronary occlusion at maslin beach on 31 december 1964 and was cremated he is commemorated on returned and services
