3rd parachute brigade was an airborne forces brigade raised by british army during second world war brigade was initially part of 1st airborne division but remained in britain when that division was sent overseas and became part of 6th airborne division alongside 5th parachute brigade and 6th airlanding brigade brigade first went into action on 5 june 1944 during operation tonga part of normandy landings objective was to destroy merville gun battery and bridges over river dives brigade achieved all its objectives and remained defending left flank of invasion zone until mid august they then crossed river dives and advanced as far as river seine before they were withdrawn while recovering in england brigade was moved to belgium in december 1944 to counter german attack in ardennes brigade remained on border between belgium and netherlands carrying out patrols until march 1945 their next airborne mission was operation varsity assault crossing of river rhine in germany after this brigade advanced towards baltic sea arriving just ahead of red army still part of 6th airborne division brigade was sent to british mandate of palestine in october 1945 after end of war carrying out an internal security role with rest of 6th airborne division it remained in palestine until it was disbanded in 1947 background impressed by success of german airborne operations during battle of france
