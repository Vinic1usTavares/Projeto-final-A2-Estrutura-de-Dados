donald wilson 25 september 1892 21 june 1978 was a united states army air forces general during world war ii wilson enlisted in maryland national guard as a private in 1916 and served with it on mexican border and western front during world war i before transferring to united states army air service after war he obtained a regular commission already qualified as an aerial observer he became a pilot in 1922 for many years he was an influential instructor at air corps tactical school wilson became a leading theorist who embraced doctrine that strategic bombing was most important aspect of air power he argued that by attacking vulnerabilities whole industries could be brought to a halt without necessarily having to destroy all of factories doctrine which wilson expounded later became basis for awpd 1 army air forces strategic war plan developed in 1941 during world war ii wilson served as chief of personnel division g l of war department general staff he became chief of staff of fifth air force in september 1942 before returning to united states in 1944 to become assistant chief of staff united states army air forces for organization commitments and requirements for a time he was acting chief of staff of army air forces in february 1945 wilson was present at battle of iwo jima as an official army air forces observer in june 1945 he assumed command of air force proving ground command after war wilson served as a member of gerow board which examined military educational system and instituted a series of long lasting reforms in 1947 he was diagnosed with neurasthenia
