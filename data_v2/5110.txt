benjamin franklin lewis december 2 1909 february 28 1963 was an american politician who served as alderman of chicago s 24th ward from 1958 until he was murdered in his ward office in 1963 he remains last elected official to be assassinated in chicago s history case remains unsolved a native of georgia but a longtime resident of chicago he was first african american political leader of a ward that had previously been dominated by jews known for his brash fashion and quick witted personality he lived a luxurious life and at time of his murder was presumptive future leader of chicago s african american community decades after his death he was fondly remembered in his ward for his service to community and local alderman had city council designate local stretch of pulaski road benjamin f lewis road in his honor in 1993 on other hand he was notoriously corrupt and thus there were many possible suspects for his murder early life benjamin franklin lewis was born in macon georgia on december 2 1909 and moved to chicago s west side with his family as a child attending crane high school and crane junior college he worked for united states unemployment service as a clerk from 1938 to 1941 and served as a second lieutenant in world war ii moving to 24th ward after war he served as a bus driver for chicago transit authority prior to quitting that job in 1955 he became a precinct captain in 1952 first african american one in 24th ward aldermanic tenure chicago s 24th ward encompassed north lawndale community area known locally as lawndale it was traditionally a russian jewish stronghold prior to white flight in 1950s after which it became predominantly african american despite jewish flight in 1950s whites continued to control ward and fought to retain that control this was a common setup on chicago s west side where such wards were known as plantation wards in contrast to south side which had had african american political representation for decades working as a city housing inspector at time lewis was elected as 24th ward alderman to replace sidney d deutsch who had been appointed city treasurer and consequently vacated office on september 24 1957 he defeated attorney arthur n hamilton who subsequently petitioned county court to void election and either declare hamilton alderman or to hold a new election alleging such irregularities as election judge misconduct and voter bribery judge carl h smith dismissed suit on june 6 holding complaint to be insufficient lewis took office on april 8 1958 during his tenure he again courted controversy when he referred clients to his own insurance business despite receiving 50 a month and 5 on commissions of business for referring clients to another insurance agent arthur x elrod inc he reached a settlement with elrod inc in july 1962 in which he promised to not refer clients of elrod inc to his own business while contract was still valid arthur x elrod had been ward s committeeman prior to his death in 1960 lewis was first african american alderman of 24th ward he often made disparaging remarks towards south side african american leaders such as william l dawson when asked how he stood on an unsuccessful attempt by dawson and others to replace 5th ward alderman leon despres with an african american he expressed indifference replying that i stand right at kedzie and roosevelt in my ward he was flashy and liked to be called big cat and was also known as duke of dixieland despite having an annual salary of 8 000 he had several mistresses took vacations in acapulco held much real estate and was known for his flashy wardrobe consisting almost entirely of suits costing at least 200 he was also noted for his quick wit although during council business he was soft spoken and rarely made a speech during his tenure actual power in ward was held by irwin horowitz a real estate manager living in gold coast president franklin d roosevelt had called 24th ward best democratic ward in country and it continued that reputation in lewis s time lewis was an ardent adherent to democratic machine and was selected by richard j daley to become 24th ward committeeman upon deutsch s death in october 1961 he was first african american committeeman of 24th ward at start of his tenure 37 of ward s 58 precincts had white precinct captains lewis gradually replaced white captains with african americans and upon hearing of white captains complaints suggested that they go to their respective ward committeemen for patronage positions at time of his murder he had signaled his intention to replace ailing thomas j o brien as congressman representing illinois s 6th congressional district and was widely pegged to replace dawson as main african american political leader in chicago murder in early hours of february 28 1963 lewis was murdered in his ward office on roosevelt road where he had moved day before he had heavily won reelection in aldermanic election two days previously defeating his opponent jeff clifford alford 13 189 votes to 888 he was found handcuffed to his desk with cigarette burns on his skin and had been shot three times in back of head case remains unsolved but murder was widely attributed to organized crime at time an fbi informant blamed outfit killers lenny patrick and dave for killing but no charges were ever filed against them jimmy hoffa historian dan claimed that hitman was a friend of jack ruby it was popularly believed at time that murder was unsolvable as there were too many suspects given lewis s activities a sentiment that was shared by director of investigation an ex convict boasted about killing lewis in 1985 but died in a fire before police could question him after lewis s death office of 24th ward alderman remained vacant until future congressman george w collins assumed it on january 21 1964 legacy lewis is last politician murdered in chicago as of 2019 according to historian richard lindberg murder capped off an open lawlessness in city with regards to its politicians that had been going on since time of al capone lewis is also remembered for his contributions to community 24th ward alderman jesse miller speaking in 1993 noted that even today ben lewis is considered to have been a stalwart in community who was a pioneer in making 24th ward an independent ward in city and successfully petitioned city council to
