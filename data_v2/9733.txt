strictly with operation of cemetery it was established in 1848 in response to growing rural cemetery movement in new england and went into service in 1850 cemetery was designed by architect john c sidney and underwent its greatest development in late 19th century under superintendent john boetcher who incorporated rare foliage and a clear landscape design strategy oakwood was fourth rural cemetery opened in new york and its governing body was first rural cemetery association created in state it features four man made lakes two residential structures a chapel a crematorium 24 mausolea and about 60 000 graves and has about of roads it is known both for its dense foliage and rolling lawns and has historically been used as a public park by lansingburgh and troy residents oakwood was
