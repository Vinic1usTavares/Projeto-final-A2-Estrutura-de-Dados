trellick tower is a grade ii listed tower block on cheltenham estate in kensal town london opened in 1972 it had been commissioned by greater london council and designed in brutalist style by architect erno goldfinger tower was planned to replace outdated social accommodation and designed as a follow up to goldfinger s earlier balfron tower in east london it was last major project he worked on and featured various space saving designs along with a separate access tower containing a plant room high rise apartments and brutalist architecture were falling out of favour by time tower was completed and it became a magnet for crime vandalism drug abuse and prostitution its fortunes gradually improved in 1980s after establishment of a residents association security measures were put in place and a concierge was employed which led to lower crime levels by 1990s tower had become a desirable place to live and although it still contains predominantly social housing demand for private flats has remained high a local landmark it has been grade ii listed since 1998 and has retained its distinctive concrete facade as a result a fire broke out in 2017 but concrete structure meant damage was limited unlike nearby grenfell tower trellick tower has featured on film and television several times location trellick tower is on cheltenham estate in golborne road kensal town royal borough of kensington and chelsea rbkc it is bounded to north and east by grand union canal and to south by a40 westway and great western main line leading from london paddington station nearest goldfinger designed entire tower block freehand on butcher s paper he planned various communal areas and purposefully put slight variations in structure so that each apartment would look different he included a number of space saving designs such as using sliding doors to access bathrooms and light switches embedded in door surrounds all apartments have large windows facing balconies in order to construction of trellick tower began in 1968 in order to replace sub standard local victorian housing tower was one of several such buildings and was initially welcomed as a way of resolving crises of post second world war housing goldfinger said whole object of building high is to free ground for children and grown ups to enjoy mother earth and not to cover every inch with bricks and mortar goldfinger had been encouraged to construct trellick tower by london county council lcc following success of balfron tower which had been commissioned in 1963 and opened four years later he took his inspiration from balfron where he had moved into one of apartments in order to experience what life would be like for tenants and invited residents round for regular cocktail parties to tell him their likes and dislikes this feedback was incorporated into design of trellick tower many immigrants from west indies and caribbean settled in trellick tower as for them it was one of few affordable places to live in london tower opened on 28 june 1972 construction costs ran to ps2 4 million it was last major project goldfinger worked on his reputation suffered as concrete tower blocks became
