francis nash october 7 1777 was a brigadier general in continental army during american revolutionary war prior to war he was a lawyer public official and politician in hillsborough north carolina and was heavily involved in opposing regulator movement an uprising of settlers in north carolina piedmont between 1765 and 1771 nash was also involved in north carolina politics representing hillsborough on several occasions in colonial north carolina general assembly nash quickly became engaged in revolutionary activities and served as a delegate to first three patriot provincial congresses in 1775 he was named lieutenant colonel of 1st north carolina regiment under colonel james moore and served briefly in southern theater of revolutionary war before being ordered north nash was made a brigadier general in 1777 upon moore s death and given command of north carolina brigade of continental army under general george washington he led north carolina s soldiers in philadelphia campaign but was wounded at battle of germantown on october 4 1777 and died several days later nash was one of ten patriot generals to die from wounds received in combat between 1775 and 1781 he is honored by several city and county names including those of nashville tennessee nashville north carolina and nash county north carolina early life and family thumb left francis nash s home in hillsborough now ruth jackson francis nash elder provided jackson with property west of hillsborough and several slaves war of regulation and pre revolution politics nash showed an interest in military affairs while living in hillsborough and received informal military training from a retired english soldier living there he worked his way up through orange county militia ranks until he eventually became its commanding colonel during war of regulation in 1768 he ordered militia to put down several riots incited by regulators but militiamen were sympathetic towards rioters and refused nash entered into a pact with others including edmund fanning adlai osborne and future governor alexander martin to protect one another s property against regulator threats but parties to that agreement lived at great distances from each other rendering pact ineffective along with fanning who was a personal friend nash was accused of extorting money from hillsborough s residents regulator leaders attempted to have nash tried for corruption but charges against him were dismissed in september 1770 a group of regulators took control of hillsborough forcing nash and other public officials to flee for fear of bodily harm nash subsequently fought alongside governor william tryon in battle of alamance against regulator militia he served in lower house of colonial assembly in 1771 and from 1773 to 1775 as a representative for hillsborough in 1774 royal governor josiah martin postponed scheduled convening of colonial assembly to prevent north carolina assembly from selecting delegates to proposed continental congress which was to begin in philadelphia in september in response members of assembly many of whom would later become patriot supporters convened first north carolina provincial congress in august 1774 nash and his brother abner were both elected to that body along with 69 other north carolinians which then selected delegates to continental congress governor martin condemned provincial congress as an extra legal body not permitted to assemble and represent people of north carolina in an attempt to quash its work governor called colonial assembly to convene on april 5 1775 but second north carolina provincial congress met in a session several hours before assembly was set to open and many of congressional delegates including nash voted to support work of continental and provincial congresses in response martin dissolved assembly royal government would never again call an assembly to session in north carolina american revolutionary war southern theater in 1775 nash served in third north carolina provincial congress which organized eight regiments of soldiers on instructions from continental congress later that year provincial congress appointed nash lieutenant colonel of 1st north carolina regiment under command of then colonel james moore in november 1st north carolina was formally integrated into continental army organization nash served as an officer under moore during maneuvers that led up to battle of moore s creek bridge in february 1776 but like moore did not participate in battle arriving after its conclusion during april 1776 nash was promoted to colonel to replace moore who had been promoted to brigadier general nash took part in expedition to aid charleston in 1776 which culminated in battle of sullivan s island immediately prior to that engagement nash had been ordered by major general charles lee commander of southern department to relieve william moultrie s south carolina troops on sullivan s island but british assault prevented that relief moultrie would go on to successfully defend island from a much larger british force while nash s unit guarded unfinished rear of fort sullivan philadelphia campaign nash returned with his regiment to north carolina in anticipation of joining general george washington s army in north but fears of british and indian attacks in georgia prevented any such action and caused nash to remain in his home state on february 5 1777 he was promoted to brigadier general by continental congress he was also tasked with recruiting more soldiers from western part of state but was forced to abandon that task after james moore s death on april 15 1777 nash was then placed in command of north carolina brigade although fellow north carolinian robert howe s commission as a brigadier general predated nash s howe had been made commander of southern department of continental army and he was forced to remain in command of troops defending south carolina nash marched north to join washington s army and commanded all nine north carolina continental army regiments at battle of brandywine battle of germantown and death thumb an artist s depiction of fighting around chew house at germantown struck at main part of british army near philadelphia in battle of germantown on october 4 initially north carolina brigade was intended to serve in continental army s reserve but washington out of a desire to defend his flank ordered nash into action nash was commanding a fighting retreat slowly moving his unit backwards to stall british advance when he was mortally wounded by a cannonball that struck him in hip and killed his horse same cannonball killed major james witherspoon son of john witherspoon president of princeton university and a signer of declaration of independence nash may have also been blinded by a musket ball that struck him in head thomas paine who saw him taken off battlefield later stated that nash s wounds had made him unrecognizable nash was treated by washington s personal physician james craik who could not stem his bleeding which was reported to have fully soaked through two mattresses nash succumbed to his wounds on october 7 at a private residence near pennsylvania his final words are alleged to have been from first dawn of revolution i have been ever on side of liberty and my country he was buried in towamencin mennonite meetinghouse cemetery in towamencin township pennsylvania on october 9 1777 along with other officers who had perished at germantown most of continental army s senior officers including washington himself attended funeral service nash s friend and colleague alexander martin who later became governor of state of north carolina and who had witnessed nash s wounding later composed a funeral poem in fallen general
