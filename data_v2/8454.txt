no 450 squadron was a unit of royal australian air force raaf that operated during world war ii established at raaf station williamtown new south wales in february 1941 it was first australian article xv squadron formed under empire air training scheme squadron embarked for middle east in april 1941 its personnel initially consisted solely of ground crew who joined pilots of no 260 squadron raf to form no 260 450 squadron which briefly operated hawker hurricane fighters in syria it was not until february 1942 that no 450 squadron now with its own pilots and equipped with curtiss p 40 kittyhawk fighters commenced operations in earnest over next 15 months it fought in north african and tunisian campaigns in both fighter and fighter bomber roles claiming 49 german and italian aircraft destroyed in air and earning nickname desert harassers beginning in july 1943 no 450 squadron took part in allied invasion of sicily and italian campaign primarily in close support role its aircraft attacked targets in yugoslavia as well as in sicily and italy squadron began converting from kittyhawks to north american p 51 mustang fighters in may 1945 but never saw action with its new aircraft it was disbanded in august 1945 following conclusion of hostilities having suffered 63 fatal casualties during war today by agreement with raaf squadron s number is carried by a royal canadian air force unit 450 tactical helicopter squadron history no 450 squadron was formed at raaf station williamtown near newcastle new south wales on 7 february 1941 raised a week before no 451 squadron it was first australian squadron established for service with british military under article xv of empire air training scheme eats no 450 squadron was intended to be an infiltration squadron which would consist initially only of ground crew and would receive a nucleus of experienced pilots after arriving in its designated theatre of operations inaugurated at ottawa canada in october 1939 eats was a plan to expand royal air force s raf capacity to train aircrews by creating a pool of personnel from various commonwealth countries australia canada britain and new zealand through establishment of a common training system consisting of a series of initial elementary and advanced training schools at conclusion of advanced training personnel were posted as required to raf controlled squadrons in britain or middle east these squadrons were designated as either raf raaf royal canadian air force or royal new zealand air force squadrons but were paid for and administered by british government and personnel could be drawn from any commonwealth nation seventeen raaf squadrons were formed during war under article xv of agreement middle east and north africa under temporary command of flight lieutenant bruce shepherd no 450 squadron left australia on 11 april 1941 embarking on troopship queen elizabeth at sydney and arriving in egypt on 3 may at raf abu sueir squadron leader gordon steege
