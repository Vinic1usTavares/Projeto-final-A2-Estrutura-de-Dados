sir albert john gould 12 february 1847 27 july 1936 was an australian politician and solicitor who served as second president of australian senate a solicitor businessman and citizen soldier before his entry into politics gould was a member of new south wales legislative assembly from 1882 to 1898 during which time he served as minister for justice in two free trade governments he later served two years in new south wales legislative council from 1899 to 1901 until his election to australian senate gould s interest in parliamentary procedure saw him become involved with relevant standing committee and he was elected unopposed as second president of senate in 1907 his tenure is remembered as more traditionalist and than his predecessor s defeated by labor nominee in 1910 following liberal government s defeat gould remained in parliament as a backbencher until 1917 when he retired after he was not re endorsed by nationalist party he was active in community and religious affairs during his long retirement early life and career gould was born in sydney son of solicitor john morton gould and his wife anne nee livingstone he attended william woolls school in parramatta and went on to study law at university of sydney although he did not take a degree he served his articles with his father and was admitted to bar in december 1870 he then worked in singleton for a sydney legal firm gould also developed significant business interests being involved with great cobar copper mining syndicate and serving as a director of electric light and power supply company city bank of sydney and oriental timber corporation on 12 september 1872 he married jeanette jessie maitland at st paul s church of england in west maitland gould was also a citizen soldier enlisting as a volunteer in west maitland company of new south wales volunteer forces he later took command of singleton company becoming a major in 1886 he later received volunteer officers decoration for long service he would retire from regiment in 1902 as a lieutenant colonel state politics in 1882 gould was elected to new south wales legislative assembly as member for patrick s plains renamed singleton in 1894 initially an opponent of coalition associated with henry parkes and john robertson he later supported robertson s government in 1885 by 1887 he was supporting his career with his own legal practice in singleton and sydney and when party system came into being in new south wales in that year he identified as a free trader in 1889 he was appointed minister for justice under parkes serving until 1891 he held position again under george reid from 1894 to 1898 during first period of his ministry he became involved in a dispute with chief justice sir frederick darley over requests for improved court accommodation and a contested punishment for contempt of two witnesses despite embarrassing his premier he remained close with parkes throughout his state career and in later years frequently gave speeches in his memory gould s career as justice minister was marked by consolidation of law tightening of licensing laws and reforms relating to police courts a supporter of federation he nevertheless opposed 1898 bill which he believed deprived new south wales of adequate recognition he was defeated in election of that year but in 1899 he was one of premier reid s twelve appointments to legislative council that enabled passage of legislation for a referendum on federation senate career following federation gould contested first federal election in march 1901 standing for senate as a free trader he was elected in third of six positions entitling him to a six year term his first speech was largely devoted to his impassioned support for free trade and in his early years as a senator he also supported decentralisation and opposed proposals to establish a federal capital a supporter of white australia policy he expressed concern over kanaka labour in queensland although his assumption that arrangement was temporary enabled his pragmatism on this issue gould was also involved in defence matters supporting introduction of conscription and maintaining loyalty to empire where there are men much more experienced in principles of government than we are at 1906 election gould was easily re elected in first position winning largest vote for any senator to that time from 1901 gould maintained an interest in procedure of senate and was a member of standing orders committee from its appointment in 1901 until 1907 he had supported opening of senate proceedings with prayer and had made frequent reference to standing orders of british house of commons he contested presidency of senate in 1904 but was defeated by incumbent sir richard baker on baker s retirement gould was unanimously elected president on 20 february 1907 he endeavoured to keep senate representative of states interests and free from party politics and his rulings focused on unparliamentary language and relevance gould appointed knight bachelor in 1908 differed from his predecessor in his adherence to british influence with election of fisher labor government in 1910 gould was defeated by harry turley for presidency despite his support for conscription gould was not endorsed by new nationalist party to contest 1917 election deeply offended gould nevertheless elected to retire rather than run as an independent and split nationalist vote later life in his retirement gould continued his community involvement a director of royal prince alfred hospital and royal alexandra hospital for children he also served as a member and chancellor of sydney and newcastle synods of church of england he died in july 1936 at rose bay aged 89 he was survived by two sons and three daughters his wife died in 1928 one daughter had also predeceased him gould was given a state funeral at st andrew s cathedral and was buried at south head cemetery references category free
