maximilian kolbe 8 january 1894 14 august 1941 was a polish conventual franciscan friar who volunteered to die in place of a stranger in german death camp of auschwitz located in german occupied poland during world war ii he had been active in promoting veneration of immaculate virgin mary founding and supervising monastery of niepokalanow near warsaw operating an amateur radio station and founding or running several other organizations and publications on 10 october 1982 pope john paul ii canonized father kolbe and declared him a martyr of charity catholic church venerates him as patron saint of amateur radio operators of drug addicts of political prisoners of families of journalists of prisoners and of pro life movement john paul ii declared him patron saint of our difficult century his feast day is 14 august day of his death due to kolbe s efforts to promote consecration and entrustment to mary he is known as apostle of consecration to mary biography childhood kolbe was born on 8 january 1894 in zdunska wola in kingdom of poland which was then part of russian empire he was second son of weaver julius kolbe and midwife maria his father was an ethnic german and his mother was polish he had four brothers shortly after his birth his family moved to kolbe s life was strongly influenced in 1906 when he was 12 by a vision of virgin mary he later described this incident that night i asked mother of god what was to become of me then she came to me holding two crowns one white other red she asked me if i was willing to accept either of these crowns white one meant that i should persevere in purity and red that i should become a martyr i said that i would accept them both franciscan friar in 1907 kolbe and his elder brother francis joined conventual franciscans they enrolled at conventual franciscan minor seminary in lwow later that year in 1910 kolbe was allowed to enter novitiate where he was given religious name maximilian he professed his first vows in 1911 and final vows in 1914 adopting additional name of maria mary kolbe was sent to rome in 1912 where he attended pontifical gregorian university he earned a doctorate in philosophy in 1915 there from 1915 he continued his studies at pontifical university of st bonaventure where he earned a doctorate in theology in 1919 or 1922 sources vary he was active in consecration and entrustment to mary during his time as a student he witnessed vehement demonstrations against popes st pius x and benedict xv in rome during an anniversary celebration by freemasons according to kolbe they placed black standard of giordano under windows of vatican on this standard archangel st michael was depicted lying under feet of triumphant lucifer at same time countless pamphlets were distributed to people in which holy father i e pope was attacked shamefully soon afterward on 16 october 1917 kolbe organized militia immaculatae army of immaculate one to work for conversion of sinners and enemies of catholic church specifically freemasons through intercession of virgin mary so serious was kolbe about this goal that he added to miraculous medal prayer o mary conceived without sin pray for us who have recourse to thee and for all those who do not have recourse to thee especially masons and all those recommended to thee thumb left maximilian kolbe on a west german postage stamp marked auschwitz in 1918 kolbe was ordained a priest in july 1919 he returned to poland which was newly independent he was active in promoting veneration of immaculate virgin mary he was strongly opposed to leftist in particular communist movements from 1919 to 1922 he taught at krakow seminary around that time as well as earlier in rome he suffered from tuberculosis which forced him to take a lengthy leave of absence from his teaching duties tb was generally considered fatal with rest and good nutrition best treatment as antibiotics had not been developed to treat it in january 1922 kolbe founded monthly periodical knight of immaculata a devotional publication based on french le messager du coeur de jesus messenger of heart of jesus from 1922 to 1926 he operated a religious publishing press in grodno as his activities grew in scope in 1927 he founded a new conventual franciscan monastery at niepokalanow near warsaw it became a major religious publishing centre a junior seminary was opened there two years later missionary work in asia between 1930 and 1936 kolbe undertook a series of missions to east asia he arrived first in shanghai china but failed to gather a following there next he moved to japan where by 1931 he had founded a franciscan monastery no sono on outskirts of nagasaki kolbe had started publishing a japanese edition of knight of immaculata no blast in mid 1932 kolbe left japan for malabar india where he founded another monastery this one closed after a while return to poland meanwhile in his absence monastery at niepokalanow began to
