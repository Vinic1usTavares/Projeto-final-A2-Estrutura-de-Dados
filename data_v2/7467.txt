big thunder ski jumping centre was a twin ski jumping hill located in thunder bay in northwestern ontario canada it constitutes part of big thunder national training centre first hills were built by knute and thor hansen and opened in 1963 they were originally known as lille norway ski area then mt norway ski area and sundance northwest resort before taking current name large and normal hills were built in 1974 and venue was taken over by provincial government in 1985 hills hosted 29 fis ski jumping world cup and 50 canadian ski jumping championships tournaments between 1975 and 1995 climaxing with fis nordic world ski championships 1995 funding was then cut and venue has since been closed and unmaintained history location was first identified by knute hansen a ski jumper who felt
