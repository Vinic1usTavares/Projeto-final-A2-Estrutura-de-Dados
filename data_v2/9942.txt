1st provisional marine brigade was a marine infantry brigade of united states marine corps usmc that existed periodically from 1912 to 1950 it was an ad hoc unit formed for specific operations and not considered a permanent usmc unit brigade saw five brief activations for service over a 40 year span first created for duty in cuba following negro rebellion brigade was not activated again until 1941 when it was hastily constructed from 6th marine regiment to garrison iceland after occupation of that country by british troops during world war ii brigade saw service once more in war during battle of guam in pacific war conducting an amphibious landing on that island s southern sector and subduing resistance from japanese troops it was activated once more in a brief organizational shift after war brigade was formed again in 1950 when it was hastily assembled for service in korean war brigade participated in a counterattack at masan before reinforcing united states army units during battle of pusan perimeter and at first and second battles of naktong bulge along naktong river brigade was deactivated for last time when it was merged with 1st marine division organization 1st provisional
