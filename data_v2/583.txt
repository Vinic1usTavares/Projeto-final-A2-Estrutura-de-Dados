foster payne house is a historic house at 25 belmont street in pawtucket rhode island built in 1878 two story multi gabled house is distinguished by its clapboarded and exterior woodwork and opulent parlors in interior property also has a matching carriagehouse with gable roof and cupola house was originally constructed and owned by theodore waters foster but it was sold to george w payne in 1882 foster payne house is architecturally significant as a well designed and well preserved late 19th century suburban residence it was added to national register of historic places in 1983 history house was built in 1878 for theodore waters foster born on may 19 1847 to chauncey foster and ophelia remington he was educated in pawtucket and joined rhode island cavalry in 1863 he served in american civil war under nathaniel p banks in red river campaign in 1873 he formed a jewelry manufacturing partnership with walter e white and samuel h baily named white foster company after white retired in 1878 company was renamed foster bailey during this time foster constructed house and lived in it for a period of four years commuting to his place of business in providence rhode island foster would continue to be successful in his jewelry manufacturing business and his son theodore clyde foster would succeed him upon his death in 1928 in 1882 theodore foster sold home to george w payne owner of
