mac curtis speedie january 12 1920 march 5 1993 was an american football end who played for cleveland browns in all america football conference aafc and national football league nfl for seven years and later served for two years as head coach of american football league s denver broncos a tall and quick runner whose awkward gait helped him deceive defenders and get open speedie led his league in receptions four times during his career and was selected as a first team all pro six times his career average of 800 yards per season was not surpassed until two decades after his retirement and his per game average of 50 yards went unequalled for 20 years after he left game speedie grew up in utah where he overcame perthes disease to become a standout as a hurdler on his high school track team and a halfback on football team he attended university of utah where he continued to excel at track and football before entering military in 1942 during world war ii he spent four years in service before joining browns in 1946 where he played as an end opposite quarterback otto graham fullback marion motley and fellow receiver dante lavelli browns a new team in aafc won league championship every year between 1946 and 1949 browns merged into nfl in 1950 after aafc disbanded and speedie continued to succeed as team won another league championship after two more years with browns however speedie left team for western interprovincial football union wifu amid a conflict with paul brown cleveland s head coach he played two full seasons in wifu and one game in a third season before leaving professional football speedie was hired in 1960 as an end coach for houston oilers in american football league afl oilers won afl championship that year but speedie left in 1961 after head coach former teammate lou rymkus was fired he then took a job as an assistant for afl s denver broncos and was promoted to head coach in 1964 his two year run with team was unsuccessful however after his resignation in 1966 speedie became a scout for broncos a job he kept until his retirement in 1982 despite lobbying by friends and former teammates speedie was not selected for induction into pro
