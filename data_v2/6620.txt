pecanac chetniks also known as black chetniks were a collaborationist chetnik irregular military force which operated in german occupied territory of serbia under leadership of vojvoda kosta pecanac they were loyal to german backed serbian puppet government thumb pecanac second from left with a german military officer and kosovo albanian collaborator deva third from left in territory of military commander in serbia 20 october 1941 pecanac was eventually denounced as a traitor by yugoslav government in exile and germans concluded that his detachments were inefficient unreliable and of little military aid to them germans and puppet government disbanded organisation between september 1942 and march 1943 and pecanac was interned for some time afterwards before being killed in mid 1944 by forces loyal to his chetnik rival draza mihailovic background pecanac chetniks were named after their commander kosta pecanac who was a fighter and later vojvoda in serbian chetnik organization who had first distinguished himself in fighting against ottoman empire in macedonia between 1903 and 1910 in first balkan war fought from october 1912 to may 1913 pecanac served as a sergeant in royal serbian army during second balkan war fought from 29 june to 10 august 1913 he saw combat against kingdom of bulgaria during world war i he led bands of serbian guerillas fighting behind bulgarian and austro hungarian lines he was most prominent figure in chetnik movement during interwar period he had a leading role in association against bulgarian bandits a notorious organisation that arbitrarily terrorised bulgarians in stip region part of modern day macedonia he also served as a commander with organization of yugoslav nationalists as a member of parliament he was present when croatian peasant party hss leader stjepan radic and hss deputies pavle radic and duro were killed by serb politician punisa racic on 20 june 1928 prior to shooting pecanac was accused by hss deputy ivan of being responsible for a massacre of 200 muslims in 1921 pecanac became president of chetnik association in 1932 by opening membership of organisation to younger members that had not served in world war i he grew organisation during 1930s from a nationalist veterans association focused on protecting veterans rights to an aggressively partisan serb political organisation with 500 000 members throughout kingdom of yugoslavia during this period pecanac formed close ties with far right yugoslav radical union government of milan stojadinovic and was known for his hostility to yugoslav communist party which made him popular with conservatives such as those in yugoslav radical union formation shortly before axis invasion of yugoslavia in april 1941 yugoslav ministry of army and navy requested that pecanac prepare for guerrilla operations and guard southern area of serbia macedonia and kosovo from pro bulgarians and pro albanians in region he was given arms and money and managed to arm several hundred men in river valley in southern serbia pecanac s force remained intact after german occupation of serbia and supplemented its strength from serb refugees fleeing macedonia and kosovo in early summer of 1941 pecanac s detachments fought against albanian bands at this time and for a considerable period after only detachments under pecanac were identified by term chetnik with formation of communist led yugoslav partisans pecanac gave up any interest in resistance and by late august came to agreements with both serbian puppet government and german authorities to carry out attacks against partisans pecanac kept organisational structure of his detachments simple all of commanders were selected personally by pecanac and consisted of former officers peasants orthodox priests teachers and merchants pecanac chetniks were also known as black chetniks collaboration with occupation and quisling forces on 18 august 1941 while he was concluding arrangements with germans pecanac received a letter from rival chetnik leader draza mihailovic proposing an arrangement where pecanac would control chetniks south of western morava river while mihailovic would control chetniks in all other areas pecanac declined this request and suggested that he might offer mihailovic position as his chief of staff he also recommended that mihailovic s detachments disband and join his organisation in meantime pecanac had arranged for transfer of several thousand of his chetniks to serbian gendarmerie to act as german auxiliaries thumb alt a copy of proclamation by pecanac pecanac s on detachments that have been formed without his approval to come together under his command he demanded that individuals hiding in forests return to their homes immediately and that acts of sabotage directed at occupation authorities cease or suffer punishment of death communist leader josip broz tito and all members of communist party of yugoslavia left belgrade on 16 september 1941 using documents issued to tito by dragoljub milutinovic who was voivode of pecanac chetniks since pecanac was already fully cooperating with germans by this time this assistance caused some to speculate that tito left belgrade with blessing of germans because his task was to divide rebel forces similar to lenin s arrival in russia in september 1941 some of pecanac s subordinates broke ranks to join partisans in fighting germans and their serbian auxiliaries in mountainous kopaonik region a previously loyal subordinate of pecanac began attacking local gendarmerie stations and clashing with armed bands of albanian muslims by end of october germans decided to stop arming unreliable elements within pecanac s chetniks and attached remainder to their other serbian auxiliary forces on 7 october 1941 pecanac sent a request to head of serbian puppet government milan nedic for trained officers supplies arms salary funds and more over time his requests were fulfilled and a german liaison officer was appointed at pecanac s headquarters to help coordinate actions on 17 january 1942 according to german data 72 chetnik officers and 7 963 men were being paid and supplied by serbian gendarmerie this fell short of their maximum authorised strength of 8 745 men and included two or three thousand of mihailovic s chetniks who had been legalised in november 1941 in same month pecanac sought permission from italians for his forces to move into eastern montenegro but was refused due to italian concerns that chetniks would move into sandzak in april 1942 german commanding general in serbia general der artillerie paul bader issued orders giving unit numbers to to pecanac chetnik detachments which were placed under command of local german division or area command post these orders required deployment of a german liaison officer with all detachments engaged in operations and also limited their movement outside their assigned area supplies of arms and ammunition were also controlled by germans in july 1942 mihailovic arranged for yugoslav government in exile to denounce pecanac as a traitor and his continuing collaboration ruined what remained of reputation he had developed in balkan wars and world war i dissolution germans found that pecanac s units were inefficient unreliable and of little military aid to them pecanac s chetniks regularly clashed and had rivalries with other german auxiliaries such as serbian state guard and serbian volunteer command and also with mihailovic s chetniks germans and puppet government commenced disbanding them in september 1942 and all but one had been dissolved by end of that year last detachment was disbanded in march 1943 his followers were dispersed to other german auxiliary forces german labour units or were interned in prisoner of war camps many deserted to join mihailovic nothing is recorded of pecanac s activities in months that followed except that he was interned for some time by serbian puppet government accounts of pecanac s capture and death vary according to one account pecanac four of his leaders and 40 of their followers were captured by forces loyal to mihailovic in february 1944 all were killed within days except pecanac who remained in custody to write his war memoirs before being executed on 5 may 1944 another source states he was assassinated on 6 june 1944 by chetniks loyal
