william joseph bill denny 6 december 1872 2 may 1946 was a south australian journalist lawyer politician and decorated soldier who held south australian house of assembly seats of west adelaide from 1900 to 1902 and then adelaide from 1902 to 1905 and again from 1906 to 1933 after an unsuccessful candidacy as a united labor party ulp member in 1899 he was elected as an independent liberal in a by election in 1900 he was re elected in 1902 but defeated in 1905 following year he was elected as a ulp candidate and retained his seat for that party australian labor party from 1917 until 1931 along with rest of cabinet he was ejected from australian labor party in 1931 and was a member of parliamentary labor party until his electoral defeat at hands of a lang labor party candidate in 1933 denny was attorney general of south australia and minister for northern territory in government led by john verran 1910 12 in august 1915 denny enlisted in first australian imperial force to serve in world war i initially as a trooper in 9th light horse regiment after being commissioned in 1916 he served in 5th division artillery and 1st divisional artillery on western front he was awarded military cross in september 1917 when he was wounded while leading a convoy into forward areas near ypres and ended war as a captain he was again attorney general in labor governments led by john gunn 1924 26 lionel hill 1930 33 and robert richards 1933 and held other portfolios in those governments including housing irrigation and repatriation denny published two memoirs of his military service and when he died in 1946 aged 73 he was accorded a state funeral early life william joseph denny was born in adelaide south australia on 6 december 1872 one of three children of thomas joseph denny a publican
