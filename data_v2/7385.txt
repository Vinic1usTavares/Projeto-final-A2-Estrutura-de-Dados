quinceanera referring to a coming of age ceremony in mexican communities is a 2006 american independent drama film written and directed by richard glatzer and wash westmoreland set in echo park los angeles film follows lives of two young mexican american cousins who become estranged from their families magdalena played by emily rios because of her unwed teenage pregnancy and carlos jesse garcia because of his homosexuality and are taken in by their elderly great uncle tomas chalo gonzalez film was inspired by glatzer and westmoreland s experience as a white gay couple moving into gentrifying neighborhood of echo park a predominantly latino working class community they wrote cast and filmed quinceanera over four months in 2005 after securing a us 400 000 budget from investors it was filmed in echo park with assistance of glatzer and westmoreland s neighbors and a cast of largely nonprofessional actors it premiered at sundance film festival on january 23 2006 where it won grand jury prize and audience award it was released in united states on august 2 2006 to mostly positive reviews and earned 2 5 million at box office plot magdalena a fourteen year old girl from a working class mexican american family in echo park los angeles attends her cousin eileen s quinceanera an extravagant coming of age ceremony to celebrate her fifteenth birthday eileen s older brother carlos who has been disowned by his family due to his homosexuality and now lives with his great uncle tomas arrives at celebrations but is forced to leave by his father magdalena herself is about to turn fifteen but her parents cannot afford to host a quinceanera as lavish as eileen s and they deny her repeated requests to hire a hummer limousine for occasion while preparing for her quinceanera magdalena learns that she is pregnant by her friend herman although they had only once engaged in non penetrative intercourse her christian father is furious believing that magdalena has had premarital sex despite her protestations that she is still a virgin she leaves her family to move in with tomas and carlos and continues to see herman until she discovers that his mother has sent him away to live with relatives to prevent him from seeing magdalena carlos becomes sexually involved with caucasian gay couple james and gary who recently bought tomas s property and are now his landlords and neighbors but he eventually begins a secret affair with gary without james s knowledge with magdalena s pregnancy progressing carlos offers to financially support her and to act as a surrogate father for child once it is born when james discovers his partner s affair with carlos he feels betrayed and tomas soon receives a letter notifying him that his landlords are evicting him tomas magdalena and carlos struggle to find an affordable place to live due to gentrification of area and rising real estate prices but tomas dies in his sleep shortly before they are due to be evicted in aftermath magdalena is reunited with her mother and together they visit a gynecologist who confirms that magdalena conceived without having penetrative sex magdalena s father apologizes to her at tomas s funeral believing her conception to be a miracle and she forgives him magdalena eventually receives quinceanera she had wished for complete with a hummer limousine with her parents in attendance and james guzman rico as maria production quinceanera was written and directed by richard glatzer and wash westmoreland filmmaking partners and a romantic couple they conceived idea for film in january 2005 based on their own experiences as a white gay couple moving into predominantly latino working class neighborhood of echo park los angeles as area underwent gentrification they were inspired to make a film about traditional quinceanera celebration after being invited to their fifteen year old neighbor s ceremony with producer anne clements they pitched idea to three investors immigrants to united states from greece and israel who agreed to provide 300 000 to finance project budget was later raised to 400 000 glatzer and westmoreland then wrote screenplay over three weeks in february casting for film took place over march 2005 through internet a los angeles based organization for latino actors and word of mouth glatzer and westmoreland chose to cast non union actors none of cast except chalo gonzalez belonged to screen actors guild most of actors were nonprofessional and had never acted in a film before emily rios s only experience before playing lead role of magdalena was in a school play while jesse garcia had only acted in commercials film s own casting director jason l wood ended up playing character of james and glatzer and westmoreland cast their cleaning lady in a small role they borrowed props from their cleaner s niece who had recently had a quinceanera and mimicked her video of ceremony to create part of film although script called for actors to speak spanglish a mixture of english and spanish neither glatzer nor westmoreland were fluent in spanish so many of actors translated their own lines from english teenage cast members also improvised dialogue for some scenes and provided their own clothes to wear in character film was shot over eighteen days in april 2005 it was filmed on location in echo park inside glatzer and westmoreland s house and in three houses on their block that their neighbors allowed them to use for little or no money many of glatzer and westmoreland s echo park neighbors also stood in as extras due to california s child labor laws they could only film with underage cast members for six hours a day so cinematographer eric steelberg used a hand held camera with few accessories to maximize time they could spend filming steelberg filmed project in high definition video format which was converted to film during post production film s soundtrack included reggaeton songs as well as music composed by westmoreland s brother as a favor since filmmakers could afford little else robin katz finished editing film in august 2005 release thumb right david w ross and jesse garcia at sundance film festival where quinceanera premiered in january 2006 quinceanera premiered on january 23 2006 at sundance film festival where it won both grand jury prize and audience award in dramatic feature category its u theatrical release film opened in limited release in united states on august 4 2006 earning 95 400 on its opening weekend from eight theatres it gradually expanded over next three weeks achieving a widest release of 96 theatres by its fourth weekend its theatrical run lasted for 14 weeks concluding with a total gross of 1 692 693 it grossed 830 094 internationally making a total box office gross of 2 522 787 it was released in dvd format on january 9 2007 extra features on dvd included an audio commentary with glatzer westmoreland and film s actors a making of featurette and a mock quinceanera home video made by glatzer and westmoreland reception critical response quinceanera received generally positive reviews from critics on rotten tomatoes film holds a rating of 86 based on 95 reviews with an average rating of 7 1 10 site s consensus reads this slice of life story of a teenage girl in echo park is both a sweet crowd pleaser and a perceptive look at socioeconomic community issues on metacritic film has a score of 72 out of 100 based on reviews from 31 critics indicating generally favorable reviews variety critic david rooney summarized quinceanera as a fresh spirited drama charming and unpretentious as well as a small gem of a movie with a stirring soul he praised subdued natural performances of inexperienced teenaged actors as well as chalo gonzalez s portrayal of tomas stephen holden of new york times described film as smart and warmhearted with a wonderfully organic feel for fluid interaction of cultures and generations in los angeles slates dana stevens commended film for avoiding cliches and for its sharp eyed analysis of class conflict she singled out performance of emily rios who she said carries movie on her square broad shoulders claudia puig of usa today awarded film three out of four stars and described it as spirited and poignant with rios performance providing heart of film wesley morris writing for boston globe found film to be a modest but remarkably poignant comedy and believed that despite predictability of broader story somehow it feels authentic in all its small details san francisco chronicle ruthe stein commented that film was directed with obvious love by glatzer and westmoreland and commended gonzalez for his hypnotic performance as tomas gianni who reviewed film for seattle post intelligencer wrote of its charm
