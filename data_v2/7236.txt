king s highway 32 commonly referred to as highway 32 was a provincially maintained highway in canadian province of ontario long route connected highway 2 in gananoque with highway 15 east of bay providing a quick alternative route between two highways it also featured an interchange with highway 401 highway 32 was assumed in 1929 and generally remained unchanged throughout its existence until 1998 when it was decommissioned and transferred to united counties of leeds and grenville it was subsequently redesignated as leeds and grenville county road 32 route description highway 32 began at highway 2 king street in gananoque and proceeded north for to highway 15 within gananoque road was known as stone street north an interchange with highway 401 lay just north of town north of which former highway travelled through farmland and forests today route is known as leeds and grenville county road 32 and lays entirely within leeds and thousand islands with exception to portion within town of gananoque history highway 32 was assumed on july 24 1929 following unimproved road between gananoque and highway 15 route was improved with a gravel surface by 1937 and paved between 1942 and 1949 interchange with highway 401 was opened along with freeway itself on august 6 1959 connecting existing kingston bypass and thousand islands parkway a new bridge was completed over gananoque river in 1961 bypassing original route a short distance to north and improving highway geometry highway 32 remained generally unchanged until january 1 1998 when entire route was decommissioned and transferred to united counties of leeds and grenville it has since been known as leeds and grenville county road 32 major intersections references external links highway 32 pictures and information 032
