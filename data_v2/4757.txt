wade echard mainer april 21 1907 september 12 2011 was an american country singer and banjoist with his band sons of mountaineers he is credited with bridging gap between old time mountain music and bluegrass and is sometimes called grandfather of bluegrass in addition he innovated a two finger banjo fingerpicking style which was a precursor to modern three finger bluegrass styles originally from north carolina mainer s main influences came from mountain music of his family in a career that began in 1934 and spanned almost six decades mainer transitioned from being a member of his brother s band into founder of his own ensemble sons of mountaineers with whom he performed until 1953 when he became more deeply involved with his christianity and left music industry after working at a general motors factory and attending gospel revivals mainer was convinced that he should restart his career as a christian gospel musician and began to tour with his wife in this capacity he continued to release albums until 1993 personal life mainer was born near weaverville north carolina on a mountain farm in buncombe county on april 21 1907 his family was poor during his childhood and they lived in a log cabin in blue ridge mountains mainer credited his father who was in mainer s words a good singer real stout voice as of one of his influences during his career as a musical artist mainer would perform many of old songs that he had heard from his father mainer grew up listening to traditional mountain music and was largely influenced by his brother in law roscoe banks he first learned to play banjo at square dances where he would pick up instruments left by performers and practice on them after moving to concord north carolina and working in a series of jobs at cotton mills he became a part of his brother j e s band known as j e mainer s mountaineers his entry into band in 1934 marked beginning of a nearly six decade career in music j e played fiddle while wade performed on banjo for string band and they played at fiddlers conventions and other gatherings mainer married julia mae brown at end of 1937 shortly after forming his own band brown was a singer and guitarist popularly known at time as hillbilly lilly she had performed from 1935 until 1937 at radio in winston salem brown is considered to be a pioneering female musical artist and later joined mainer during his performances musical career mainer s first recordings came in 1934 and are compiled on ragged but right 30 s country bands mainer performed with mountaineers on tracks such as maple on hill seven and a half and johnson s old grey mule also included on compilation are mainer s later collaboration short life and it s trouble with zeke morris his solo effort riding on that train 45 and a sample song mitchell blues from his band sons of mountaineers throughout his career he was noted for his unique and innovative two finger banjo fingerpicking style which some view as a precursor to three finger bluegrass banjo styles mainer took jobs at local radio stations to increase visibility of his relative s ensemble recording classics such as take me in lifeboat during this time he appeared on many regional stations including wbt in charlotte in raleigh in knoxville and in mount airy mainer performed in a series of live radio shows with mountaineers sponsored by crazy water crystals laxatives in 1934 j w fincher head of company observed their popularity at first gig crazy water crystal barn dance a radio program out of charlotte under name j e mainer s crazy mountaineers they toured american south on live radio shows and recorded fourteen songs for bluebird records maple on hill which according to national endowment for arts was their biggest hit had originally been composed in 1890s by gussie l davis mainer was in his brother j e s band for two years until he left for more traditional work which at time was far more profitable than his musical career making only five dollars a week under sponsorship mainer found that he could earn up to three times as much working at a yarn mill which he described as being gold for era after leaving his brother s group in 1936 he began to perform duet work with zeke morris who was a bandmate from mountaineers after a time working on this project mainer left to form short lived smilin rangers which later became sons of pioneers zeke morris then got together with his brother wiley to form morris brothers sons of mountaineers mainer named this new band sons of mountaineers its initial lineup included jay hugh hall and clyde moody as guitarists with steve ledford as a fiddler among musicians who would join group later were jack and curly shelton tiny dodson red rector and fred smith band got its start performing on radio and recording songs for bluebird records and their first hit entitled sparkling blue eyes was recorded in 1939 from 1935 through 1941 mainer recorded over 165 songs for record label rca victor in various lineups ranking him among one of most prolifically recorded country music artists of that period sons of mountaineers briefly stopped playing during world war ii because mainer could not afford to squander valuable gasoline required for journey to radio stations one notable exception however came in 1942 when they were invited to white house by eleanor roosevelt there in washington d c they played several tunes including down in willow garden a song personally requested by president franklin delano roosevelt during this time they also appeared in a version of chisholm trail in new york city at wars end band was reorganized and once again began to play at stations across north carolina recordings at this time were sporadic due to declining popularity of genre in 1953 after having renewed his commitment to christianity mainer left group and exited industry for a time later life in 1953 mainer and his wife settled in flint michigan where he found work at a general motors factory although renouncing both music industry and his trademark instrument banjo he and julia did continue to sing at gospel revival meetings in early 1960s molly o day convinced him that he could use banjo in gospel recordings which spurred a series of religiously themed banjo albums beginning in 1961 he also began to record and tour with his wife mainer retired from general motors in 1973 limited to united states pete smith of british newspaper advertiser in a report for mainer s 100th birthday cited mainer as one of most influential figures in development of modern bluegrass noting his picking style and his efforts in bringing bluegrass closer to mainstream in addition smith also credits him for making banjo an instrument previously described as satanic acceptable for spiritually themed music mainer continued to live with his wife in flint where he celebrated his centenary in 2007 and performed at a concert for his 101st birthday in 2008 awards and honours mainer is a recipient of a 1987 national heritage fellowship awarded by national endowment for arts which is united states government s highest honor in folk and traditional arts in 1996 he received michigan
