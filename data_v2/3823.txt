piotr skarga less often piotr 2 february 1536 27 september 1612 was a polish jesuit preacher hagiographer polemicist and leading figure of counter reformation in polish lithuanian commonwealth due to his oratorical gifts he has been called polish skarga is remembered by poles as a vigorous early advocate of reforms to polish lithuanian polity and as a critic of commonwealth s governing classes as well as of its religious tolerance policies he advocated strengthening monarch s power at expense of parliament sejm and of nobility szlachta he was a professor at krakow academy and in 1579 he became first rector of wilno academy later he served in jesuit college at krakow he was also a prolific writer and his lives of saints zywoty swietych 1579 was for several centuries one of most popular books in polish language his other important work was sejm sermons kazania sejmowe 1597 a political treatise which became popular in second half of 19th century when he was seen as patriotic seer who predicted partitions of poland life skarga was born on 2 february 1536 north of grojec in small folwark manor of also known as or his family are often described as lesser landless szlachta gentry or nobility but it seems likely most of his ancestors had been peasants later townsfolk who had only recently become minor nobility he was reared at family estate and lost his parents when he was young his mother died when he was eight years old and his father michal skarga four years later thereafter he was supported by his brothers one of whom stanislaw skarga was a priest piotr started his education at a parochial school in grojec before moving to krakow where in 1552 he enrolled at krakow academy precursor to jagiellonian university his teachers included priests marcin and jan he finished his studies in 1555 immediately after he finished his education he served for two years as rector of collegiate school at st john s church in warsaw from october 1557 he tutored jan teczynski son of magnate andrzej teczynski and visited vienna with his pupil where he likely became closely acquainted with society of jesus a key order of counter reformation he then returned to poland which emerged as one of main terrains of struggle between protestant reformation movement and catholic church s counter reformation from 1562 he served as a parson in and around 1564 he took holy orders that year he became a canon and following year he also served as chancellor of lwow chapter from 1566 to 1567 he was chaplain at court of castellan jan krzysztof tarnowski royal secretary to king sigismund ii augustus after tarnowski s death he returned to lwow taking up position of cathedral preacher in 1568 he departed for rome arriving in 1569 and joining society of jesus in 1571 he returned to poland and preached successively at lwow jaroslaw warsaw where he delivered a sermon before sejm and plock where he visited court of queen anna jagiellon who would become one of his patrons a leading proponent of counter reformation skarga commonly preached against non catholic denominations and helped secure funds and privileges for society of jesus thumb right 200px krakow plaque commemorating skarga in 1573 he was rector of wilno jesuit college precursor to wilno academy vilnius university in 1577 he became a professor at krakow academy that year he also finished one of his most important works lives of saints zywoty swietych which was published two years later in 1579 he became first rector of wilno academy in 1576 he published pro sacratissima eucharistia contra haeresim zwinglianam ad andream volanum for most sacred eucharist against zwinglian heresy to andrzej wolan in 1582 he published artes duodecim sacramentariorum sive zwinglio calvinistarum seven pillars on which stands catholic doctrine on most sacred sacrament of altar both these works formed part of skarga s dialogue with calvinist author andrzej wolan which took form of a series of rival polemics over a number of years wolan was a notable figure in commonwealth politics not only a publicist but a royal secretary diplomat and sejm deputy in 1584 skarga was transferred to new jesuit college at krakow on 26 march 1587 he founded polish version of mount of piety a pawnbroker run as a charity and called in polish bank pobozny lit pious bank in 1588 newly elected king sigismund iii vasa established new post of court preacher and skarga became first priest to hold it skarga became a valued adviser to king and sigismund became so fond of him that when priest considered retirement sigismund rejected this requesting that he remain at court for as long as possible as part of his counter reformation policies skarga was also a major proponent of union of brest a merger between roman catholic church and a faction of eastern orthodox church his influence on king sigismund whom he supported or encouraged in opposing religious tolerance and seeking to strengthen royal power was a factor that has been cited as a cause of civil war ultimately unsuccessful zebrzydowski rebellion of 1606 in which royal faction confronted a popular movement among nobility led by zebrzydowski family who sought to depose sigismund some critics referred to skarga as principal mischief maker of kingdom in latin in 1611 he delivered his final sermon before sejm and published his final work an ideological testament wzywanie do jednej zbawiennej wiary a call for one redeeming faith he remained sigismund s court preacher until april 1612 four months before his death skarga died on 27 september 1612 and was buried in saints peter and paul church in krakow writer in addition to being a popular and well known preacher skarga was author of numerous theological texts and polemics and it is as a writer that his fame has endured his two most important works are lives of saints zywoty swietych 1579 and sejm sermons kazania sejmowe 1597 former a hagiography won him fame in his lifetime while sermons gained recognition only in subsequent centuries during partitions of poland tazbir describes lives as skarga s chief work and as a major attack on religious tolerance promoted by warsaw confederation book was immensely popular first edition selling out by 1583 a second edition was published in 1585 and by mid 17th century twelve editions had been printed making it one of most popular books published in poland and lithuania in that era rivaled by kronika polska polish chronicles of marcin bielski it was skarga s most popular work and continued to rank highly with polish readers until 18th and 19th centuries when it was eclipsed by his sermons sermons a political treatise composed in guise of sermons went almost unnoticed by contemporaries in sermons skarga discusses what he sees as problems of ailing commonwealth lack of patriotism internal quarrels tolerance of heretics king s relative powerlessness perverse laws a critique of nobility s golden
