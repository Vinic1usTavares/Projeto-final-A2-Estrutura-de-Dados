john barney hines 1878 1958 was a british born australian soldier of world war i known for his prowess at taking items from german soldiers hines was subject of a famous photo taken by frank hurley that depicted him surrounded by german military equipment and money he had looted during battle of polygon wood in september 1917 this image is among best known australian photographs of war born in liverpool england in 1878 hines served in british army and royal navy and worked in several occupations he arrived in australia in 1915 and volunteered for australian imperial force in august 1915 although discharged due to poor health in early 1916 he rejoined in august that year and served on western front from march 1917 to mid 1918 when he was discharged again for health reasons during his period in france he proved to be an aggressive soldier and gained fame for collection of items that he amassed but was undisciplined when not in combat and frequently punished following world war i hines lived in poverty on outskirts of sydney until his death in 1958 early life hines was born johannes heim on 11 october 1878 in liverpool england to german immigrant parents jacob and dora heim his father jacob heim was one of many germans who emigrated to england to work in sugar refining industry he married hannah maher at our lady s church in eldon street liverpool in 1899 they had two children together scottie press he was known as john heim or in liverpool but by time of his first criminal conviction in new zealand in november 1904 he was using alternate surname hines he had a very bad criminal record in new zealand after over a decade in new zealand he moved to australia he worked his passage to australia as a fireman on ship somerset under name j heim ship arrived at sydney australia on 18 august 1915 nsw news 19 august 1915 world war i hines first joined australian imperial force aif on 24 august 1915 falsely claiming to be 28 years of age in year before he joined army he had worked as a seaman engineer and shearer he was discharged from aif as medically unfit on 20 january 1916 on 8 may hines successfully rejoined aif this time giving an age of 36 years and seven months by this stage of war medical requirements were less strict due to need for reinforcements to make good aif s casualties hines was assigned to 45th battalion and departed sydney for europe onboard hmat a18 wiltshire on 22 august 1916 after completing training in england hines joined 45th battalion on western front in march 1917 in june that year he captured a force of 60 germans during battle of messines by throwing hand grenades into their pillbox and was later wounded he returned to his battalion in time for battle of polygon wood in september where frank hurley photographed him on 27 september surrounded by loot he had captured hines was an aggressive soldier and it has been claimed that he killed more germans than any other member of aif though brave in battle and admired by his fellow soldiers his behaviour was erratic at times wartime commander of 45th battalion arthur samuel allen described hines to a journalist in 1938 as a tower of strength to battalion while he was in line hines enthusiasm for collecting german military equipment and german soldiers personal possessions became well known within and possibly outside of his battalion and earned him nickname of souvenir king although he collected some items from battlefields at ypres and somme region most were stolen from german prisoners of war he kept items he collected for himself and there are no records of any being handed over to australian war records section aif unit responsible for collecting items for later display in australia hines sold some of items he collected to other soldiers including for alcohol photograph of hines at battle of polygon wood was published in late 1917 under title wild eye souvenir king and became one of best known australian photographs of war many soldiers identified with hines and were amused by his collection of souvenirs photograph was used as propaganda and a false story developed that german kaiser wilhelm ii had become enraged after seeing it away from front line hines developed a record of indiscipline he was court martialled on nine occasions for drunkenness impeding military police forging entries in his pay book and being absent without leave he also claimed to have been caught robbing strongroom of a bank in amiens though this is not recorded in his army service record as a result of these convictions hines lost several promotions he had earned for his acts of bravery he was also fined on several occasions and resulting need for money may have been one of factors that motivated his looting a member of 3rd battalion described hines as not normally a weak man but rather one uncontrolled an officer from 45th battalion stated after war that hines had been two pains in neck in mid 1918 hines was discharged from aif as being medically unfit due to problems he arrived back in australia on 19 october 1918 while his army service file records that he was lightly wounded on two occasions hines later claimed to have been wounded five times later years hines was traumatised by his experiences during world war i for 40 years afterwards he lived in a humpy made of cloth bags near mount druitt on outskirts of sydney and never married humpy was surrounded by a fence on which he hung helmets taken from german soldiers he became well known to locals though school children were afraid of him hines was unable to find consistent work and lived on his army pension as well as income from odd jobs and selling his souvenirs he gained renewed fame when photo of him at polygon wood was displayed at temporary australian war museum in sydney predecessor of australian war memorial from 1933 and several newspapers and magazines aimed at former servicemen published profiles of him an article in returned sailors and soldiers imperial league of australia s magazine reveille in 1934 highlighted hines desperate living conditions and stated that he had been unemployed for four years several former soldiers sent money to him in response to this article hines pension was also doubled though this income made him ineligible for relief work during great depression despite his poverty hines travelled to concord repatriation hospital each week to donate a suitcase of vegetables from his garden to former soldiers being treated there hines told a journalist in june 1939 that he was seeking to join militia and hoped to fight in another war he attempted to enlist in military during world war ii despite being in his 60s but was rejected an article published in nepean times during 1943 claimed that hines had attempted to stow away on a troop ship in 1940 but was found and sent ashore before vessel sailed on 28 january 1958 hines died at concord repatriation hospital aged 84 or 85 he was buried in rookwood cemetery in a grave which was unmarked until 1971 when mount druitt sub branch of returned services league of australia paid for a headstone blacktown city council also renamed street on which he lived in suburb of to john hines avenue and a monument commemorating him was built at nearby mount druitt waterholes remembrance garden in 2002 a large version of famous photograph of hines was accorded a prominent position in australian war memorial s permanent building in canberra after it opened in 1941 photo was also included in 2014 redevelopment of memorial s permanent world war i exhibition in a short biography of hines published in 2002 historian peter stanley commented that wild eye s bravado conceals a deeper pathos and he was a man whose skills in fighting were needed and
