air commodore sir hughie idwal edwards 1 august 1914 5 august 1982 was a senior officer in royal air force governor of western australia and an australian recipient of victoria cross highest decoration for gallantry in face of enemy that can be awarded to members of british and commonwealth armed forces serving as a bomber pilot in royal air force raf edwards was decorated with victoria cross in 1941 for his efforts in leading a bombing raid against port of bremen one of most heavily defended towns in germany he became most highly decorated australian serviceman of second world war born in fremantle western australia edwards joined royal australian air force in 1935 and a year later was granted a short service commission with raf serving throughout second world war he gained a permanent commission and continued his career in raf after war he retired in 1963 with rank of air commodore returning to australia he was appointed governor of western australia in 1974 early life edwards was born in fremantle western australia on 1 august 1914 third of five children to welsh parents hugh a blacksmith and farrier and his wife jane nee watkins who had emigrated to australia in 1909 named after his father he was always referred to by his middle name of idwal in his family edwards received his initial education at white gum valley school before attending fremantle boy s school where he achieved well academically although he later claimed this was due to a good memory rather than high intelligence however edwards was reluctantly forced to leave school at age of fourteen as family finances could no longer support him described as a shy under confident introspective and imaginative lad at this stage in his life he gained employment as a shipping office clerk with onset of great depression edwards found himself unemployed before gaining a job with a horse racing stable in fremantle his position entailed him taking horses to beach each morning for a swim grooming them and attending twice weekly race meetings a lifelong interest in horse racing consequently emerged at this time after later working for a brief period in a factory he enlisted in australian army in march 1934 and was posted to 6th heavy battery royal australian artillery with rank of private during this period edwards was an active sportsman excelling in australian rules football where he played six matches with leading western australian football league wafl club south fremantle and cricket with fremantle garrison team early air force career in 1935 he was selected for flying training with royal australian air force at raaf point cook after which he transferred to raf being granted a short service commission as a pilot officer on 21 august 1936 posted to no 15 bomber squadron he was appointed adjutant of no 90 squadron in march 1937 flying bristol blenheim bombers he received a promotion to flying officer on 21 may 1938 london gazette 7 june 1938 in august 1938 edwards was piloting a blenheim near scottish border when he flew into a storm at when ailerons froze aircraft was forced down to and edwards ordered navigator and rear gunner to bail out of aircraft down to he made an effort to jump clear but his parachute became entangled with bomber s radio mast pylon in ensuing crash he sustained head injuries and a badly broken leg which was only saved after extensive surgery which left that leg shorter than other after accident he was declared unfit for flying duties until april 1940 when he was posted to no 139 squadron for active service due to outbreak of war he was promoted to flight lieutenant on 21 may 1940 london gazette 25 june 1940 second world war in may 1941 edwards became commanding officer of no 105 squadron replacing previous incumbent who had been killed in an anti shipping raid on stavanger at that time squadron was engaged in a series of daylight operations against germany and occupied countries with its principal targets being enemy shipping power installations shipbuilding yards locomotives steelworks and marshalling yards on 15 june by now an acting wing commander edwards led six blenheims on a search for enemy shipping and soon sighted a convoy of eight merchantmen anchored near hague he launched an attack at low level his bombs striking a 4 000 ton ship he was subsequently awarded distinguished flying cross for this action on 4 july 1941 edwards led a daylight attack operation wreckage against port of bremen one of most heavily defended towns in germany edwards force of twelve blenheims attacked at a height of about 50 feet through telephone wires and high voltage power lines bombers successfully penetrated fierce anti aircraft fire and a dense balloon barrage but further fire over port itself resulted in loss of four of attacking force edwards brought his remaining aircraft safely back although all had been hit and his own blenheim serial had been hit over 20 times
