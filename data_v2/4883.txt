eleanor sophia smith 15 june 1858 30 june 1942 was an american composer and music educator she was one of founders of chicago s hull house music school and headed its music department from 1893 to 1936 born into a musical family smith taught herself to play piano and later became a classically trained musician earning a teaching degree she began publishing music compositions for children using philosophy of friedrich frobel advocating for less memorization and drilling and more attention to intuitive appreciation of music studying composition and voice in germany she also toured country observing choirs and their teaching techniques returning to united states in 1890 smith began working at settlement house hull house as a music instructor within three years she had co founded hull house music school a school which followed her progressive teaching ideas cross training students in vocal music as well as instruments simultaneously she worked in several institutions in chicago area which trained music educators smith published numerous compilations of songs including two six volume textbook series which were widely used throughout united states most of her writings were focused on children s voices and contained short songs written with attention paid to limited range and short attention span of children many of her compositions were still being used in music education programs in latter part of 20th century early years and education born in atlanta illinois in 1858 eleanor was daughter of willard newton and matilda smith she came from a musical family as her father was a noted bass in first presbyterian church choir in chicago between 1860 and 1871 in an interview she gave in 1908 smith claimed that both sides of her family were musical noting that a paternal great grandfather composed hymns and that her maternal line was a musical mix of danish french german and jewish ancestry smith taught herself to play piano and did not begin any formal training for voice or composition until she was eighteen years old she had very poor vision which limited her ability to use her eyes for long periods of time she studied at hershey school of musical art and published her first cantata golden asp while still a student there she later graduated from cook county normal school but continued to study music privately as a pupil of frances a root voice works as cradle song a million little diamonds only thine own mine art quest and she kisses with her eyes smith gained a reputation as a songwriter by early 1890s and many of her works were translated into german during this period she also wrote hymns though they may have remained in manuscript composition smith began publishing for children in 1887 with her first volume of children s music entitled songs for little children part 1 she was a proponent and follower of teaching philosophy of friedrich frobel smith incorporated frobel s ideas that childhood is a universal experience in which children learn by imitation using their natural abilities her compositions also recognized that children learn by movement and play but have limited attention spans thus compositions she created were usually short melodies with limited vocal ranges and were easy to sing songs were in major keys typically c f and g and extended only from c1 to f2 most melodies were made in stepped triads and provided pauses for activity for example in her song wind mill there is an eight measure rest for children to make motions of turning blades thumb eleanor smith 1901 newspaper photo from article about rhythm patterns in this volume most of compositions were smith s own work though some of pieces were adaptations of german folk songs smith s first textbook series modem music series in six volumes should be allowed to learn intuitively it set forth a new movement in music instruction which rejected traditional sight singing memorization method of training and instead relied on song method in songs and became basis for similar textbooks based on her ideas and works such as publication in 1909 by charles h congdon called congdon music readers series which incorporated 21 songs by smith smith published a second textbook series eleanor smith music series in 1908 which also contained six volumes and was widely distributed in us public schools in 1910 smith published songs of a little
