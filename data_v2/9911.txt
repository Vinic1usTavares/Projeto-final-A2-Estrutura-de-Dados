joe hicks tipton february 18 1922 march 1 1994 was an american professional baseball player he played as a catcher in major league baseball mlb from 1948 through 1954 with cleveland indians chicago white sox philadelphia athletics and washington senators in 417 career games tipton recorded a batting average of 236 and accumulated 29 home runs and 125 runs batted in rbi born and raised in georgia tipton played amateur baseball until cleveland indians signed him he spent two years in minor leagues before serving in world war ii he played two more years in minor leagues upon his return then made his mlb debut with indians in 1948 indians traded him to white sox in 1949 then white sox sent him to philadelphia athletics in 1950 tipton spent two and a half years with athletics before returning to cleveland after spending a season with washington senators he played and managed in minor leagues for a few seasons before being banned from minor leagues after ban he moved to birmingham alabama where he died in 1994 early life and career tipton was born and raised in georgia as fifth of seven children his birthplace was on tennessee border so it was originally registered as copperhill tennessee tipton attended epworth high school and copperhill high school in tennessee where he played on high school basketball and football teams he moved to shannon georgia at age of 16 in order to pursue a career in baseball tipton began his playing career in textile league with brighton mills in shannon in 1938 and spent next three years with talladega team while with talladega he was spotted by a scout from cleveland indians organization who signed tipton to a professional contract after being signed by indians tipton made his professional debut with appleton papermakers of wisconsin state league where he had a 298 batting average and 11 home runs in 59 games he spent most of year there and played in ten games with flint arrows of michigan state league in 1942 tipton was promoted to charleston senators indians class c minor league affiliate in one of his first games with senators tipton hit two triples and a home run to give his team win by a score of seven to four in 81 games with team tipton had a 313 batting average military service before 1943 season began tipton joined military to serve in world war ii he served in united states navy and was part of pacific theater of operations from 1943 to 1945 during his time in pacific he served on uss bay ship was part of a kamikaze attack in january 1945 tipton emerged unscathed from attack after end of war tipton returned to baseball he spent 1946 season with harrisburg senators and had a 327 batting average in 104 games in 1947 he was promoted to wilkes barre barons of eastern league that season he had a 375 batting average which led league following season tipton joined cleveland indians for spring training by end of march he was considered a sure thing to make major league roster major league career cleveland indians decided to keep only two catchers on roster for 1948 cleveland indians season and manager lou boudreau chose tipton to back up jim hegan he made his major league debut on may 2 and a month later in a game on june 6 he had five hits in five plate appearances to give cleveland a win against philadelphia athletics tipton played in 47 games over course of season and had a 289 batting average he also played in one game during 1948 world series striking out in his only appearance after world series indians owner bill veeck wanted to improve pitching staff despite finding tipton to be worthy of a starting job and despite veeck considering trading hegan instead tipton was traded to chicago white sox for joe haynes early in season white sox manager jack onslow accused tipton of tipping pitches after a loss to st louis browns and fined him 500 two then fought and tipton threatened to leave team but they buried hatchet a short time later he played in 67 games during season and ended year with a 204 batting average and three triples on october 19 1949 white sox traded tipton to philadelphia athletics for nellie fox fox played 14 years for white sox and went on to become a hall of famer and as a result trade is considered one of most lopsided in baseball history during spring training for athletics tipton battled mike guerra and joe astroth for starting job at catcher all three ended up splitting time over course of season tipton finished year hitting 266 with six home runs and 20 rbis in 64 games jimmy dykes took over as athletics manager in 1951 and had high hopes for tipton heading into season saying that he is a lot better catcher than most people think in may tipton was hit in head by a stray baseball bat during a game and spent some time in hospital as a result he split time with astroth and ray murray during season but appeared in a career best 72 games hitting 239 with 51 walks after season ended tipton was part of lefty o s american baseball team that toured japan to start 1952 season tipton was rumored to be subject of a trade involving boston red sox and two of their players but deal never materialized tipton missed part of may with a kidney infection and struggled upon returning hitting 191 in 23 games in late june he was waived by athletics and picked up by indians he spent rest of year with them and finished with nine home runs 30 rbis and a 225 average in 66 games tipton remained backup catcher to hegan during 1953 season he battled a myriad of injuries throughout season including a bruised hand a broken thumb a split finger and losing a fingernail he finished year with a 229 batting average in 47 games on january 20 1954 tipton was traded to washington senators for mickey grasso in an exchange of catchers manager bucky harris traded for tipton in hopes that he would compete for starting job due to his defense tipton served as backup catcher to ed fitz gerald in 1954 he made his last mlb appearance on september 26 and in 54 games he had a 223 average later life new york giants purchased contracts of tipton and wayne terwilliger on january 14 1955 who sent them to minor league minneapolis millers while terwilliger played for millers that year tipton had planned to retire from baseball and never played a game for minneapolis later that year white sox general manager frank lane wanted him to join memphis chickasaws so that young pitchers in minor leagues could work with a veteran catcher tipton obliged and joined team for 1955 season in 60 games for chickasaws that season tipton had a 277 batting average and seven home runs after playing in 60 games tipton was suspended after an argument with chickasaws manager jack cassini and after season ended he retired from game to work at his service station he returned to baseball in 1957 and spent a season with birmingham barons and partway through 1958 season he joined panama city fliers as manager he was fired a month later and in protest fliers refused to play their next game tipton retired afterwards and a year later was banned for life from minor leagues after it came to light that he accepted payouts by acting as a liaison for jesse levan who was intentionally fouling off balls in order for gamblers in stands to take advantage of them his baseball career over tipton moved to birmingham alabama with his wife and three
