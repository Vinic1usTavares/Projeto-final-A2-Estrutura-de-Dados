st ceinwen s church cerrigceinwen is a former parish church in countryside of central anglesey north wales present building dates from 1860 although site has been used for worship since at least 7th century doorway reuses some old carved gravestones one from 9th to 11th centuries and another from 12th century church grounds contain a well once thought to have healing properties church and well are both named after st ceinwen an early celtic female saint church is closed and no longer used for worship by church in wales and as of july 2012 was for sale it is a grade ii listed building a national designation given to buildings of special interest which warrant every effort being made to preserve them in particular because it is a simple rural church from 19th century that reuses older
