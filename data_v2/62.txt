churchill war rooms is a museum in london and one of five branches of imperial war museum museum comprises cabinet war rooms a historic underground complex that housed a british government command centre throughout second world war and churchill museum a biographical museum exploring life of british statesman winston churchill construction of cabinet war rooms located beneath treasury building in whitehall area of westminster began in 1938 they became fully operational on 27 august 1939 a week before britain declared war on germany war rooms remained in operation throughout second world war before being abandoned in august 1945 after surrender of japan after war historic value of cabinet war rooms was recognised their preservation became responsibility of ministry of works and later department for environment during which time very limited numbers of public were able to visit by appointment in early 1980s imperial war museum was asked to take over administration of site and cabinet war rooms were opened to public in april 1984 museum was reopened in 2005 following a major redevelopment as churchill museum and cabinet war rooms but in 2010 this title was shortened to
