amal women s training center and moroccan restaurant is a non profit organization in marrakesh morocco that helps disadvantaged women gain work experience by training them in preparation of moroccan food and international food center was established in 2012 by nora belahcen fitzgerald each year 30 40 women complete four to six months of training which often leads to them finding employment in a relevant field history thumb alt a banner with center s logo hanging next to 2 large wooden doors that open to restaurant s courtyard entrance to amal women s training center and moroccan restaurant nora belahcen fitzgerald first conceived of idea of creating an organization to help morocco s underprivileged women through career training in 2006 after meeting a single mother begging on street who lived on 2030 dirhams per day about eur2 70 3 40 in 2006 belahcen fitzgerald was inspired by association feminine which helps single mothers overcome poverty and social stigma by providing them with shelter counselling and vocational training she decided to begin helping women by teaching them to make baked goods in her family s language center this proved to be successful and participants were able to make more money using their new skills than by begging in 2008 belahcen fitzgerald began hiring underprivileged moroccan women to cook for events she organized for her friends in 2012 belahcen fitzgerald found a property to rent and registered amal women s training center and moroccan restaurant as a non profit organization in early 2013 center raised more than 7300 through crowdfunding on rockethub exceeding its 5000 goal money was used to purchase kitchen equipment and to ensure kitchen was up to code restaurant opened in april 2013 and in july 2013 center was awarded a three year grant by drosos foundation with goal of making program self sustaining by 2016 first group of trainees began training in february 2014 within first month of operation several of restaurant s customers had expressed interest in hiring future amal graduates to work at local hotels and restaurants center completed a second round of crowdfunding on rockethub in 2014 raising more than 9600 in october 2015 center was awarded eur25 000 by orange foundation belahcen fitzgerald stated that prize money would be used to help women start their own catering micro businesses restaurant thumb alt trainees
