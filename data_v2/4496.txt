6th regiment massachusetts volunteer militia was a peacetime infantry regiment that was activated for federal service in union army for three separate terms during american civil war regiment gained notoriety as first unit in union army to suffer fatal casualties in action during civil war in baltimore riot and first militia unit to arrive in washington d c in response to president abraham lincoln s initial call for 75 000 troops private luther c ladd of 6th massachusetts is often referred to as first union soldier killed in action during war in years immediately preceding war and during its first enlistment regiment consisted primarily of companies from middlesex county during its first term of service four out of ten companies of regiment were from lowell massachusetts colonel edward f jones commanded regiment during its first term he later commanded 26th massachusetts and was awarded honorary grade of brevet brigadier general during its second and third terms of service unit was commanded by colonel albert s follansbee regiment first enlisted for a 90 day term of service which lasted from april 16 to august 2 1861 following their engagement in baltimore riot 6th massachusetts proceeded to washington and then returned to baltimore to guard locations within city as well as baltimore and ohio railroad station at elkridge maryland their second term of service
