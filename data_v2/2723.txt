jose maria caro martinez 1830 11 november 1916 was a chilean politician and civil servant in may 1894 he was unanimously elected as first mayor of commune of pichilemu with pedro nolasco de mira and francisco reyes made second and third magistrate respectively caro martinez had previously served for several years as administrator or llavero of san antonio de petrel hacienda and between 1891 and 1892 was subdelegate of 13th subdelegation of san fernando department which comprised district of cahuil eleven year mayorship of caro martinez which lasted from 1894 and 1905 was described by journalist and local historian jose arrano acevedo as most fruitful in history of commune in his four terms caro martinez built roads connecting pichilemu and san fernando capital of department of same name and founded several schools in pichilemu and parts of current marchigue he resigned in may 1905 and completed his fourth mayoral term as a segundo alcalde as a mayor he was succeeded by francisco javier asalgado who held office for two non consecutive terms caro martinez was father of following progeny jose maria caro rodriguez who became first chilean cardinal of roman catholic church francisco adriano caro rodriguez who became first regidor for multiple terms intermittently since 1906 and then eighth mayor of pichilemu on resignation of luis barahona fornes in december 1925 as well as pedro pablo caro rodriguez a lawyer from university of chile who became an active judge in several cities early life thumb left caro martinez was llavero administrator of hacienda san
