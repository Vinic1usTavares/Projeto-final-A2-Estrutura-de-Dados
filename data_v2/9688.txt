fort jackson was an american civil war era fortification in virginia that defended southern end of long bridge near washington d c long bridge connected washington d c to northern virginia and served as a vital transportation artery for union army during war fort jackson was named for jackson city a seedy suburb of washington that had been established on south side of long bridge in 1835 it was built in days immediately following union army s occupation of northern virginia in may 1861 fort was initially armed with four cannon used to protect bridge but these were removed after completion of arlington line a line of defenses built to south after 1862 fort lacked weapons except for small arms and consisted of a wooden palisade backed by earthworks two cannon were restored to fort in 1864 following battle of fort stevens garrison consisted of a single company of union soldiers who inspected traffic crossing bridge and guarded it from potential saboteurs following final surrender of confederate states of america in 1865 fort jackson was abandoned lumber used in its construction was promptly salvaged for firewood and construction materials and due to its proximity to long bridge earthworks were flattened in order to provide easier access to long bridge in early 20th century fort s site was used for footings and approaches to several bridges connecting virginia and washington today no trace of fort remains though site of fort is contained within arlington county s long bridge park and a national park service 2004 survey of site indicated some archaeological remnants may
