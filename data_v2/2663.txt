zou chenglu 17 may 1923 23 november 2006 better known as chen lu tsou was a chinese biochemist he was a professor of shanghai institute of biochemistry and later a professor and deputy director of institute of biophysics chinese academy of sciences cas he made important contributions to synthesis of insulin and was elected an academician of cas and world academy of sciences twas he won twas prize in biology in 1992 for his pioneering study of enzyme inhibition kinetics and was a six time laureate of state natural science award three times each for first class and second class his wife physicist li lin was also an academician of cas tsou was a strong advocate against academic fraud and pseudoscience and led a public campaign against what he called unhealthy practices such as administrators interference in scientific research early life and education tsou was born on 17 may 1923 in qingdao shandong province with his ancestral home in wuxi jiangsu during second sino japanese war he sought refuge in kunming in china s interior and studied chemistry at national southwestern associated university after graduation in 1945 he briefly served in army during world war ii after war tsou was awarded a government scholarship to study in england initially bound for university of birmingham on recommendation of wang yinglai he was accepted by university of cambridge to study under david keilin at molteno institute for research in parasitology career thumb left tsou aboard a ship to england august 1947 tsou s doctoral thesis was on properties of cytochrome c according to edward slater research was first step towards eventual discovery of protein s structure after he and his wife both acquired their ph d degrees in 1951 they returned to newly established people s republic of china and tsou became a research professor at shanghai institute of physiology and biochemistry where wang yinglai served as a deputy director after 1958 tsou was a member of team at shanghai institute of biochemistry that first achieved total chemical synthesis of insulin in 1965 his major contribution to project was to form bridges by joining two synthetic polypeptides using oxidation his method for calculating chemical modification of proteins is known as tsou s equation in 1981 he was awarded state natural science award first class for this achievement in 1970 tsou moved to beijing to help look after his ailing father in law li siguang and transferred to institute of biophysics chinese academy of sciences it was in midst of cultural revolution when scientific activity was frozen in anti intellectual political atmosphere when american biochemist emil l smith a fellow alumnus of molteno institute visited tsou following richard nixon s 1972 visit to china tsou used reagent bottles filled with water to maintain a pretense of scientific research after end of cultural revolution tsou was able to resume his research and was elected as an academician of chinese academy of sciences in 1980 he pioneered study of enzyme inhibition kinetics for which he was awarded twas prize in biology in 1992 despite losing an entire decade of his prime he published at least 118 papers mostly in international journals in 1990 his autobiography was published in comprehensive biochemistry volume 27 by end of his career he won state natural science award first class three times and second class three times activism thumb upright tsou and li at their wedding with li s parents li siguang and xu tsou was a strong advocate against academic fraud and pseudoscience and display of his achievement in cell formation criticism poisoned his relationship with bei making tsou feel uneasy at institute scientist rao yi later raised same objections and praised tsou s probity personal life in 1948 tsou married li lin anna tsou a fellow chinese student at department of metallurgy of cambridge li was daughter of renowned geologist li siguang who was in england to preside
