2 43rd battalion was an infantry battalion of australian army raised in july 1940 in south australia as part of 24th brigade battalion was initially part of 8th division until 24th brigade was re allocated to 9th division in late 1940 it was with this formation that 2 43rd saw service in middle east in 1941 1942 taking part in fighting at tobruk and in first and second battles of el alamein it also undertook garrison duties in syria before returning to australia early in 1943 to fight against japanese in pacific in 1943 1944 after re organisation and training to prepare for jungle warfare 2 43rd fought in new guinea as part of operations to capture lae and secure huon peninsula after returning to australia in early 1944 a long period of training followed on atherton tablelands in queensland before battalion undertook its final campaign of war taking part in allied operations to re take borneo from japanese in june 1945 following end of war battalion was disbanded in february 1946 one member of battalion private tom starcevich was awarded victoria cross history formation and training 2 43rd battalion was raised at woodside south australia on 17 july 1940 from second australian imperial force 2nd aif volunteers it was one of three infantry battalions assigned to 24th brigade other two being 2 25th and 2 28th battalions which was initially assigned to 8th division like other australian infantry battalions of time 2 43rd had an authorised strength of around 900 personnel and was organised into four rifle companies designated a through to d each consisting of three platoons these were supported by a battalion headquarters and a headquarters company with six specialist platoons signals pioneer anti aircraft transport carriers and mortars upon formation 2 15th was placed under command of lieutenant colonel william crellin an australian staff corps officer and duntroon graduate of permanent military forces who arrived on promotion from major having previously served in world war i with 14th battalion colours initially chosen for battalion s unit colour patch ucp were chocolate and dark blue in an oval shape inside a circle of grey these were same as those of 43rd battalion a south australian unit that was part of first australian imperial force during world war i that unit had been re raised in south australia in 1921 as a militia formation eventually adopting designation of hindmarsh regiment 2 43rd s unit colour patch was later changed following unit s involvement in fighting at tobruk when it adopted a t shaped ucp consisting of red and white battalion s personnel during early stages of its existence were volunteers who enlisted within 4th military district which encompassed south australia and broken hill new south wales many of commissioned officers and some of non commissioned officers had previous military experience in militia while bulk of enlisted personnel had no prior military service rudimentary individual soldier training was conducted along with sports meets throughout august followed by skill at arms training and range shoots in september while key appointment holders were sent away on various promotion and other specialist training courses in early october battalion was presented with its battle flag by veterans who had served in 43rd battalion during world war i and a short time later it was announced that 24th brigade would be transferred to join 9th division in middle east more intense training including company and battalion level field training was conducted in weeks that followed in late november 2 43rd received their unit colour patches in preparation for deployment and battalion marched through centre of adelaide finally on 28 december 1940 men entrained at oakbank and were transported by rail to melbourne where they embarked on transport mauretania north africa and middle east escorted by cruiser hmas canberra mauretania proceeded along southern australian
