arunachalesvara temple also called annamalaiyar temple is a hindu temple dedicated to deity shiva located at base of arunachala hill in town of thiruvannamalai in tamil nadu india it is significant to hindu sect of saivism as one of temples associated with five elements pancha bhoota and specifically element of fire or agni shiva is worshiped as arunachalesvara or annamalaiyar and is represented by lingam with his idol referred to as agni lingam his consort parvati is depicted as unnamalai amman presiding deity is revered in 7th century tamil saiva canonical work tevaram written by tamil saint poets known as nayanars and classified as paadal petra sthalam 9th century saiva saint poet composed here temple complex covers 10 hectares and is one of largest in india it houses four gateway towers known as gopurams tallest is eastern tower with 11 stories and a height of making it one of tallest temple towers in india built by sevappa nayakkar nayakar dynasty temple has numerous shrines with those of arunachalesvara and unnamalai amman being most prominent temple complex houses many halls most notable is thousand pillared hall built during vijayanagar period temple has six daily rituals at various times from 5 30 a m to 10 p m and twelve yearly festivals on its calendar deepam festival is celebrated during day of full moon between november and december and a huge beacon is lit atop hill it can be seen from miles around and symbolizes shiva lingam of fire joining sky event is witnessed by three million pilgrims on day preceding each full moon pilgrims circumnavigate temple base and arunachala hills in a worship called girivalam a practice carried out by one million pilgrims yearly present masonry structure was built during chola dynasty in 9th century while later expansions are attributed to vijayanagara rulers of sangama dynasty 1336 1485 ce saluva dynasty and tuluva dynasty 1491 1570 ce temple is maintained and administered by hindu religious and charitable endowments
