tropical storm jose was a short lived tropical storm which made landfall in central mexico during august 2005 jose was tenth named storm of 2005 atlantic hurricane season and fourth of six tropical cyclones three hurricanes and three tropical storms to make landfall in mexico in that year tropical storm jose formed in bay of campeche on august 22 and made landfall in mexican state of veracruz next day it retained tropical characteristics for less than one day before dissipating but still brought heavy levels of rainfall to region mudslides caused by rainfall killed eight people six of those directly and caused 45 million 2005 usd in damage meteorological history tropical storm jose was first identified as a tropical wave that moved off western coast of africa on august 8 2005 on august 13 system spawned tropical depression ten over central atlantic wave itself continued westward entering caribbean on august 17 slight development took place as system moved over yucatan peninsula however by time it entered bay of campeche on august 21 little convection was associated with system following morning convection exploded under highly favorable divergence from an upper level anticyclonic flow according to readings from quikscat satellite a well defined low pressure center developed by 1200 utc prompting national hurricane center to classify system as tropical depression eleven at this time depression was situated roughly 110 mi 175 km east of veracruz mexico situated over very warm waters and within an area of low wind shear depression was able to quickly organize however due to its proximity to land nhc noted system does not have very long to take advantage of these favorable conditions located to south of a mid level ridge depression tracked just north of due west and kept this motion through remainder of its existence depression rapidly intensified as it moved to west becoming tropical storm jose just six hours later global model guidance initially failed to resolve storm s track well with some models indicating that it would stall offshore jose continued to strengthen as it moved towards coast and made landfall north of veracruz city early on august 23 with winds reaching a peak of 60 mph 95 km h as jose made its landfall an eye was beginning to form although storm was still well short of hurricane intensity tropical storm jose
