bass maltings in sleaford england are a large group of eight disused malt houses originally owned by bass brewery of burton upon trent constructed between 1901 and 1907 to herbert a couchman s design maltings are largest group of malt houses in england they have been designated grade ii on national heritage list for england recognising them as particularly important of more than special interest part of predominantly agricultural county of lincolnshire sleaford area was a major producer of barley in 1880s when germinated and dried to form malt barley forms a key ingredient in production of 19th century sleaford area was a major barley producer and by 1880s sleaford was a stop on railway lines connecting town to boston grantham bourne spalding and lincoln these qualities made town desirable for production of malt a crucial ingredient in beer production brewers bass ratcliffe and gretton ltd were attracted to town and in 1880 proposed a new malting complex there which would consolidate malting closer to barley source and reduce costs by up scaling production drilling in 1892 revealed suitable artesian water sources under sleaford plans were submitted nine years later and over 13 acres of land off mareham lane was purchased work started in 1901 and was complete by 1907 company ran complex at full capacity until after second world war however in 1950s bass installed new and more efficient pneumatic malting systems at their original burton on trent plant making sleaford complex redundant and paving way for its closure in 1959 vacant space was let to businesses but enormous complex was only ever partially occupied after bass left vulnerable to damage and poorly maintained fires started in 1969 and 1976 latter resulting in major damage to three of malthouses fire
