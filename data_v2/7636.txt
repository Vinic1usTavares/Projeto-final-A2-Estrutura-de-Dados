mohawk airlines flight 411 a fairchild fh 227b twin engine turboprop registered n7811m was a scheduled domestic passenger service operated by mohawk airlines between albany and glens falls new york on november 19 1969 it crashed into pilot knob mountain killing all 14 passengers and crew on board national transportation safety board ntsb concluded that crash was caused by captain s improper execution of an instrument approach combined with a severe downdraft at a low altitude which resulted in aircraft descending uncontrollably into terrain history of flight on evening of november 19 1969 at about 20 03 est mohawk airlines flight 411 a twin engine fairchild fh 227b turboprop departed from albany international airport near albany new york it was operating as a scheduled passenger cargo flight on an instrument flight rules ifr flight plan to its destination of warren county airport in glens falls new york northeast with an estimated flight time of about 15 minutes at 20 07 32 est just 4 minutes after takeoff flight 411 was cleared by air traffic control atc for a vor approach to runway 19 aircraft overflew glens falls airport and proceeded northbound subsequently reversing
