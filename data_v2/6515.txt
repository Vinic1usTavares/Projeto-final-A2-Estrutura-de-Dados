ginetta sagan june 1 1925 august 25 2000 was an italian born american human rights activist best known for her work with amnesty international on behalf of prisoners of conscience born in milan italy sagan lost her parents in her teenage years to black brigades of benito mussolini like her parents she was active in italian resistance movement gathering intelligence and supplying jews in hiding she was captured and tortured in 1945 but escaped on eve of her execution with help of nazi defectors after studying in paris she attended graduate school in child development in us and married leonard sagan a physician couple then resettled in atherton california where sagan founded first chapter of amnesty international in western us she later toured region helping to establish more than 75 chapters and organized events to raise money for political prisoners in 1984 sagan was elected honorary chair of amnesty international usa us president bill clinton awarded her presidential medal of freedom in 1996 and italy later awarded her rank of grand ufficiale ordine al merito della repubblica italiana grand official order of merit of italian republic amnesty international founded an annual ginetta sagan award for activists in her honor childhood and world war ii ginetta sagan was born in milan italy to a catholic father and a jewish mother both of her parents were doctors facing rising antisemitism in europe her parents arranged false papers identifying her as christian to hide her jewish roots when world war ii began both of her parents became active in italian resistance movement opposing fascist rule only to be arrested in 1943 by mussolini s black brigades her father was later shot in a staged attempted escape and her mother sent to auschwitz where she died ginetta then seventeen years old was already active in resistance movement delivering food coupons and clothing to jews who were in hiding following her parents disappearance she became a courier for resistance forces in northern italy as well as helping to print and distribute antigovernment pamphlets on one occasion she dressed as a cleaning lady to steal letterhead from government offices so that it could be used to forge letters of safe passage to switzerland due to her energy and small size she never grew to more than five feet tall she received nickname topolino little mouse in late february 1945 sagan was betrayed by an informer in movement and like her parents arrested by black brigades during her 45 days of imprisonment she was beaten raped and tortured leading up to a scheduled april 23 execution at one point a jailer tossed her a loaf of bread that contained a matchbox with word courage written inside a moment which would motivate much of her later work on behalf of prisoners on day of her scheduled execution she was being beaten by guards in a villa in italy when a pair of german officers forced her italian captors to release her into their custody she later recalled watching stars from window of their car and thinking i will never see another dawn however germans revealed themselves to be nazi defectors collaborating with her resistance comrades and they delivered sagan safely to a catholic hospital sagan annually celebrated date of april 23 for rest of her life post war life after sagan recuperated she lived in paris for a time with her godfather attending sorbonne in 1951 she emigrated to us to study at university of illinois at chicago majoring in child development while there she met leonard sagan then a young medical student couple were married following year and would remain together until leonard s death in 1997 following their marriage pair moved to washington d c for leonard s work sagan also worked part time teaching cooking classes to wives of us congressmen couple later lived in boston and japan before settling in atherton california in 1968 sagan lived there until her death from cancer on august 25 2000 ginetta is survived by her three
