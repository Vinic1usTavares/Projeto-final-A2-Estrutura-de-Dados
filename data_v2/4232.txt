aberdour castle is located in village of easter aberdour fife scotland parts of castle date from around 1200 making aberdour one of two oldest datable standing castles in scotland along with castle in argyll which was built at around same time tabraham scotland s castles p 33 earliest part of castle comprised a modest hall house on a site overlooking dour burn over next 400 years castle was successively expanded according to contemporary architectural ideas hall house became a tower house in 15th century and was extended twice in 16th century final addition was made around 1635 with refined renaissance details and whole was complemented by a walled garden to east and terraced gardens to south terraces dating from mid 16th century form one of oldest gardens in scotland and offer extensive views across firth of forth to edinburgh castle is largely creation of douglas earls of morton who held aberdour from 14th century earls used aberdour as a second home until 1642 when their primary residence dalkeith house was sold a fire in late 17th century was followed by some repairs but in 1725 family purchased nearby aberdour house and medieval castle was allowed to fall into decay today only 17th century wing remains roofed while tower has mostly collapsed aberdour castle is now in care of historic environment scotland and is open to public all year history origins barony of
