percy herbert cherry vc mc 4 june 1895 27 march 1917 was an australian recipient of victoria cross highest decoration for gallantry in face of enemy that can be awarded to members of british and commonwealth armed forces award was granted posthumously for cherry s actions during an attack on french village of lagnicourt which was strongly defended by german forces born in australian state of victoria cherry moved to tasmania at age of seven when his family took up an apple orchard becoming an expert apple packer he was also a skilled rifle shot and member of franklin rowing club in 1913 he was commissioned as a second lieutenant in 93rd infantry regiment citizens military force and served as a drill instructor at outbreak of war enlisting in australian imperial force in march 1915 he served at gallipoli before transferring to western front and fighting in battle of somme in early march 1917 during battle of arras cherry was decorated with military cross following an attack on malt trench in which he led a party in capturing two german machine gun posts he was killed by a german shell day following his victoria cross action early life cherry was born on 4 june 1895 at drysdale victoria to john cherry and his wife elizabeth nee russel when he was seven years old family moved to tasmania and took up an apple orchard near cherry attended local state school until he was thirteen after which he received private tuition he worked with his father and became an expert apple packer at fourteen he won a local case making competition at launceston fruit show by packing thirty five cases of apples in an hour joining australian army cadets in 1908 cherry soon became a sergeant and later a second lieutenant where he used to drill cadets in four different districts at age of sixteen he won president s trophy and gold medal for being best shot at rifle range in franklin he also rowed with franklin rowing club played cornet in franklin brass band and sang in anglican church choir in 1913 cherry joined citizens military force and was commissioned into 93rd infantry regiment as a second lieutenant first world war enlistment march 1915 to western front march 1917 at outbreak of war cherry was sent to claremont camp and assumed duties as a drill instructor on 5 march 1915 he enlisted in australian imperial force and was allotted to 26th battalion where he qualified as an infantry officer but was considered too young for a commission in australian imperial force and was instead made a quartermaster sergeant on 29 june battalion embarked from brisbane for egypt with cherry aboard hmat aeneas on arrival battalion spent several months training in desert where cherry was promoted to company sergeant major in august on 12 september battalion landed at gallipoli and played a defensive role at courtney s and steele s posts and russell s top thumb alt black and white head and shoulders portrait of a man in a suit upright left 180px studio portrait of cherry while recovering from wounds in london on 1 december injured by bomb wounds to his face and head cherry was evacuated to egypt where he was commissioned as a second lieutenant on 9 december three days later 26th battalion was withdrawn from gallipoli and evacuated to egypt where cherry rejoined them in preparation for service on western front remaining in egypt during this time cherry was selected to attend a machine gun course in march 1916 after completion of this he was transferred to 7th machine gun company in france he commanded company s 1st battery at fleurbaix messines and on somme until 5 august when he was wounded in a duel with a german officer at pozieres he and a german officer who was leading an attack against cherry s position were exchanging shots from neighbouring shell holes eventually they both rose firing simultaneously german officer hit cherry in neck but was mortally wounded by cherry in return cherry approached dying man who pulled a package of letters from his pocket and asked cherry to have them censored and posted cherry promised to do so and german handed over letters with words and so it ends he died shortly afterwards as a result of his wounds cherry was evacuated to england for treatment promoted to lieutenant on 25 august 1916 cherry returned to his unit on somme in november following month he was made a temporary captain and transferred back to 26th battalion as commanding officer of c company his rank was confirmed on 14 february 1917 and on 1 2 march he was involved in battalion s actions around village of warlencourt at 03 00 on 2 march cherry led his company in an attack on german held position of malt trench situated between villages of warlencourt and bapaume artillery fire had failed to adequately cut barbed wire lines for advancing troops and cherry led a section of men along wire until he found a break in it once through gap he rushed two machine gun posts capturing one single handed and turned one on fleeing germans before being wounded himself for his actions during engagement cherry was awarded military cross notification of which was published in a supplement of london gazette on 26 april 1917 victoria cross on 26 march 1917 7th brigade of which 26th battalion was part was tasked with capture of lagnicourt as part of battle of arras it was during this engagement where cherry was to earn victoria cross an artillery barrage opened up on village at 05 15 and continued for twenty minutes allowing infantry to close in plan was that cherry s company would storm village itself while battalion s other companies encircled it for assault cherry split his company into two sections he commanded one section himself and placed other under lieutenant william frederick joseph hamilton thumb right percy cherry s medals on display at australian war memorial canberra company was soon in action cherry s section captured a large fortified farm on edge of village as they entered muddy main street australians came under heavy fire from houses lining road and from an adjacent stable yard cherry rushed stable yard and a fierce firefight developed before germans surrendered as cherry and his men moved through lagnicourt they found main resistance coming from a large chalky crater spotted with white chalk at crossroads in village centre germans stout defence was holding up advance and cherry sent a messenger back for more stokes mortars becoming impatient at delay cherry decided to rush position under cover of lewis gun and rifle grenade fire capturing crater he found lieutenant harold hereward bieske at bottom wounded bieske had taken over command of second section when lieutenant hamilton was wounded and it had now been reduced to six men after capturing crater cherry pushed on through village where his party emerged from among buildings on far side another stiff fight ensured with a group of germans in dug outs by side of road before company was able to meet up with its fellow units which had bypassed village and were already established to north and east of area cherry s orders were to fall back into reserve but he disregarded them as he sensed a counter attack was forthcoming at 09 00 on 27 march germans launched a very strong counter attack under heavy artillery fire at one point cherry noticed that germans were firing yellow flares to pinpoint australian positions to their gunners he found some of these flares and fired them away from his position german attempts to retake lagnicourt at a cost to 7th brigade of 377 casualties raged all day before they abandoned counterattack death and legacy in afternoon of 27 march 1917 a shell burst in a sunken road to east of lagnicourt killing cherry and several other men he was buried in
