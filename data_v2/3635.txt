boris vasilievich snetkov 27 february 1925 18 september 2006 was a soviet army and briefly russian ground forces army general snetkov fought in world war ii as a self propelled artillery officer and during cold war rose to command positions he commanded 1st guards tank army and served as first deputy commander of group of soviet forces in germany during 1970s snetkov led siberian military district and leningrad military district in 1980s and in 1987 became commander of group of soviet forces in germany gsfg which in 1989 became western group of forces as cold war wound down snetkov was removed from command of group in december 1990 and transferred to group of inspectors general of ministry of defense before retiring in 1992 early life and world war ii thumb 243x243px an isu 152 of type used by 395th guards regiment snetkov was born on 27 february 1925 in saratov and graduated from pugachyov s school no 2 he was drafted into red army in 1942 and in october 1943 graduated from accelerated courses at 2nd kiev self propelled artillery school as a junior lieutenant as commander of an su 152 self propelled gun in 9th separate guards breakthrough tank regiment of 1st ukrainian front s 38th army snetkov saw his first combat west of in battle of kiev on 25 november during which his crew was reported by a superior to have destroyed a tiger tank and two guns for his actions snetkov received order of red star on 12 december as advance westwards across ukraine continued into winter he and his regiment fought in zhitomir berdichev offensive and korsun offensive as part of 38th army during latter operation on 26 january his crew was reported by a superior to have engaged a german tank column of about 20 tanks and destroyed three one of which was a tiger on road for this action snetkov received order of patriotic war 1st class on 2 february in february 1944 his regiment was withdrawn from front and converted into 395th guards heavy self propelled artillery regiment it was also reequipped with updated isu 152 self propelled gun one of which snetkov became commander of snetkov and his regiment did not return to front until june 1944 where they became part of 3rd belorussian front he was promoted to lieutenant around this time and from late june he fought in operation bagration soviet strategic offensive that recaptured belarus and eastern poland between 22 and 27 june during fighting in area snetkov s crew was reported by a superior to have destroyed a self propelled gun two bunkers and two anti tank guns for this he received a second order of red star on 10 july regiment as part of front fought in baltic offensive and operation during fall thumb snetkov in 1945 snetkov became adjutant to regimental commander and was promoted to senior lieutenant from january 1945 395th guards heavy self propelled artillery regiment fought in east prussian offensive according to a report made by his regimental commander snetkov helped coordinate entry of self propelled guns into battle and often personally visited front lines under heavy fire to pass on orders between 13 and 21 january on 15 january 2nd battery commander s radio malfunctioned leading to battery getting lost despite heavy german mortar fire snetkov was able to pass on orders to battery allowing it to fulfil its mission for these actions he received medal for courage on 20 february regiment fought in battle of konigsberg and samland offensive in last weeks of war between 23 and 25 april in fighting around pillau snetkov s battery destroyed a self propelled gun 9 guns 8 bunkers a mortar battery and 15 machine gun positions for this action he received order of patriotic war 2nd class on 6 may after end of war in europe 395th guards heavy self propelled artillery regiment was transferred to far east for soviet invasion of manchuria snetkov became its chief of intelligence and assistant chief of staff for intelligence and ended war with rank of captain between 8 august and 3 september regiment fought in soviet invasion of manchuria attached to 5th army and on 8 september he received a second order of patriotic war 1st class for providing crucial intelligence gained from reconnaissance to his unit cold war between 1946 and 1950 snetkov was assistant chief of staff for operations of a regiment and then entered military academy of armored forces graduating in 1953 until 1965 he successively served as a regimental chief of staff chief of a division s operations staff department and commander of a tank regiment between 1965 and 1966 snetkov was chief of staff of a division and in 1968 he graduated from military academy of general staff after graduating snetkov took command of a tank division in kiev military district and in may 1971 transferred to group of soviet forces in germany gsfg as chief of staff and first deputy commander of 3rd shock army he was promoted to major general in 1972 and would serve in gsfg for better part of decade he was appointed commander of 1st guards tank army in august 1973 after which he was promoted to lieutenant general in 1974 and became first deputy commander in july 1975 he left east germany to command siberian military district in january 1979 and was promoted to colonel general that year moving westwards again to lead leningrad military district in november 1981 on 7 may 1986 snetkov was promoted to army general in november 1987 snetkov was appointed commander of gsfg which became western group of forces wgf in june 1989 when it was shifted to a defensive role during upheavals in east germany during october and november wgf s troops did not intervene in leipzig monday demonstrations and fall of berlin wall as a result of orders given to snetkov by soviet ambassador in east germany vyacheslav which were confirmed by soviet government after fall of berlin wall and easing of cold war tensions discipline in wgf began to break down in response to charges of fatal recruit abuse widespread desertion and weapons smuggling among soviet troops snetkov held a november 1990 press conference to deny charges in which he stated that only 84 soldiers had died in 1990 mostly in training or traffic accidents that 83 soldiers had left units without permission and denied allegations weapons smuggling stating that only seven weapons were missing in response to german citizens complaints he also halted all training on weekends and holidays and ended low level flying over populated places in december after former east german leader erich honecker was issued an arrest warrant by prosecutors in berlin and charged with giving orders to fire on escapees snetkov refused to hand over honecker who was under soviet protection at a military hospital he opposed withdrawal of soviet troops from germany and stated in multiple early 2000s interviews with russian military newspaper krasnaya zvezda that his opposition to a soviet pull out was reason for his dismissal from command of wgf later that month however according to a contemporary izvestia report he was fired along with deputies after desertions of a regimental commander and a supply unit leader on 29 november who took two missiles three tank shells and other armaments with them after his dismissal snetkov joined group of inspectors general of ministry of defense traditionally a pre retirement post for elderly generals he retired in may 1992 after elimination of group of inspectors general later life snetkov lived in moscow and died there on 18 september 2006 he was buried in troyekurovskoye cemetery personal life and politics snetkov joined communist party of soviet union in 1945 and was a deputy of supreme soviet of soviet union at its tenth and eleventh convocations which lasted from 1979 to 1989 he was also a candidate member of central committee of communist party between 1986 and 1990 during march 1989 soviet union legislative election first relatively free soviet elections snetkov who was backed by communist party lost his seat which represented a district in yaroslavl to reformist lieutenant colonel viktor in a campaign focused on issue of existence of soviet draft snetkov married aleksandra ivanovna who died 2011 and had a son vladislav borisovich snetkov who also became an army officer and who died in 1997 awards snetkov was awarded following decorations order of lenin order of october revolution order of patriotic war 1st class 3 order of patriotic war 2nd class order of red star 2 order for service to homeland in armed forces of ussr 3rd class medal for courage notes
