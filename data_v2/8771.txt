bani na im bani na im is a palestinian town in southern west bank located east of hebron in hebron governorate known as brekke in pre roman era it was later referred to as caphar barucha in 4th century town is best known as burial place of lot following muslim conquest its name was arabized as kafr al barik tomb of lot was turned into a mosque during islamic rule and remained so under crusader rule later arab tribe of bani nu aym settled there giving town its current name bani na im first used by muslim scholar abd al ghani al nabulsi in 1690 bani na im grew in population during british mandate for palestine it joined 1936 39 arab revolt as site of a battle between irregular palestinian arab forces of abd al qadir al husayni and british army following 1948 arab israeli war bani na im was annexed by jordan it came under israeli occupation after israel captured west bank during six day war in 1967 in 1997 bani na im was transferred to palestinian administration and consequently became a municipality today it serves as a commercial center for hebron area villages although most government services are in hebron town had a population of 20 084 inhabitants in 2007
