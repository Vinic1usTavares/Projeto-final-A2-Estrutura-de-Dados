poquita ropa is twelfth studio album by guatemalan singer songwriter ricardo arjona it was released on august 24 2010 through warner music it was recorded in mexico city new york city and los angeles and was produced by lee levin and dan warner under their production name los gringo with additional works from matt rollings and brian lang markedly different in style from his previous albums poquita ropa was produced using as few musical instruments as possible arjona s goal was to produce songs almost as they were born he achieved this by using nonexistent sounds to make songs sound more acoustic arjona achieved a production style that made songs sound like a cappella performances and he said that music and women look better with little clothes album was critically and commercially successful became his third number one album on billboard top latin albums and latin pop albums chart and also topped charts in argentina and mexico it was certified platinum in argentina and mexico and was certified gold in chile colombia and united states album was nominated at 53rd grammy awards for best latin pop album poquita ropa was given generally positive reviews jason birchmeier of allmusic considered album a stripped down acoustic effort and said that it was impressive poquita ropa spawned three singles puente a song with cuban influences was released as lead single from album and peaked at number 36 on billboard latin pop songs chart other two singles released were vida and marta both of which failed to appear on charts instead of promoting album with a worldwide tour
