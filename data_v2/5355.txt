typhoon rammasun known in philippines as typhoon butchoy was recognized as second typhoon of 2008 pacific typhoon season by japan meteorological agency jma and philippine atmospheric geophysical and astronomical services administration pagasa rammasun was also recognised as third tropical storm second typhoon and first super typhoon of 2008 pacific typhoon season by joint typhoon warning center jtwc rammasun formed on may 5 as a tropical disturbance next day joint typhoon warning center issued a tropical cyclone formation alert on developing disturbance on may 7 both jtwc and japan meteorological agency designated disturbance as a tropical depression while pagasa named depression butchoy later that day both jma and jtwc upgraded depression to a tropical storm with jma naming storm rammasun on may 9 both jtwc and jma upgraded rammasun to a typhoon next day jma and jtwc reported that rammasun had reached its peak winds of 195 km h 120 mph and 250 km h 155 mph respectively which made rammasun a category 4 super typhoon on saffir simpson hurricane scale after reaching its peak intensity rammasun weakened and was downgraded to severe tropical storm on may 12 before jtwc declared it extratropical and issued their final advisory jma did not issue their final advisory until several hours later within japan an estimated 10 hectares 24 7 acres of farmland were damaged in
