antonio berni rosario 14 may 1905 buenos aires 13 october 1981 was an argentine figurative artist he is associated with movement known as nuevo realismo new realism a latin american extension of social realism his work including a series of juanito laguna collages depicting poverty and effects of industrialization in buenos aires has been exhibited around world biography thumb left berni in 1920s early life berni was born in city of rosario on may 14 1905 his mother margarita was argentine daughter of italians his father napoleon an immigrant tailor from italy died in first world war in 1914 berni became apprentice of catalan craftsman n at and co stained glass company he later studied painting at rosario catala center where he was described as a child prodigy in 1920 seventeen of his oil paintings were exhibited at salon mari on november 4 1923 his impressionist landscapes were praised by critics in daily newspapers la nacion and la prensa paris jockey club of rosario awarded berni a scholarship to study in europe in 1925 he chose to visit spain as spanish painting was in vogue particularly art of joaquin sorolla ignacio zuloaga anglada and julio romero de torres but after visiting madrid toledo segovia granada cordoba and seville he settled in paris where fellow argentine artists horacio butler alfredo xul solar hector and lino enea spilimbergo were working he attended city of lights workshops given by andre lhote and othon at academie de la grande chaumiere berni painted two landscapes of de paris landscape of paris mantel amarillo yellow tablecloth la casa del crimen house of crime nude and muerta con guitarra still life with guitar he went back to rosario for a few months but returned to paris in 1927 with a grant from province of santa fe studying work of giorgio de chirico and rene magritte berni became interested in surrealism and called it a new vision of art and world current that represents an entire youth their mood and their internal situation after end of world war a dynamic and truly representative movement his late 1920s and early 1930s surrealist works include la torre eiffel en la pampa eiffel tower in pampa la siesta y su sueno nap and its dream and la muerte en cada esquina death lurks around every corner he also began studying revolutionary politics including marxist theory of henri lefebvre who introduced him to communist poet louis aragon in 1928 berni continued corresponding with aragon after leaving france later recalling it is a pity that i have lost among many things i have lost letters that i received from aragon all way from france if i had them today i think they would be magnificent documents because in that correspondence we discussed topics such as direct relationship between politics and culture responsibilities of artist and intellectual society problems of culture in colonial countries issue of freedom several groups of asian minorities lived in paris and berni helped distribute asian newspapers and magazines to which he contributed illustrations thumb right desocupados 1934 nuevo realismo in 1931 berni returned to rosario where he briefly lived on a farm and was then hired as a municipal employee argentina of 1930s was very different from paris of 1920s he witnessed labor demonstrations and miserable effects of unemployment and was shocked by news of a military coup d etat in buenos aires see infamous decade surrealism didn t convey frustration or hopelessness of argentine people berni organized de estudiantes y artistas and became a member of local communist party berni met mexican artist david alfaro siqueiros who had been painting large scale political murals on public buildings and was visiting argentina to give lectures and exhibit his work in an effort to argentine people his popular nuevo realismo paintings include desocupados unemployed and manifestation both were based on photographs berni had gathered to document as graphically as possible abysmal berni s post 1950s work can be viewed as a synthesis of pop art and social realism in 1958 he began collecting and collaging discarded material to create a series of works featuring a character named juanito laguna series became a social narrative on industrialization and poverty and pointed out extreme disparities existing between wealthy argentine aristocracy and of slums as he explained in a 1967 le monde interview one cold cloudy night while passing through miserable city of juanito a radical change in my vision of reality and its interpretation occurred i had just discovered in unpaved streets and on waste ground scattered discarded materials which made up authentic surroundings of juanito laguna old wood empty bottles iron cardboard boxes metal sheets etc which were materials used for constructing shacks in towns such as this sunk in poverty latin american art expert mari carmen ramirez has described juanito works as an attempt to seek out and record typical living truth of underdeveloped countries and to bear witness to terrible fruits of neocolonialism with its resulting poverty and economic backwardness and their effect on populations driven by a fierce desire for progress jobs and inclination to fight ramirez mari carmen cantos university of texas at austin 1999 p 190 notable juanito works include retrato de juanito laguna portrait of juanito laguna el mundo a juanito world promised to juanito and juanito va a la ciudad juanito goes to city art featuring juanito and ramona montiel a similar female character won berni grand prix for printmaking at venice death after march 1976 coup berni moved to new york city where he continued painting engraving collaging and exhibiting new york struck him as luxurious consumerist materially wealthy and spiritually
