laurent gabriel eketebi later eketebi 13 may 1936 february 2006 was a congolese politician who served as president of equateur province from june 1960 until september 1962 and as president of moyen congo province from then until june 1964 he later served as state commissioner of transport and communications from july 1972 until january 1975 when he was dismissed and charged with various financial crimes eketebi was convicted but received a pardon in 1994 he died in 2006 biography early life laurent eketebi was born on 13 may 1936 in coquilhatville belgian congo to a ngombe father and a mongo mother he attended administrative and commercial section of groupe scolaire officiel run by freres des ecoles chretiennes in coquilhatville where he received six years of primary education and six years of secondary education graduating in 1954 he later married beatrice y and had nine children with her eketebi worked in colonial administration initially serving as a clerk for equateur provincial finance service in third class of sixth grade of civil service in 1957 he was promoted to a position in fourth grade a level normally reserved for belgians he held post until 1960 from 1952 to 1955 he was a member of council of centre extra de coquilhatville an organisation with responsibility of overseeing activities of native congolese in coquilhatville eketebi was a member of association du personnel indigene de la colonie a labour union and in 1958 he served as its provincial secretary he also served as secretary general of coquilhatville section of federation du nord de l equateur eketebi obtained a prominent position in equateur branch of mouvement national congolais a political party but later left it to join jean bolikango s parti de l unite nationale puna provincial government career in march 1960 eketebi was appointed to equateur executive college a transitional body meant to administer region until congo s independence on 30 june together with its two other members he monitored disorders that occurred during subsequent general elections he used his multi ethnic background to broker a compromise that resulted in a coalition between puna parti national du progres and union des mongo equateur provincial assembly subsequently elected him president of province after elections puna gradually divided into two different wings one led by eketebi and other by bolikango thumb eketebi served as president of equateur province highlighted from 30 june 1960 until september 1962 eketebi took office on 30 june despite secessions of katanga and south kasai in july and august respectively he actively discouraged separatist activities in equateur his government undertook development projects during its tenure he approved of colonel joseph desire mobutu s coup in september which overthrew central government and subsequent installation of college of commissioners general eketebi participated in coquilhatville conference in may 1961 that year increasing tensions between ngombe and mongo factions in provincial assembly brought about an administrative breakdown eketebi worked intensively to improve relations between two groups an attempt by several deputies in october to censure his government and force his dismissal failed his tenure ended in september 1962 equateur was subsequently broken up into smaller provinces on 14 september 1962 eketebi filed his candidacy for president of new province of moyen congo new provincial assembly subsequently elected him to post he was unanimously reelected by assembly in april 1963 and was invested with public functions portfolio in december eketebi in a dispute with bolikango defected from puna and joined parti democratique congolais following month bolikango called a puna congress to lisala to reorganise his party despite such political conventions being prohibited by central government on 26 january conflict broke out when gendarmes attempted to disperse attendees by end of next day 19 people were shot and killed bolikango and eketebi both denied responsibility but local population held latter at fault he became increasingly politically isolated and faced disagreement in his own cabinet his vice president requested that central government depose him an investigation by a public prosecutor did not hold anyone responsible for 26 27 january incident but nevertheless concluded that moyen congo was in crisis central government subsequently proclaimed an etat d exception in province and placed it under control of a special commissioner opposition deputies from lisala and bumba were thus able to meet to discuss dismantling of eketebi s government in early june lisala bumba deputies replaced pro eketebi president of assembly and forced all of ministers of government to resign eketebi narrowly escaped a censure vote only due to his popular support in and lisala territories then on 21 june provincial deputies brought up a motion of censure accusing him of partisan and discriminatory politics disorganisation lacking in an effective government programme misappropriating public funds for personal purposes encouraging corruption stoking conflict between bumba and lisala regions and holding responsibility in january incident they voted to dismiss him still in possession of support of some riverine peoples he challenged legitimacy of government that replaced his however in 1965 elections bolikango was reelected to his seat in national parliament by a wide margin thus eclipsing eketebi s popularity in region national government career eketebi served as deputy director of office of head of state from december 1965 until 1969 from 1969 until 1970 he served as ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to tanzania from january to july 1971 he served as ambassador to conseil de l entente with residence in abidjan on 2 july 1971 eketebi was appointed vice minister of foreign affairs by president mobutu in july 1972 he was made state commissioner of transport and communications on 7 january 1975 he was dismissed from his post later that month eketebi was stripped of national order of leopard and a criminal investigation was opened against him government accused him of misappropriating 14 million 58 million belgian francs and 727 000 united states dollars and filed 48 charges of corruption and financial crimes against him
