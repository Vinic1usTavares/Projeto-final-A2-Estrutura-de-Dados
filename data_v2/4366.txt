sir hugh cloberry christian kb 1747 23 november 1798 was an officer of royal navy who saw service during american war of independence and french revolutionary wars details of his early life are obscure but he appears to have served initially in english channel and mediterranean before obtaining rank of captain and going out to north america with commodore joshua rowley christian was rowley s flag captain on for several years and saw action in several of naval engagements of american war of independence returning to britain at end of war he spent a period without active employment before receiving a post as second captain aboard lord howe s flagship 100 gun during spanish armament he temporarily left her when crisis abated but outbreak of french revolutionary wars led to his return to queen charlotte christian stepped down from her in 1794 to join transport board and in 1795 was promoted to rear admiral he was made commander in chief in leeward islands station and given task of transporting a large troop convoy twice he attempted crossing of atlantic and twice he was forced back by severe gales which ravaged his fleet and wrecked a number of merchant ships in convoy he made a third attempt in 1796 and succeeded in shepherding fleet to its destination he was then active in using troops and his naval forces to capture islands of saint lucia saint vincent and grenada before returning to england he was made second in command at cape of good hope in 1797 succeeding to commander in chief following year and he died while in command there in november 1798 family and early life christian descended from residents of isle of man was born at hook norton oxfordshire in 1747 his father thomas christian was a captain with his own privateer whilst his mother anne penny was a poet he followed his father into navy in 1761 spending time in english channel and mediterranean but few details of his early service survive other than that he took his lieutenant s examination in 1767 and received his commission on 21 january 1771 he married ann leigh resident of isle of wight on 6 march 1775 and was promoted to master and commander in 1778 american war of independence christian received a further promotion to flag captain on 8 december 1778 and took command of 74 gun flying broad pennant of commodore joshua rowley rowley sailed from spithead on 25 december in command of a squadron of seven ships of line which was part of fleet under overall command of lord shuldham escorting trade convoys to colonies rowley and suffolk were assigned to west indies bound convoy suffolk arrived in leeward islands on 12 february 1779 and christian remained with rowley in west indies serving with vice admiral john byron at battle of grenada on 6 july 1779 christian led van of british attack and suffolk lost seven killed and 25 wounded byron returned to england after this being replaced by rear admiral hyde parker with rowley retaining suffolk as his flagship towards end of 1779 intelligence reached parker that three french ships had been sighted from morne fortune sailing northward parker sent rowley to intercept them and after chasing them for several hours all three were captured they were revealed to be 42 gun fortunee 36 gun blanche and 28 gun ellis with all three being added to royal navy christian was again in action on 18 december helping to capture and destroy a large french convoy off martinique and took part in engagements off martinique under admiral george rodney in april 1780 over three engagements that took place suffolk lost one man killed and 33 wounded rowley shifted his flag to 74 gun after these clashes and christian was transferred to command 38 gun frigate he had helped to capture in 1779 he was present at battle of chesapeake on 5 september 1781 after which he returned to west indies he and fortunee formed part of sir samuel hood s fleet with christian being present at battle of st kitts on 25 and 26 january 1782 attached to centre division he was still with rodney s fleet when battle of saintes was fought against comte de grasse on 9 to 12 april 1782 end of war christian sailed north from jamaica on 21 july 1782 with admiral hugh pigot s fleet arriving at new york city on 5 september returning to port royal with hood s fleet in february following year he spent several months cruising in area he returned to england with hood in april 1783 and left active service following end of war with signing of treaty of paris he presumably did not receive any further employment until spanish armament in 1790 when he is recorded as being appointed as second captain aboard lord howe s flagship 100 gun easing of tensions meant that queen charlotte was paid off at end of year and christian again found himself without a ship french revolutionary wars outbreak of french revolutionary wars in early 1793 provided christian with further employment queen charlotte was recommissioned and he again became her second captain still under lord howe he stepped down from his post in august following year becoming a commissioner of transport board and on 1 june 1795 he was advanced to rear admiral of blue he was then appointed commander in chief of leeward islands station in 1796 and was instructed to take a fleet out with a convoy of transports carrying soldiers for operations against french and dutch colonies there he duly hoisted his flag aboard 98 gun and assembled his squadron and transports numbering over two hundred merchants carrying 16 000 men and making up largest troop convoy to leave england to that date attempts to cross atlantic fleet did not leave spithead until 16 november departure having been delayed until late in season delay proved disastrous two days after departing a westerly gale blew up dispersing fleet and driving ships back to port several of merchantmen were wrecked with heavy loss of life over 200 bodies washed up on coastline between portland and bridport christian shifted his flag to 90 gun after deeming prince george to be too badly damaged to take to sea and after gathering his fleet again set sail on another attempt to cross atlantic on 9 december again ships encountered severe gales that caused considerable damage forcing nine of warships and fifty of merchantmen to struggle back to port on 29 january some of convoy successfully made crossing while others were wrecked or captured by enemy ships storms were afterwards alluded to as christian s gales christian again shifted his flag this time to 74 gun while he waited for repairs to his ships to be completed and merchantmen gathered to make a third attempt to reach west indies while ashore he was invested as a knight companion of order of bath on 17 february 1796 and on 20 march he left spithead again bound for west indies third voyage was successful and he arrived at carlisle bay barbados on 21 april he joined with forces of station s current commander sir john laforey several days later and took over command from him christian assembled his forces and on 26 april sailed to invade st lucia with a detachment of troops under lieutenant general sir ralph abercromby invasion was successful and island was surrendered to british on 25 may christian went on to use his naval forces to support reduction and capture of islands of saint vincent and grenada later years christian was succeeded as commander in west indies by rear admiral henry harvey who had arrived in late june and christian returned to england in october aboard he was advanced to rear admiral of white on 20 february 1797 and was appointed as second in command of cape of good hope station later in year and duly sailed to take up post on 44 gun hms virginie he succeeded admiral thomas pringle as station commander in 1798 but died on 23 november that year at age of 51 his wife who had been seriously ill for sometime died two months later without having heard of her husband s death hugh cloberry christian had been created a peer and chose title of lord ronaldsway to honour his ancestor manx politician but died before patent reached him he was buried at cape he and his wife had two sons and
