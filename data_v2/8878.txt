made them popular among motorcycle riders originally developed in 1930s for firemen working on steam locomotives boots gained substantial popularity in post world war ii era during a growing motorcycling culture they became popular symbols of teenage rebellion in 1950s and a common component of greaser wear they were later adopted by skinheads and punks in 1970s by 2010s engineer boots history origins around 1860 frye company produced a popular line of harness boots quickly adopted by united states cavalry they featured an ankle strap that attached to metal rings a style which may have served as inspiration for design of engineer boot during 1930s chippewa shoe manufacturing company developed a pair of boots with stovepipe shafts based on style of english horse riding boots west coast shoe company wesco began manufacturing their engineer boots in 1939 engineer boots were originally meant as protective gear for firemen working on steam railway engines i e engineers as
