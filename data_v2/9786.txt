djadoeg djajakusuma 1 august 1918 28 october 1987 was an indonesian film director and promoter of traditional art forms born to a nobleman and his wife in temanggung central java djajakusuma became interested in arts at a young age choosing to pursue a career in theatre during japanese occupation from 1943 to 1945 he was a translator and actor and in four year national revolution which followed he worked for military s educational division several news agencies and in drama in 1951 djajakusuma joined national film corporation perfini at invitation of usmar ismail after making his directorial debut with embun djajakusuma released a further eleven films with company before leaving in 1964 he then returned to traditional indonesian theatre including wayang although he continued to direct movies independently of perfini most of his energies were dedicated to promoting traditional art forms and teaching cinematography after over a decade of poor health and high blood pressure djajakusuma collapsed during a ceremony and died he was buried in karet bivak cemetery dedicated but easily angered djajakusuma was influenced by usmar ismail s realist views although he focused more on traditional aspects of life his theatrical performances attempted to modernize traditional forms so that they could be better received in a modern world he is credited with revitalising betawi theatre form lenong and received numerous awards for his filmmaking including a lifetime achievement award at indonesian film festival biography early life djajakusuma was born on 1 august 1918 in parakan temanggung central java dutch east indies to a priyayi father raden mas djojokoesomo and his wife djajakusuma was fifth child of six born to couple who lived comfortably off djojokoesomo s salary as a government official while young he enjoyed watching stage performances such as wayang puppetry and traditional dance form at times he would furtively leave his home after bedtime to watch productions with his friends he would act out bedtime stories his mother told him when imported hollywood films began to be screened he was an avid viewer watching westerns and works starring charlie chaplin owing to his position as son of a nobleman djajakusuma was able to receive an education he completed his studies in semarang central java graduating from natural sciences programme at a senior high school there in 1941 although his family hoped that he would become a government employee like his father djajakusuma decided to go into performing arts he returned to his hometown for a short time before realising that he would have little opportunity in parakan accordingly in early 1943 almost a year after indies were occupied by empire of japan djajakusuma moved to colony s political centre jakarta to find work djajakusuma became employed at cultural centre as a translator and actor under armijn pane among works he translated were several pieces by swedish playwright august strindberg and norwegian playwright henrik ibsen as well as a history of japan and several kabuki stage plays while with centre djajakusuma wrote several of his own stage plays in his free time djajakusuma helped establish amateur theatre company maya together with artists such as hb jassin rosihan anwar and usmar ismail troupe formed in response to a desire for greater artistic freedom performed translations of european works and original works by ismail and el hakim to promote a sense of indonesian nationalism while still conforming with japanese censorship bureau s rules several of maya s plays did not explicitly promote japan but rather greater east asia co prosperity sphere themes supporting indonesian nationalist movement meanwhile remained implicit in works with maya djajakusuma travelled from village to village putting on performances indonesian national revolution president sukarno proclaimed indonesia s independence on 17 august 1945 days after bombings of hiroshima and nagasaki expecting dutch colonial government to return djajakusuma and ismail helped establish independent artists seniman merdeka as a form of resistance group travelled throughout city spreading news of indonesia s proclaimed independence while performing from an open air truck after arrival of netherlands indies civil administration group sometimes attempted to spy on europeans or hide information which would be considered useful to returning dutch forces owing to this dangerous work djajakusuma began carrying a pistol and went to banten to ask a kyai to make him impervious to bullets in early 1946 with dutch colonial forces in control of jakarta djajakusuma fled to new national capital at yogyakarta there he spent a time with national news agency antara before joining military s educational division rising to rank of captain for military djajakusuma edited weekly tentara he also contributed articles to ismail s cultural magazine arena despite his involvement in press he did not abandon theatre with he established a troupe which performed for soldiers and raised morale sometimes travelling to frontlines djajakusuma was hired by ministry of information in 1947 to teach at a school for performance arts mataram entertainment foundation through mataram he and ismail were introduced to filmmakers andjar asmara and two studied under these more established individuals meanwhile djajakusuma was put in charge of censoring radio broadcasts in republican held areas a duty he held until dutch captured yogyakarta on 19 december 1948 djajakusuma fled city then met up with republican forces using an old radio and a bicycle powered generator djajakusuma listened to international news broadcasts and wrote them down information from these broadcasts was then printed in underground newspapers left thumb usmar ismail who drew djajakusuma to perfini in 1951 after indonesian national revolution ended with dutch recognition of indonesia s independence in 1949 considered inspired by italian neorealist movement djajakusuma remained a lecturer with academy until 1970 and his students considered him humorous and easy to approach upon his return to indonesia djajakusuma began work on tjambuk api whips of fire 1958 a critique of widespread corruption in indonesia this theme led to film being held by censorship bureau for almost a year director followed this with drama pak prawiro mr prawiro which was sponsored by post savings bank and meant to convey importance of having savings during this period he studied traditional theatre of india travelling to calcutta madras and new delhi he hoped that this first hand experience would inspire him in filming of traditional indonesian stories in 1960 djajakusuma released his first film based on traditional wayang stories lahirnja gatotkatja traditional puppetry had fascinated him as a child and he greatly enjoyed character shot in yogyakarta film featured a cast of stars from jakarta and local talent in backing roles it was however controversial and others versed in wayang argued that director had ignored too many traditional aspects of puppetry that year djajakusuma also served as production manager for ismail s pedjuang warriors for freedom and directed mak tjomblang mrs tjomblang a comedy adapted from nikolai gogol s 1842 drama marriage djajakusuma released another comedy masa topan dan badai time of cyclones and storms in 1963 film centres around family dynamics of a conservative father liberal mother and their two teenaged daughters who are in throes of adolescence following year djajakusuma directed his last film with perfini rimba bergema echoing jungles which was meant to promote nation s rubber industry that year he helped establish film and tv employee s union a response to lekra sponsored indonesian film league as with ismail and most perfini employees djajakusuma was staunchly against communist affiliated lekra cultural group was likewise hostile towards those affiliated with perfini later career towards end of his time with perfini djajakusuma again became active in traditional arts he devoted considerable time towards promotion of wayang in 1970s he organised two wayang weeks in 1970 and 1974 as well as a national wayang festival in 1977 furthermore he established two wayang orang troupes jaya budaya 1971 and bharata 1973 hoping to save arguing for proper remuneration for performers through 1970s lenong was performed at ismail marzuki hall drawing considerable audiences and several lenong performers found mainstream acclaim in film
