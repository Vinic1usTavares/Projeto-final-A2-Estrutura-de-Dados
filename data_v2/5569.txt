battle of marash was a battle that took place in early winter of 1920 between french forces occupying city of maras in ottoman empire and turkish national forces linked to mustafa kemal ataturk it was first major battle of turkish war of independence and three week long engagement in city ultimately forced french to abandon and retreat from marash and resulted in a turkish massacre of armenian refugees who had just been repatriated to city following armenian genocide controversial retreat along with massacre that took place under french watch was subsequently dubbed marash affair background after surrender of ottoman empire to allies in october 1918 city of marash had come under joint occupation of british and french armies latter largely composed of armenians from french armenian legion hovannisian payaslian 2008 p 497 in february 1919 field marshal edmund allenby appointed a number of french officers to oversee administration of region of cilicia and repatriation of tens of thousands of armenians who had been deported during war in course of genocide within a few months approximately 150 000 armenians had been repatriated including 20 000 natives from marash see vahram repatriation of armenian refugees from arab middle east 1918 1920 in armenian cilicia pp 419 56 in months following end of war cilicia had also become a source of dispute between british and french who both aspired to establish influence in region british government however was under strong domestic pressure to withdraw and demobilize its forces in middle east and on 15 september 1919 prime minister david lloyd george begrudgingly accepted a proposal by prime minister georges clemenceau to have french formally assume control of cilicia transfer of command took place on 4 november but
