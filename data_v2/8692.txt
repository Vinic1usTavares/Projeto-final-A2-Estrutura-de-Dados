james mccune smith april 18 1813 november 17 1865 was an african american physician apothecary abolitionist and author in new york city he was first african american to hold a medical degree and graduated at top in his class at university of glasgow in scotland after his return to united states he became first african american to run a pharmacy in that nation in addition to practicing as a doctor for nearly 20 years at colored orphan asylum in manhattan smith was a public intellectual he contributed articles to medical journals participated in learned societies and wrote numerous essays and articles drawing from his medical and statistical training he used his training in medicine and statistics to refute common misconceptions about race intelligence medicine and society in general invited as a founding member of new york statistics society in 1852 which promoted a new science he was elected as a member in 1854 of recently founded american geographic society but he was never admitted to american medical association or local medical associations he has been most well known for his leadership as an abolitionist a member of american anti slavery society with frederick douglass he helped start national council of colored people in 1853 first permanent national organization for blacks douglass called smith single most important influence on his life morgan 2006 p 612 smith was one of committee of thirteen who organized in 1850 in new york city to resist newly passed fugitive slave law by aiding refugee slaves through underground railroad other leading abolitionist activists were among his friends and colleagues from 1840s smith lectured on race and abolitionism and wrote numerous articles to refute racist ideas about black capacities both smith and his wife
