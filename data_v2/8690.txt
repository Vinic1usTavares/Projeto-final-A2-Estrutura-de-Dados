iris c cummings born december 21 1920 also known by her married name iris critchell is an american aviator and former competition swimmer who represented united states at 1936 summer olympics in berlin germany after an active athletic career in swimming which included a reign as u s national 200 meter breaststroke champion from 1936 to 1939 she was accepted into university of southern california s first civilian pilot training program in 1939 after graduation she worked as a flight instructor prior to being selected to serve her country during world war ii as a member of women airforce service pilots wasps following conflict she returned to california where she developed and taught a curriculum on civilian flight for veterans returning from war at university of southern california after racing airplanes competitively during 1950s cummings and her husband howard critchell helped found bates aeronautics program at harvey mudd college in 1962 they ran it together until he retired in 1979 at which point iris continued alone until program s end in 1990 a long time certified faa pilot examiner she is recipient of several international aviation awards and is a member of pacific coast championships cummings joined los angeles athletic club laac in 1934 and received her first financial support in 1935 helping her attend that year s far western championships she
