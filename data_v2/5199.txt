eusebia cosme almanza 5 march 1908 11 july 1976 known as eusebia cosme internationally was an afro cuban poetry reciter and actress who gained widespread fame in 1930s because of racial segregation cosme did not pursue an acting career in traditional cuban theater instead focusing on art of declamation or poetry reading she was sole cuban woman and one of few black women to participate in african themed declamation her performances went beyond reciting poems as she used gestures facial expression and vocal rhythm to convey emotion of written word focusing on works that served as social commentary on race gender and disparity of position of blacks in both latin america and united states cosme was recognized as a master of her craft beginning her career in variety shows she performed in cuba until late 1930s before embarking on international tours in 1938 cosme moved to united states she became a naturalized us citizen in 1940s she performed to sold out houses at venues including carnegie hall town hall and historically black universities she performed with both marian anderson and langston hughes and brought works of african american poets to hispanic audiences via eusebia cosme show which aired on cbs radio from 1943 to 1945 she performed recitations in united states through late 1950s worked as an abstract painter in 1960s and began acting in film and television in 1964 cosme lived in mexico city from 1966 to 1973 when she appeared in such films as pawnbroker and white roses for my black sister her most noted role was as mama dolores which she played repeatedly
