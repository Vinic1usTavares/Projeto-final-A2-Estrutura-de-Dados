history hachijo jima has been inhabited since at least jomon period and archaeologists have found and other remains under ritsuryo system of early nara period island was part of suruga province it was transferred to izu province when izu separated from suruga in 680 during heian period minamoto no was banished to izu oshima after a failed rebellion but per a semi legendary story escaped to hachijo jima where he attempted to establish an independent kingdom during edo period island became known as a place of exile for convicts most notably a daimyo who was defeated at battle of sekigahara originally island was a place of exile mainly for political figures but beginning in 1704 criteria for banishment were broadened crimes punishable by banishment included murder theft arson brawling gambling fraud jailbreak rape and membership of an outlawed religious group criminals exiled to island were never told length of their sentences and history of island is filled with foiled escape attempts its use as a prison island ended during meiji restoration after a general amnesty in 1868 most of island s residents chose to move to mainland however policy of banishment was not officially
