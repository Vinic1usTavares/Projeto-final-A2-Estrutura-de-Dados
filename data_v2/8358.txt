glanville davies affair was a scandal in english legal profession which resulted in greater reform of regulatory processes for solicitors and was one of justifications for courts and legal services act 1990 glanville davies was a well respected solicitor and a member of council of law society of england and wales who massively overcharged his client leslie persons sending him a bill for ps197 000 that was reduced on taxation to ps67 000 davies was not punished by law society s internal regulatory committees which allowed him to resign from council on grounds of ill health with his reputation intact following litigation and public criticism law society commissioned an internal report that found administrative failures wrong decisions mistakes errors of judgement failures in communication and insensitivity a private member s bill reformed way in which law society investigated disciplinary complaints although not to extent initially proposed and paved way for courts and legal services act 1990 that created an independent disciplinary body background in 1982 leslie parsons filed a complaint against glanville davies a respected solicitor and member of council of law society of england and wales solicitors professional body abel 1998 p 255 davies had charged parsons ps197 000 for legal services a grossly inflated and inaccurate legal bill white 1991 p 5 parsons complained to law society repeatedly from 1976 to 1982 despite this law society took no disciplinary action allowing davies to resign from council on grounds of ill health with his reputation intact parsons finally brought legal action against davies in 1982 and on 18 november anthony mccowan of high court of justice reduced bill to ps67 000 cohen 1986 p 51 mccowan also said that davies was guilty of at least gross and persistent misconduct and recommended that he be struck off on 24 october 1983 j struck davies from roll of solicitors investigation following start of litigation an investigation by lay observer and law society itself which became known as ely report highlighted an appalling catalogue of errors insensitivity and poor judgment in handling of davies affair by law society internal disciplinary organisation report found administrative failures wrong decisions mistakes errors of judgement failures in communication and insensitivity whole affair was a disgrace to society society paid compensation to parsons for their mishandling of situation and said that they would compensate victims of similar cases where they had failed to investigate complaints with reasonable care law society asked coopers lybrand to produce a report on law society which included its disciplinary topics among areas to be reported on draft report published in 1984 recommended that law society should transfer its disciplinary processes to an independent solicitor s complaints board which would be made up of both solicitors and lay people although with a majority of its members being solicitors in 1985 national consumer council published a survey showing that only 15 of respondents felt that law society should be investigating complaints and only 5 felt that if an independent body was created it should contain a solicitor majority with 36 wanting equal representation of solicitors and lay people and 55 preferring a majority of lay people aftermath as a result of this and similar controversies alf dubs introduced a private member s bill named solicitors independent complaints procedure bill on 12 february 1985 which would move responsibility of regulating solicitors profession to a body outside law society abel 1998 p 256 professional opinion was split on this national consumer council legal action group and many of smaller law societies supported proposal while larger law societies and law society of england and wales opposed it law society of england and wales persuaded coopers lybrand to include an alternate reform proposal in their report and after pressure from law society and several of larger regional societies alternate proposal was used this was far weaker than original proposal and kept responsibility of regulating solicitors profession within law society but increased separation of functions within society and required that majority of people on regulatory committees be lay people not solicitors events surrounding this scandal have been directly linked to reforms pushed through in courts and legal services act 1990 which created an independent disciplinary body for solicitors references bibliography category 1982 in
