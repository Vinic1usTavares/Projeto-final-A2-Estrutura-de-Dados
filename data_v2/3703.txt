cincinnati lebanon and northern railway cl n was a local passenger and freight carrying railroad in southwestern part of u s state of ohio connecting cincinnati to dayton via lebanon it was built in late 19th century to give town of lebanon and warren county better transportation facilities railroad was locally known as highland route since it followed ridge between little and great miami rivers and was only line not affected by floods such as great dayton flood of 1913 line was completed in 1881 and cl n was formed in 1885 company went through multiple bankruptcies until pennsylvania railroad gained control in 1896 cl n continued its own operations until 1921 and existed until 1926 when parent company merged cl n and other smaller companies except for several years in mid 1880s when line was under control of narrow gauge toledo cincinnati and st louis railroad it was not a major line in part due to its steep approach to downtown cincinnati for this reason portions of line have been abandoned beginning in 1952 with a segment north of lebanon passenger service was eliminated entirely in 1934 conrail pennsylvania railroad s successor sold remaining trackage in 1980s to indiana and ohio railway a short line now owned by railamerica that company continues to provide local freight service on ex cl n and lebanon mason monroe railroad operates tourist trains on a portion
