count roman ignacy potocki generally known as ignacy potocki 1750 1809 was a polish nobleman member of influential magnate potocki family owner of and near kurow a politician writer and office holder he was marshal of permanent council rada in 1778 1782 grand clerk of lithuania from 1773 court marshal of lithuania from 1783 grand marshal of lithuania from 16 april 1791 to 1794 he was an educational activist member of commission of national education and initiator and president of society for elementary textbooks he was an opponent of king stanislaw ii august in 1770s and 1780s and a major figure in polish politics of that era during great sejm he was a leader of patriotic party and reform movement and eventually backed king in many reform projects an advocate of a pro prussian orientation he helped conclude an alliance with prussia in 1790 he co authored constitution of 3 may 1791 life youth potocki was born in radzyn on 28 february 1750 into influential magnate potocki family he was son of eustachy potocki and marianna brother of jerzy michal potocki jan eryk potocki and stanislaw kostka potocki potocki was an alumnus of collegium nobilium in warsaw where he was a student in years 1761 1765 from 1765 he studied theology and law in rome where he attended collegium up to about 1769 his parents intended for him to join ranks of clergy but he refused to follow this path after traveling through italy and germany he returned to poland around 1771 on 27 december 1772 he married elzbieta lubomirska this marriage brought him close to political faction of familia early on potocki made a major impression on many of his contemporaries being groomed as next leader of familia from 1772 he was invited to king stanislaw ii augustus thursday dinners political career as a member 1772 1791 of poland s commission of national education world s first ministry of education he was initiator of and presided over society for elementary textbooks founded in 1775 he presided over renovation of s library in 1774 he was involved in development of numerous projects such as history curriculum in 1781 he reviewed and endorsed hugo kollataj s work at cracow academy his involvement with educational projects earned him a nickname holder of degree teacher his involvement with educational reforms lessened only during era of great sejm 1788 1792 when he became increasingly involved with wider reform program thumb left ignacy potocki by anna rajecka on 29 may 1773 he received office of great clerk writer of lithuania a relatively low ranked position that was seen by some as below magnates of potocki family he participated in partition sejm of 1773 where he sat on several commissions seeing himself in opposition to king he refused a seat on permanent council that he was offered in march 1774 king tried to appease him with order of saint stanislaus on 14 july that year but that failed to bring potocki to his side instead potocki became for next decade and half one of his chief political critics and opponents on 1776 he went to moscow to argue unsuccessfully for limiting power of king and russian ambassador otto magnus von stackelberg later that year his election to sejm was disputed and king and stackelberg managed to block his election in 1778 however growing rift between king and stackelberg allowed him to take through political maneuvering chairmanship of permanent council marshal of sejm that year he also became a knight of order of white eagle in 1779 potocki joined freemasonry and by 1780 he advanced to head of a freemasonry lodge he became de facto head of familia and of anti royal opposition succeeding its previous leader stanislaw lubomirski upon his death in 1783 that year also saw sudden death of his wife during a trip to italy and france in absentia influence of familia resulted in his appointment to office of court marshal of lithuania he continued to oppose various royal projects at sejms of 1784 and 1786 in 1785 he lost some face for his involvement in affair in which king was falsely accused of an instigation of a poisoning attempt disappointed with russia s lack of support for any serious reforms in poland he shifted to favoring an alliance with kingdom of prussia instead although this resulted in split of anti royalist opposition he was seen as leader of opposition patriotic party when great sejm begun in 1788 after some initial political issues of a closer relation with prussia that would eventually grew into polish prussian alliance and a major reform of government both with which he was closely involved begun accelerating in 1789 at first supportive more of a republican form of a government political reality such as royal faction victory at elections of 1790 resulted in his acceptance of a more constitutional monarchy approach in 1790 through mediation of scipione piattoli king and potocki begun drifting closer together working on a draft document that would eventually become 3 may 1791 constitution alongside poniatowski kollataj and piattoli he is seen as one of major authors of that document he supported quasi coup d etat in which constitution was passed on 3 may 1791 on 17 may 1791 he had to resign his position in commission of national education to take an appointment minister of police in newly created government guard of laws from march 1792 he also held position of minister of war during war in defence of constitution in 1792 he went on an unsuccessful diplomatic mission to berlin to request assistance from prussian government on 4 july 1792 a sudden depression made him resign his ministerial positions a vocal opponent of targowica confederation and likely an author of an anonymous anti targowica brochure he was specifically requested by russian government to not be involved in negotiations he also refused to join targowica confederation even after poniatowski s accession to it final years following victory of targowica confederation and abrogation of may 3rd constitution potocki emigrated from polish lithuanian commonwealth settling in leipzig together with tadeusz kosciuszko he proposed a plan for a french polish alliance of republics that was however not met with much support in france he co authored a work with hugo kollataj on adoption and fall of polish constitution of 3 may o i polskiej 3 go maja 1793 potocki participated in preparations for kosciuszko uprising of 1794 in early april he left leipzig and arrived in krakow he was involved in unsuccessful diplomatic negotiations with various foreign powers in a vain attempt to gain support for insurgents during uprising he served as a member of supreme national council as a chief of its diplomatic department upon suppression of uprising instead of emigrating again he took part in surrender negotiations which gained him respect in many quarters eventually on 21 december 1794 he was imprisoned by tsarist russian authorities he has lost most of his wealth following uprising as most of his estates were confiscated near end of his life he would be troubled by his inability to pay off debts from 1780s released in 1796 following death of catherine great potocki retired to kurow pulawy county central poland there he devoted himself to historical studies publishing several books translations and commentaries he also wrote poems but those were never published during his lifetime historians still debate over his potential authorship of several anonymous works primarily political brochures he distanced himself from activists discussing a new insurrection but was nonetheless arrested and imprisoned by austrian authorities again in years 1798 1800 in 1801 he joined warsaw scientific society he returned to politics shortly after much of galicia was liberated by napoleon and attached to duchy of warsaw during negotiations with napoleon in dresden he contracted severe diarrhea and died on 30 august 1809 he was buried in he had no direct descendants his only surviving daughter krystyna born 1778 died in 1800 his reduced estates were inherited by a nephew aleksander potocki remembrance in private life he is said to have had a weakness for gambling but he also had a reputation of an honest reformer who puts good of country above his own he is one of figures immortalized in jan matejko s 1891 painting constitution
