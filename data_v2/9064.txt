mountain grove campground was a campground active between 1872 and 1901 that formed part of 19th to early 20th century camp meeting movement where camping was combined with worshiping and listening to preachers these meetings were often held by protestants in this case methodists mountain grove campground was a site and resort on danville hazleton and wilkes barre railroad in black creek township luzerne county pennsylvania in united states it existed for approximately 30 years and was run by mountain grove camp meeting association a businessman named g m shoop was original owner of campground s land many prominent citizens of surrounding area were in association during campground s operation between 1872 and 1901 it was renovated and expanded several times while camp meetings that were held there were intended for methodists non methodists occasionally attended them location and description mountain grove campground was located approximately halfway between bloomsburg and hazleton it was adjacent to historical community of mountain grove which had a population of 65 in 1880 and was located on danville hazleton and wilkes barre railroad most attenders came by railroad came from stations in catawissa or hazleton campground had its own railroad station and post office campground s elevation was more than above sea level and it was situated in a wooded grove immediately east of mccauley mountain and in close proximity to scotch valley mccauley mountain protected site from strong west winds mountain grove campground was designed with a layout similar to a miniaturized version of washington d c it had a center avenue and five other avenues called a b c d and e center avenue ran from a large tabernacle to a living area for preachers there were also many tents and sites of cottages in campground and a large administration building on site housed a number of offices campground had outdoor fireplaces which encouraged picnics main hall which was constructed in 1887 was and had two stories according to a local atlas mountain grove campground was semicircular in shape with avenues radiating out towards sides an open space for worshiping was on camp meeting site and it was
