battle of san marino was an engagement on 17 20 september 1944 during italian campaign of second world war in which german army forces occupied neutral republic of san marino and were then attacked by allied forces it is also sometimes known as battle of monte san marino had declared its neutrality earlier in war and had remained broadly unaffected by events in europe until 1944 when allied forces had advanced a sizable distance up italian peninsula a major german defensive position gothic line ran across peninsula a short distance south of sammarinese border and in late june country was bombed by royal air force killing 35 people in belief that german army had taken up positions on its territory in operation olive launched in late august a strong allied force attacked at very eastern end of line aiming to pass through rimini just east of san marino and break out onto plains north of city whilst san marino was southwest of rimini plan was for it to be bypassed entirely in response to allied movements germans sent a small force into san marino to guard their lines of communication and act as artillery observers thumb right neutrality placards at borders of san marino after a few days main thrust of offensive was halted south of rimini by strong resistance and severe weather and british and indian flanking forces began to push westwards taking frontline towards san marino on 17 september 4th indian infantry division attacked forces of 278 infanterie division holding two hills just across sammarinese border after heavy fighting to gain control of hills situation stabilised on 19th and allied forces began to push into city of san marino itself city was captured by afternoon of 20 september and 4th indian division left country on 21st leaving it under control of local defence forces background of san marino in northern italian peninsula and fully surrounded by italy had played little role throughout second world war it had a fascist government closely aligned with
