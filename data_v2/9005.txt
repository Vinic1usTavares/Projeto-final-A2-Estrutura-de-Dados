august rodney mancuso december 5 1905 october 26 1984 nicknamed blackie was an american professional baseball player coach scout and radio sports commentator he played as a catcher in major league baseball with st louis cardinals 1928 1930 32 1941 42 new york giants 1933 38 1942 44 chicago cubs 1939 brooklyn dodgers 1940 and philadelphia phillies 1945 mancuso was known for his capable handling of pitching staffs and for his on field leadership abilities he was a member of five national league pennant winning teams and played as catcher for five pitchers who were eventually inducted into baseball hall of fame mancuso was regarded as one of top defensive catchers of 1930s baseball career thumb right mancuso with giants c 1933 mancuso was born in galveston texas to son of a sicilian immigrant and daughter of german immigrants his father died in his forties and his mother continued to support family by working as a midwife mancuso first began to play baseball as a nine year old after graduating from high school he went to work as a teller at a bank although he was hired more for his talent as a baseball player than as a teller playing as a member of bank s baseball team he eventually caught attention of president of houston buffaloes of texas league and began his professional baseball career with them in 1925 in 1927 he hit for a 372 batting average for syracuse stars in international league he made his major league debut with st louis cardinals at age of 22 on april 30 1928 and stayed with them until july when he was sent to minneapolis millers in american association he spent 1929 with st louis s american association farm club rochester red wings and with houston cardinals returned mancuso to major leagues in 1930 primarily because of a contract dispute with branch rickey that made baseball commissioner kenesaw mountain landis void mancuso s minor league contract he served as a reserve catcher to jimmie wilson who mancuso considered one of best catchers of era although they competed for same job veteran wilson provided mancuso with valuable help in learning intricacies of catching in major leagues mancuso became a student of game learning to study daily box scores to learn strengths and weaknesses of opposing teams on september 12 wilson sprained his ankle and would not play for rest of season mancuso rose to occasion ending season with a 366 batting average during a hitters year when league average was above 300 for first and only time cardinals won national league pennant but eventually lost to connie mack s philadelphia athletics in 1930 world series in 1931 mancuso was once again reserve catcher to wilson leading league with a 54 3 caught stealing percentage as cardinals won their second consecutive national league pennant 1931 world series was a rematch with athletics as cardinals were victorious in a seven game series mancuso only had one plate appearance during series as a late inning pinch hitter during 1932 season wilson turned 32 years old and shared catching duties with mancuso who caught 82 games to wilson s 75 and hit for a 284 average mancuso was traded to new york giants before 1933 season where new manager bill terry was rebuilding team after resignation of longtime manager john mcgraw terry wanted to build his team around speed defense and pitching he replaced slow footed shanty hogan and installed agile mancuso as giants starting catcher terry allowed mancuso to be in charge of giants pitching staff that included carl hubbell s sharp breaking screwball hal schumacher s diving sinker ball freddie fitzsimmons knuckleball and roy who threw a variety of different pitches under mancuso s guidance giants pitching staff led national league in earned run average as team won their first pennant in nine years although mancuso led national league catchers in errors and passed balls he also led in games played baserunners caught stealing putouts and finished second in assists and in range factor giants went on to defeat washington senators in 1933 world series terry credited mancuso as a major factor in moving giants from sixth place in 1932 to world series champions in 1933 in an associated press poll of sportswriters mancuso finished second to yankees bill dickey as majors league s all star catcher based on his ability to handle pitchers so skillfully mancuso ranked sixth in voting for 1933 national league most valuable player award in 1934 giants finished in second place two games behind eventual world champion st louis cardinals as mancuso had an off year with his batting average dropping almost twenty points along with 10 fewer runs batted in in 1935 mancuso improved to hit for a 298 batting average with five home runs and 56 runs batted in he also earned a spot as a reserve for national league team in 1935 all star game he had his best season in 1936 hitting for a 301 batting average with career highs of nine home runs and 63 runs batted in as giants once again claimed national league pennant he led league s catchers in putouts baserunners caught stealing finished second in assists and once again guided giants pitching staff to lowest earned run average in league giants would go down in defeat against powerful new york yankees in 1936 world series mancuso finished year ranked eighth in voting for 1936 national league most valuable player award in 1937 mancuso was hitting for a 283 average at mid season to earn his second berth as a reserve for national league team in 1937 all star game a few days after all star game a foul tip broke ring finger on mancuso s right hand harry danning replaced him and played well enough that when mancuso was healthy again two shared catching duties for rest of season giants clinched their second consecutive national league pennant and once again faced new york yankees in 1937 world series mancuso started first two world series games but when he went hitless danning took over as yankees went on to win series in five games danning took over as giants starting catcher for 1938 season with mancuso hitting a respectable 348 with a 411 on base percentage in 52 games giants traded mancuso to chicago cubs before 1939 season where he shared catching duties with 38 year old player manager gabby hartnett he ended season with a 231 batting average with only two home runs and 17 runs batted in with shortage of major league players due to second world war mancuso saw his career extended for another four years he played 1940 season with brooklyn dodgers before cardinals brought him back to st louis for 1941 season mancuso helped develop cardinals rookie catcher and future star walker cooper but when cooper was injured mancuso caught majority of cardinals games in 1941 he was credited with helping cardinals to a second place finish due to his work with rookie pitchers ernie white 17 7 and howie krist 10 0 but only managed to post a 229 batting average mancuso returned to giants in 1942 as a backup catcher and pitching coach when harry danning was drafted into united states army mancuso became club s starting catcher at age 37 sharing job with 35 year old future hall of fame member ernie lombardi for 1943 and 1944 seasons at end of 1944 season one month before his 39th birthday he was released by giants former teammate freddie fitzsimmons was managing philadelphia phillies in 1945 and convinced mancuso to play one more season as a catcher and full time pitching coach he caught 70 games while sharing catching duties with andy seminick but when fitzsimmons was fired mancuso left club before end of season playing his final game on september 11 at age of 40 career statistics in a seventeen year major league career mancuso played in 1 460 games accumulating 1 194 hits in 4 505 at bats for a 265 career batting average along with 53 home runs 543 runs batted in and an on base percentage of 328 he retired with a 977 fielding percentage a two time all star he led national league catchers three times in putouts and twice in baserunners caught stealing and in range factor his 50 69 career caught stealing percentage ranks 10th all time among major league catchers mancuso was a member of five pennant winning teams he caught for five pitchers who were inducted into baseball hall of fame carl hubbell grover cleveland alexander dizzy dean burleigh grimes and jesse haines in his book bill james historical baseball abstract baseball historian bill james ranked mancuso 74th all time among major league catchers coaching and broadcasting career in 1946 mancuso became player manager of minor league tulsa oilers and in 1948 he took over as manager of san antonio missions in 1950 he was hired as pitching coach for cincinnati reds mancuso began a career as a broadcaster in 1951 with his hometown houston team in texas league he later moved to st louis where he worked with play by play announcer harry caray on cardinals radio network until 1954 he then served as a scout for st louis cardinals and houston colt 45s his younger brother frank mancuso also was a major league catcher in mid 1940s in 1962 mancuso was seriously injured in a traffic accident which killed his wife lorena mancuso mancuso was inducted into texas sports hall of fame in 1981 and was elected to national italian american sports hall of fame in 1984 mancuso contracted emphysema
