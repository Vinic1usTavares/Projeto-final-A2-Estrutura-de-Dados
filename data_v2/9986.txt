burton pynsent house is a historic country house in parish of curry rivel somerset england it is a grade ii listed building house was built in stages between 1565 and 1765 when it was bequeathed to william pitt 1st earl of chatham by sir william pynsent 2nd baronet who did not want house to go to lord north pitt had an additional wing built to a design by lancelot brown and subsequent owner demolished everything but this wing in 1805 house was extended around this wing in 20th century overlooking of formal gardens and parkland to northwest of house is burton pynsent monument a column with an urn finial built to commemorate pynsent s generosity to pitt history house at burton pynsent was built section by section over period of many years construction was commenced by marmaduke jennings in approximately 1565 and was continued by jennings family throughout 17th century house passed to william pynsent who married mary star widowed daughter of thomas jennings pynsent bequeathed house to his political idol william pitt 1st earl of chatham despite never meeting him his intention was to disinherit lord north a relative of his wife who had supported a tax on somerset cider which pynsent disagreed with and which pitt had blocked pynsent died in 1765 and his relatives were unhappy at being left just 1 000 guineas each and so unsuccessfully contested will over following six years pitt immediately commissioned lancelot brown to design a monument in memory of pynsent s generosity built by philip pear at a cost of ps2 000 brown also advised on landscaping of parkland and possibly designed wing that pitt built on eastern end of house despite complaints from pynsent family pitt started landscaping gardens and other works in 1766 pitt sold part of estate to help repurchase his seat at hayes place kent but still resided at burton pynsent for periods until his death in 1778 his widow remained at estate until her death in 1803 estate was split up and sold in 1805 to pay for john pitt s gambling debts with house and park being purchased for ps8 810 new owner colonel pinney demolished majority of house keeping 18th century wing house passed through a number of hands during 19th century before being purchased by a relation of harold peto mrs crossley house remained in hands of crossley family for majority of 20th century grounds were laid out in mid 18th century by pitt and lancelot brown and they include early 20th century formal gardens designed by peto formal gardens are listed grade ii on register of historic parks and gardens of special historic interest in england house house is built on a flat terrace near south west of corner of estate two storey building is constructed of brick in a classical style dressed with ham stone features and a hipped tile roof garden frontage of house is symmetrical with a pediment at centre above a cupola it includes a door with some semi circular stone steps toward garden entrance facade is asymmetrical with a tall arcaded entrance projecting out from house on south west corner western facade of house was altered during 20th century to compensate for demolition of large parts of house in 1805 house is now largely built around wing that had been built for william pitt pitt s wing contained a library for himself and a bird room for his wife away from their children s rooms house was designated grade ii listed status on 1 june 1984 in 2001 house was put up for rent for ps36 000 described as having seven bedrooms four bathrooms and half a dozen reception rooms and offices ownership house is
