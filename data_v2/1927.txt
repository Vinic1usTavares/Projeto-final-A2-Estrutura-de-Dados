norman alexander dutton july 23 1897 march 15 1987 was a canadian ice hockey player coach and executive commonly known as red dutton and earlier by nickname mervyn he played for calgary tigers of western canada hockey league wchl and montreal maroons and new york americans of national hockey league nhl a rugged and physical defenceman dutton often led his team in penalty minutes won wchl championship in 1924 as a member of tigers and was twice named a wchl all star dutton coached and managed americans and later purchased team before suspending operations in 1942 due to world war ii he served as second president of nhl between 1943 and 1946 before resigning position after nhl s owners reneged on a promise to allow americans to resume operations following war he served as a stanley cup trustee for 37 years but otherwise limited his involvement with nhl following americans demise he was inducted into hockey hall of fame in 1958 a successful businessman dutton operated a construction company that built calgary s mcmahon stadium in 1960 and he served for a time as president of calgary stampeders football club and later calgary exhibition and stampede he was invested as a member of order of canada in 1981 and is honoured by both manitoba hockey and alberta sports halls of fame he was posthumously awarded lester patrick trophy in 1993 in recognition of his contributions to game of hockey in united states he was last surviving former player of calgary tigers early life dutton was born in russell manitoba on july 23 1897 his father bill was a successful contractor who helped build canada s transcontinental railway system and younger dutton often helped his father at his work when he was a boy he had at least two elder brothers and a sister his given name was norman alexander dutton but his birth names were quickly ignored a family friend of duttons refused to call him norman as name had a negative connotation for her so she instead called him mervyn a name that stuck his friends called him red after colour of his hair and to most he was known as mervyn red dutton dutton attended school at st john s college in winnipeg he left school in 1915 to volunteer with canadian expeditionary force cef in world war i and served with princess patricia s canadian light infantry he lied about his birth date on his cef enlistment papers as he was not yet 18 and served for four years dutton fought in battle of vimy ridge in 1917 where he suffered a shrapnel wound to his leg serious enough that doctors pressed for amputation dutton refused and spent following 18 months working to recover full use of his leg he played hockey almost constantly to regain strength at one point playing in seven different winnipeg leagues at same time following war dutton sought to build his own contracting business however an economic depression in 1920 forced its closure he then worked in a packing plant in winnipeg that also ceased operations penniless with only his pride preventing him from asking his father for help he was met by owner of a hockey team in calgary who had sought him out dutton was offered 2 500 to play in alberta city playing community spirit dutton played a major role in helping calgary and surrounding area shed its rural image in 25 years following world war ii personal life dutton and his wife had four children sons
