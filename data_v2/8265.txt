st cynfarwy s church is a medieval parish church in llechgynfarwy anglesey north wales first church in vicinity was established by st cynfarwy a 7th century saint about whom little is known in about 630 but no structure from that time survives present building contains a 12th century baptismal font indicating presence of a church at that time although extensive rebuilding in 1867 removed datable features of previous edifice it is a grade ii listed building a national designation given to buildings of special interest which warrant every effort being made to preserve them in particular because it is a simple rural church of medieval origins church is still used for worship by church in wales one of nine in a combined parish although there has not been an incumbent priest since september 2009 history and location st cynfarwy s church is set within a churchyard at side of road in centre of llechgynfarwy a hamlet in anglesey north wales settlement is in countryside about to north west of llangefni county town and about from port of holyhead according to 19th century anglesey historian angharad llwyd first church was established here by st cynfarwy in about 630 date of present structure is uncertain although one 19th century historian suggested that it might be from 15th century there was a church here before 15th century however since font is from 12th century and a church was recorded in this location in norwich taxation of 1254 on 5 november 1349 possessions of deceased clergyman who had been incumbent priest of llechgynfarwy were dealt with by an inquisition at beaumaris anglesey one of several dead clergymen whose goods were considered that day according to historian antony carr timing suggests that priests had been victims of black death and he notes that clergy as a class were hit particularly hard by it a chapel was added on south side of building in 1664 st cynfarwy s was largely reconstructed in 1867 under henry kennedy architect of diocese of bangor kennedy s work which rebuilt church almost from foundations in words of a 2009 guide to buildings of region left no datable features when llwyd described church in 1833 she called it a spacious and handsome cruciform edifice with transepts on north and south sides there is nothing on north side of present building apart from porch in north west corner added by kennedy in 1867 st cynfarwy s is still used for worship by church in wales and is one of nine churches in combined benefice of bodedern with llanfaethlu as of 2012 nine churches do not have an incumbent priest and position has been vacant since september 2009 church is within deanery of llifon and talybolion archdeaconry of bangor and diocese of bangor people associated with church include owen humphrey davies a 19th century composer conductor and quarry worker who became a clergyman in his late forties he was rector of church from 1895 until his death in 1898 architecture and fittings st cynfarwy s is built in early decorated style using rubble masonry dressed with freestone roof is made from slate and has a stone bellcote decorated with tudor roses at west end arched inner door of porch which was added by kennedy in 1867 with its ornate hinges leads into north west corner of nave inside 19th century roof with its exposed woodwork can be seen above nave and chancel which together measure 40 feet 3 inches by 16 ft 9 inches 12 3 by 5 1 m there is no structural division between them apart from two steps that lead up into chancel 17th century chapel is on south side of church and wooden trusses of its roof can also be seen there are external buttresses to support structure at north east corner and alongside porch north wall of church has a window of two lights sections of window separated by mullions perpendicular style window in south wall has three lights topped with trefoils a pattern of three overlapping circles set in a rectangular frame south wall of chapel has a two light window in an arch above it there is a stone with date 1664 and initials w b these are initials of william bold a member of family owning an estate and house about from church east window has two lights with three trefoils in decorative stone tracery at top set in an arched frame with a hoodmould on outer wall geometric patterns of stained glass dating from about 1860 decorate east window a survey in 1937 by royal commission on ancient and historical monuments in wales and monmouthshire also noted a brass plate by north nave window commemorating 18th century bequest of a catherine roberts to two old housekeepers of unblemished character 12th century stone font which is in diameter and high has a small circular bowl decorated with four panels carved with saltire crosses a fifth panel is plain it is set on a 19th century base at west end of church monuments include one in south chapel to helen bold died 1631 mother of william bold it has a large inscribed brass plate set in marble showing genealogical links between bold family and tudor dynasty there are two 18th century memorials on north wall of nave less elaborate than bold memorial a survey of church plate within bangor diocese in 1906 said that church s chalice dated 1632 was an interesting and very rare example from time of king charles i reigned 1625 1649 and had a mixture of characteristics of different periods it has a hexagonal stem engraved in medieval style with a base depicting crucifixion and a more modern engraved feather like decoration inscription and coat of arms record that it was donated by william bold it measures just under high and is about in diameter survey also noted a 19th century silver eight sided paten decorated with similar engravings to chalice assessment st cynfarwy s has national recognition and statutory protection from alteration as it has been designated as a grade ii listed building lowest of three grades of listing designating buildings of special interest which warrant every effort being made to preserve them it was given this status on 12 may 1970 and has been listed because it is regarded as a simple rural church of medieval origin cadw welsh government body
