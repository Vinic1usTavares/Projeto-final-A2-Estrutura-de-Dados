bristol historical and preservation society founded in 1936 bristol historical society is committed to saving collecting and promoting historical research and interest in southern new england with a specific focus on town of bristol originally bristol historical society was located in rogers free library but a fire destroyed a part of collections on july 27 1957 jail which had been abandoned by state in june of same year was selected by society as its new home and leased from state in 1972 bristol historical society was renamed to bristol historical and preservation society to reflect its preservation interests in 1973 society purchased bristol county jail from state society restored jail cells through a matching grant from national park service and opened cells as a public exhibit importance bristol county jail was added to national register of historic places in 1973 jail is significant as a relatively unaltered historic jail that was important to 18th century bristol county it is also significant in that it currently serves as home of bristol historical and preservation society society also houses its library and collections in jail and runs a small museum shop from
