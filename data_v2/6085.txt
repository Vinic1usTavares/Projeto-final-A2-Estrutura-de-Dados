crane and company old stone mill rag room is one of oldest surviving buildings built in 1844 of crane co one of oldest papermaking businesses in berkshire county massachusetts it is located in southwestern dalton on a site where paper has been manufactured since early 19th century building originally used for processing rags has housed crane museum of papermaking since 1930 and was designated a national historic landmark in 1983 zenas crane began making paper in dalton in 1801 taking full ownership of an established operation at rag room site in 1822 in mid 1840s his sons constructed old stone mill of which rag room is only surviving portion rag room is where crane s grandson winthrop murray crane learned business through his efforts crane secured a monopoly contract to provide paper for nation s currency which it still holds today rag room is a gray fieldstone building with a slate roof its interior is a large open space that houses exhibits and artifacts of company s history it is open to public at no charge history zenas crane 1777 1845 was educated in processes of paper manufacturing in a mill owned by his brother stephen in newton lower falls massachusetts after a stint at another paper mill in worcester crane traveled west into berkshire county in 1799 looking for potential papermaking sites toomey and quinn p 610 he found a highly desirable site in dalton but owner a local farmer was unwilling to part with it crane instead established his business on a nearby parcel in 1801 valente p 121 other papermakers followed over next ten years notably henry and david carson who acquired more desirable site and established what became known as red mill through a series of partnership and purchase transactions crane eventually acquired control of red mill taking full ownership in 1822 valente p 122 thumb left upright zenas crane founder of crane mill complex in dalton in 1842 zenas crane retired leaving control of red mill to his sons zenas marshall crane and james brewer crane they founded crane co and set about expanding business beginning construction of what became known as stone mill valente p 123 rag room was part of this construction effort and was built in 1844 in 1869 stone mill was damaged by fire while it was being repaired in 1870 red mill was destroyed by fire stone mill was then expanded and also became known as pioneer mill valente p 129 stone mill was used by firm until 1890s when most of it was demolished leaving only rag room in 1930 company adapted rag room for use as company museum a role it has filled since following plans developed by architect charles s keefe company reinforced building s foundation with steel and concrete filling in a number of its windows with stonework interior was significantly reworked with new structural woodwork and flooring upon completion interior was said to resemble that of old ship church in hingham massachusetts in 1967 company retained olmsted brothers firm to design landscaping of area although their work has since been compromised to some extent by later development on property site was declared a national historic landmark and added to national register of historic places in 1983 in 2005 it became part of listed historic district which includes among other properties 1816 home of zenas crane rag room was where zenas marshall crane s son winthrop murray crane 1853 1920 began his career in papermaking business in 1878 he was responsible for acquiring crane co s exclusive government contract to manufacture paper used in currency of
