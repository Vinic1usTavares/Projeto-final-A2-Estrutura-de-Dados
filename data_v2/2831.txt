london road fire station is a former fire station in manchester england it was opened in 1906 on a site bounded by london road whitworth street minshull street south and fairfield street designed in edwardian baroque style by woodhouse willoughby and langham in red brick and terracotta it cost ps142 000 to build and was built by j gerrard and sons of swinton it has been a grade ii listed building since 1974 in addition to a fire station building housed a police station an ambulance station a bank a coroner s court and a gas meter testing station fire station operated for eighty years housing firemen their families and horse drawn appliances that were replaced by motorised vehicles a few years after its opening it was visited by royalty in 1942 in recognition of brigade s wartime efforts after war it became a training centre and in 1952 became first centre equipped to record emergency calls however fire station became expensive to maintain and after council reorganisation decline set in building was headquarters of manchester fire brigade until brigade was replaced by greater manchester fire service in 1974 fire station closed in 1986 since when it has been largely unused despite
