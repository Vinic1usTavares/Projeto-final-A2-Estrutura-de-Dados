matthew paul deady may 12 1824 march 24 1893 was a politician and jurist in oregon territory and state of oregon of united states he served on oregon supreme court from 1853 to 1859 at which time he was appointed to newly created federal court of state he served as a united states district judge of united states district court for district of oregon in portland as sole judge until his death in 1893 while on court he presided over trial that led to united states supreme court decision of pennoyer v neff concerning personal jurisdiction prior to joining court deady served in legislature of oregon territory including time served as president of council and was elected as president of oregon constitutional convention in 1857 a native of state of maryland his first profession was as a blacksmith he would also spend time as a teacher in both ohio and oregon deady read law in ohio and practiced law for a time in that state before immigrating to oregon territory via oregon trail in oregon he helped codify laws of state and assisted in foundation of multnomah county library in portland he also was president of university of oregon s board of regents university renamed deady hall
