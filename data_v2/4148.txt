linguistic society of america lsa is a learned society for field of linguistics founded at end of 1924 in new york city lsa works to promote scientific study of language society publishes two scholarly journals language and open access journal semantics and pragmatics its annual meetings held every winter foster discussion amongst its members through presentation of peer reviewed research as well as conducting official business of society since 1928 lsa has offered training to linguists through courses held at its biennial linguistic institutes held in summer lsa and its 3 600 members work to raise awareness of linguistic issues with public and contribute to policy debates on issues including bilingual education and preservation of endangered languages history linguistic society of america lsa was founded on 28 december 1924 when about 75 linguists met to select officers ratify a constitution and present papers in order to facilitate communication within field of linguistics foundational members included 31 women most of whom worked as educators rather than as scholars at research institutions by 1935 half of female foundational members had left society a rate similar to that of male members largely due to professionalization of field of linguistics that disproportionately affected women as most worked outside of academia before foundation of lsa a number of other similar societies existed including american philological society and modern language association and with publication of sapir s language and saussure s course in general linguistics in 1921 and 1922 field of linguistics began to take shape as an independent discipline though an international discipline founders of lsa had a growing feeling of an american linguistics different from traditional european topics and methodologies popular at time one of founding members leonard bloomfield explained need for and establishment of society so that science of language similar to but separate from other sciences could build a professional consciousness from start lsa focused on establishing science of linguistics separate from other fields such as philology and anthropology founders were characterized as scientific revolutionaries as early scholarship of society s members contributed to development of descriptive linguistics through their rejection of previous linguistic scholarship and methods in favor of new ones though when views of female members of society are taken into account society was less revolutionary and more diverse in their scholarship lsa published first edition of its flagship journal language in march 1925 that same year society elected its first president hermann collitz in 1927 three years after organization s founding lsa was admitted into american council of learned societies following year one of founding members of lsa edgar sturtevant organized first of linguistic institutes which lsa still holds biennially as lsa grew it began to take on a larger role outside of professional sphere during world war ii lsa helped united states government with language training programs through its linguistic institutes after longtime secretary treasurer archibald hill retired from his position in 1969 lsa made large changes to its organizational structure to better accommodate its new and growing role responsibilities of secretary treasurer were expanded and lsa established a secretariat in washington d c in order to act as a liaison between members federal government and other professional organizations in 1981 lsa and 9 other professional accomplishes this goal through advocacy efforts society recognizing its growing role in advocacy established a secretariat in washington d c in 1969 to better liaise between its membership and government around that same time lsa began working with other professional organizations to meet and exchange research as part of consortium of social science associations cossa during reagan administration after cuts to social science funding in 1981 lsa and 9 other professional organizations founded cossa as an advocacy effort for funding of social science research advocacy efforts are not only limited to science funding but larger issues of public policy as well over years lsa membership have passed a number of resolutions regarding issues of public policy in 1987 lsa officially took a stand against english only movement in united states stating that english only measures are based on misconceptions about role of a common language in establishing political unity and are inconsistent with basic american traditions of linguistic tolerance furthering that stance membership ratified a statement on linguistic rights in 1996 declaring government and people of united states have a special obligation to enable indigenous peoples to retain their languages and cultures and declared 7 fundamental linguistic rights including right to have their children educated in a manner that affirmatively acknowledges their native language abilities which includes possibility of education in a language other than english 5 years later lsa lent its support for recognition of sign languages as equal to that of other languages resolution passed in 2001 affirm ed that sign languages used by deaf communities are full fledged languages with all structural characteristics and range of expression of spoken languages and urged that sign languages be given same respect as other languages in academic and political life society has also engaged in more targeted advocacy efforts in 1997 an lsa resolution supported oakland school board in its attempt to favor teaching that is sensitive to distinctive characteristics of african american vernacular english so called ebonics debate more recently lsa has advocated for passage of bills funding revitalization programs for native american languages their efforts are not limited strictly limited to language however citing an interest in promoting diversity particularly linguistic diversity lsa along with other professional societies signed an amicus curiae brief in supreme court case of fisher v university of texas stating importance of affirmative action policies and urging for their retention awards lsa presents a series of awards during its annual meeting list of awards their descriptions and their current holders
