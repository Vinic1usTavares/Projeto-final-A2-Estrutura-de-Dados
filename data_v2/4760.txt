paul stephenson obe born 6 may 1937 is a community worker activist and long time campaigner for civil rights for british african caribbean community in bristol england as a young social worker in 1963 stephenson led a boycott of bristol omnibus company protesting against its refusal to employ black or asian drivers or conductors after a 60 day boycott supported by thousands of bristolians company revoked its colour bar in august in 1964 stephenson achieved national fame when he refused to leave a public house until he was served resulting in a trial on a charge of failing to leave a licensed premises his campaigns were instrumental in paving way for first race relations act in 1965 stephenson is a freeman of city of bristol and was awarded an obe in 2009 early life stephenson was born in
