toluid civil war was a war of succession fought between kublai khan and his younger brother ariq boke from 1260 to 1264 mongke khan died in 1259 with no declared successor precipitating infighting between members of tolui family line for title of great khan that escalated to a civil war toluid civil war and wars that followed it such as berke hulagu war and kaidu kublai war weakened authority of great khan over mongol empire and split empire into autonomous khanates background tolui family successfully enthroned their candidate for great khan mongke in of 1250 and 1251 ogedeid candidate for great khan and his cousin were embittered by their loss and plotted a failed assassination of mongke mongke took revenge by purging his opponents in royal house and members of both chagatai and ogedei families mongke handed control over caucasus region to golden horde in 1252 with approval of mongke berke succeeded his brother batu as khan of golden horde in russia in 1255 hulagu of ilkhanate seized control of caucasus from golden horde and his sacking of baghdad in 1258 angered berke a convert to islam mongke khan died in 1259 without appointing a successor he likely favored ariq boke whom mongke designated in 1258 as commander of karakorum then capital of empire but he did little else to secure ariq boke s claim to throne civil war kublai khan was campaigning against southern song in 1260 when he received news that ariq boke was challenging him for succession of throne ariq boke formed alliances with powerful members of mongol nobility to endorse him as a candidate for great khan most of mongke s immediate family supported ariq boke including member of ogedei chagatai and jochi kublai withdrew from song and mobilized his troops to fight ariq boke in china kublai summoned a kurultai at kaiping where he was elected great khan this was first kurultai to proclaim a great khan outside mongol homeland or central asia ariq boke convened his own kurultai in karakorum that proclaimed him great khan a month later creating two rival claimants for throne hulagu embarked for mongolia to attend kurultai but mamluk defeat of mongols at battle of ain jalut forced him to retreat back to middle east berke capitalized on mamluk victory by invading ilkhanate beginning berke hulagu war ariq boke allied with berke khan of golden horde and alghu of chagatai khanate hulagu of ilkhanate was sole ally of kublai khan berke supported ariq boke because he was resentful of hulagu who had close ties with kublai hulagu and berke however became occupied with their own war and could not intervene in toluid civil war kublai had access to supplies from fertile lands of china while ariq boke had to import resources to karakorum in semi arid steppes kublai khan depended on these supplies from china and therefore needed chinese popular support to win civil war kublai ingratiated himself to his subjects with help of his chinese advisers he presented himself as a sage emperor capable of uniting chinese korean and his fellow mongol without ease while calling out ariq boke as a destructive usurper kublai promised to reduce taxes modeled his government institutions to resemble those of chinese dynasties and adopted era name of which means moderate rule his policies were popular in northern china but had no impact on his relations with southern song song invaded while kublai was preoccupied with civil war and recovered territory previously lost to mongols kublai dispatched a diplomat hao jing to discuss prospects of a peaceful resolution to war with southern song song however rejected kublai s overtures and imprisoned hao for next decade kublai now controlled three of four possible supply lines to karakorum kadan kublai s ogedeid ally defended territories of former western xia from ariq boke and commanded forces stationed in gansu kublai s troops guarded area surrounding yan modern beijing only supply line still open to ariq boke was yenisei river valley in northwest when kublai s army advanced towards karakorum in late 1260 ariq boke retreated from karakorum to a tributary of yenisei oncoming winter then compelled kublai and ariq boke to encamp their armies and wait for spring thumb left 200px ariq boke s victory against alghu in interim kublai acquired more supplies and men he fortified yan and border defenses of northern china kadan defeated and executed a general sent to secure vital central asian trade routes for ariq boke lian xixian one of kublai s confucian advisers commanded soldiers for khan in western china he won a victory against ariq boke s ally liu taiping in northwestern china seizing food supplies intended for ariq boke s army lian also drove supporters of ariq boke out of towns of and ganzhou in southwestern china his forces protected sichuan from ariq boke s encroaching troops kublai paid kadan and lian xixian handsomely for their military service in gifts and promotions he rewarded kadan with 300 packs of silk and 300 taels of silver and appointed lian xixian to position of prime minister of right in secretariat kublai s victories left alghu as ariq boke s only standing ally ariq boke convinced alghu to take control of chagatai khanate in central asia chagatai s khan qara hulegu had recently died alghu fought and killed a rival claimant to throne endorsed by kublai as ruler of khanate alghu was one of ariq boke s strongest supporters and he appointed him khan of chagatai khanate became an important source of ariq boke s provisions ariq boke gave alghu complete control over tax revenues in region in 1261 kublai and ariq boke engaged in battle at ariq boke lost battle and retreated he returned to region ten days later to challenge kublai s forces near khingan mountains of eastern mongolia troops that ariq boke attacked were not personally led by kublai and made up only a small portion of kublai s army even so battle ended in a stalemate meanwhile most of mongolia was now under control of kublai threatening ariq boke s control of yenisei valley supply line a weakened ariq boke entreated alghu for help alghu refused and executed envoys sent by ariq boke who had demanded a share of alghu s tax revenues at this time a rebellion in china distracted kublai from civil war and he departed for kaiping instead of further pursuing ariq boke ariq boke went to war with alghu after threat of an attack by kublai receded alghu defeated ariq boke s commander khara near ili river in xinjiang but lost his headquarters at to ariq boke he withdrew to oasis cities of tarim basin ariq boke was now left with few allies and several of his supporters deserted from his side tash son of mongke defected taking his father s tamga seal from ariq boke and giving it to kublai as a symbol of his loyalty alghu then returned to ili river to remove ariq boke from xinjiang ariq boke lacked resources or allies to defend himself he travel to shangdu alone and surrendered to kublai in 1264 ending civil war aftermath kublai imprisoned ariq boke but did not immediately punish him kublai s supporters wanted retribution so kublai ignored ariq boke for a year as punishment he conducted a purge to eliminate officials in mongol government who sympathized with ariq boke kublai accused bolghai an important mongol official who served under mongke of treachery for conspiring with ariq boke kublai authorized execution of bolghai and other ariq boke supporters kublai summoned a kurultai to decide a punishment for ariq boke and solidify his own claim to throne kublai was reluctant to punish his brother without public support of mongol nobility ariq boke died mysteriously in 1266 while still imprisoned leading to speculation that kublai had secretly poisoned him ariq boke s defeat by kublai could not stop fracturing of empire when kublai convened his kurultai to confirm his status as great khan none of three other khans attended berke and hulagu continued fighting until hulagu died in 1265 ogedei line sought to exploit disunity to advance interests of their own family they held a grudge against tolui family for kurultai of 1251 and subsequent purge of ogedeids after assassination plot kaidu from ogedei family believed that a member of ogedeids deserved title of great khan and started an insurrection in 1269 against kublai that lasted for decades most of western khanates did not recognize kublai as great khan although some of them still asked kublai to confirm enthronement of their new regional khans four khanates were functionally independent sovereign states ilkhanate based in persia and yuan dynasty based in china had close diplomatic relations and shared scientific and cultural knowledge but military cooperation between all four mongol
