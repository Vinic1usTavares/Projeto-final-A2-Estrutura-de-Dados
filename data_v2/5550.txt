topics from journalism to autobiography to political philosophy and history he was a member of a group of intellectuals known as generation of 1837 who had a great influence on nineteenth century argentina he was particularly concerned with educational issues and was also an important influence on region s literature sarmiento grew up in a poor but politically active family that paved way for much of his future accomplishments between 1843 and 1850 he was frequently in exile and wrote in both chile and in argentina his greatest literary achievement was facundo a critique of juan manuel de rosas that sarmiento wrote while working for newspaper el progreso during his exile in chile book brought him far more than just literary recognition he expended his efforts and energy on war against dictatorships specifically that of rosas and contrasted enlightened europe a world where in his eyes democracy social services and intelligent thought were valued with barbarism of gaucho and especially caudillo ruthless strongmen of nineteenth century argentina while president of argentina from 1868 to 1874 sarmiento championed intelligent thought including education for children and women and democracy for latin america he also took advantage of opportunity to modernize and develop train systems a postal system and a comprehensive education system he spent many years in ministerial roles on federal and state levels where he travelled abroad and examined other education systems sarmiento died in asuncion paraguay at age of 77 from a heart attack he was buried in sarmiento was well known for his modernization of country and for his improvements to educational system he firmly believed in democracy and european liberalism but was most often seen as a romantic sarmiento was well versed in western philosophy including works of karl marx and john stuart mill he was particularly fascinated with liberty given to those living in united states which he witnessed as a representative of peruvian government he did however see pitfalls to liberty pointing for example to aftermath of french revolution which he compared to argentina s own may revolution he believed that liberty could turn into anarchy and thus civil war which is what happened in france and in argentina therefore his use of term liberty was more in reference to a laissez faire approach to economy and religious liberty though a catholic himself he began to adopt ideas of separation of church and state modeled after us he believed that there should be more religious freedom and less religious affiliation in schools this was one of many ways in which sarmiento tried to connect south america to north america sarmiento believed that material and social needs of people had to be satisfied but not at cost of order and decorum he put great importance on law and citizen participation these ideas he most equated to rome and to united states a society which he viewed as exhibiting similar qualities in order to civilize argentine society and make it equal to that of rome or united states sarmiento believed in eliminating caudillos or larger landholdings and establishing multiple
