hugo von pohl 25 august 1855 23 february 1916 was a german admiral who served during first world war he joined navy in 1872 and served in various capacities including with new torpedo boats in 1880s and in reichsmarineamt imperial navy office in 1890s he eventually reached rank of vizeadmiral and held position of chief of admiralty staff in 1913 he commanded german high seas fleet from february 1915 until january 1916 as commander of surface fleet he was exceedingly cautious and did not engage high seas fleet in any actions with british grand fleet pohl was an outspoken advocate of unrestricted submarine warfare and he put policy into effect once he took command of fleet on 4 february 1915 seriously ill from liver cancer by january 1916 pohl was replaced by reinhard scheer that month pohl died a month later biography early career hugo von pohl was born in breslau prussian silesia on 25 august 1855 he entered kaiserliche marine imperial navy as a cadet in april 1872 tucker p 924 at age of 24 pohl was promoted and given command of sailing corvette information quarterly p 149 in 1880s he served with then korvettenkapitan alfred von tirpitz in his so called torpedo gang which advocated a greater emphasis on torpedo boats in german fleet pohl took command of spar torpedo vessel an early experimental torpedo boat in 1882 kelly p 54 two years later in late september 1884 he was involved in an experiment with new torpedo boat designs from schichau thornycroft ag vulcan and ag weser pohl commanded one of schichau boats during exercises pohl s boat collided with boat commanded by august von heeringen former s boat sprung a leak while latter s rudder was damaged but both safely returned to port kelly pp 58 59 by 1890s pohl had been transferred to reichsmarineamt imperial navy office where he would again work with tirpitz after latter was appointed as state secretary of navy in 1900 pohl was assigned to fleet that was sent to china to help suppress boxer rebellion while in china he commanded protected cruiser which bombarded taku forts in may of that year pohl was promoted to korvettenkapitan he was promoted to rank of konteradmiral in 1906 thereafter he served as commander of reconnaissance forces of german fleet philbin p 16 pohl was promoted again to vizeadmiral in january 1913 that year he was elevated to nobility and in april became chief of admiralty staff a position he would hold for two years mombauer p 323 as chief of admiralty staff pohl was involved in german deliberations during july crisis in aftermath of assassination of archduke franz ferdinand by serbian terrorists previous month pohl helmuth von moltke chief of german general staff and theobald von bethmann hollweg chancellor met kaiser after monarch returned from a cruise to norway with bulk of high seas fleet pohl and others were present at several meetings with kaiser mombauer p 198 which ultimately produced blank check wilhelm ii extended to austria hungary this decision ultimately helped to push europe into first world war by end of month fischer p 54 first world war thumb dreadnoughts of high seas fleet steaming in line left at outbreak of war pohl along with georg von muller chief of imperial naval cabinet and friedrich von ingenohl commander of high seas fleet believed that since war would be over quickly fleet should be preserved intact together they agreed that fleet should be restrained to provide local defense of german coast rather than seek a decisive battle against numerically superior british grand fleet philbin p 50 pohl argued that u boats should be used to attack british merchant shipping philbin p 95 he pushed for fewer restrictions on conduct of commerce war beginning in late 1914 he further advocated abandoning cruiser rules that handicapped german effort in favor of unrestricted submarine warfare butler p 125 he presented his first plans for an unlimited commerce war in november 1914 but these were rejected by kaiser and bethmann hollweg so as not to antagonize neutral nations in particular united states butler p 126 after battle of dogger bank pohl replaced ingenohl as commander of high seas fleet throughout his tenure as its commander pohl adopted a very cautious strategy in order to preserve strength of fleet pohl was a controversial choice to replace ingenohl in large part owing to his caution with employment of surface fleet two days after taking command on 4 february 1915 pohl gave order to use unrestricted submarine warfare against allies philbin p 117 he had managed to convince bethmann hollweg by falsely promising him that u boat commanders could distinguish between enemy and neutral steamers and thus avoid provoking united states butler p 127 policy was short lived however as on 7 may 1915 u boat torpedoed and sank passenger liner causing a diplomatic crisis with united states to avoid drawing america into war germany reinstated restrictions on u boat fleet butler p 128 aboard his flagship pohl conducted a series of short operations into north sea over course of 1915 none of these operations ventured outside of southern end of north sea and fleet never encountered any british forces staff p 15 pohl became seriously ill from liver cancer on 8 january 1916 and was taken to a hospital ship he was subsequently moved to berlin for surgery he was relieved of command due to his poor health on 23 january his replacement as fleet commander was reinhard scheer pohl died a month later on 23 february shortly before his death he was awarded order of red eagle with oak leaves and swords for his command of fleet in 1920 pohl s widow ella published some of his papers in an effort to defend his reputation from postwar criticisms kelly p 429 citations
