church of st peter tsarkva sveti petar or church of saints peter and paul is a small medieval bulgarian orthodox church located in village of berende in dragoman municipality sofia province in westernmost bulgaria most likely constructed and decorated in 14th century church of st peter features simple stone architecture but contains a number of remarkable interior frescoes though located next to village s graveyard and often interpreted as a cemetery church church of st peter may have been in some way related to bulgarian royal court as a now lost portrait with a caption most likely referencing bulgarian tsar ivan alexander was once visible on one of its exterior walls history original researchers of church of st peter karel skorpil and ivanov ascribed it to 13th century owing to a now lost exterior mural of a person on west wall of church described by one source as an image of a bulgarian tsar image bears following caption in middle bulgarian ivan asen in christ our god faithful tsar and autocrat of all and greeks skorpil and ivanov identified person mentioned in inscription as tsar ivan asen ii of bulgaria r 1218 1241 beginning in 1920s and publications of andre grabar however church and its art have been usually dated to 14th century based on stylistic and architectural similarities to other works of that period formula of exterior inscription is also considered to be similar to inscriptions of tsar ivan alexander r 1331 1371 who is known to have used name ivan asen on several other occasions due to its location church of st peter is often described as a cemetery church however scholar bistra nikolova considers it improbable that an ordinary village cemetery church would have an inscription by a bulgarian tsar in her opinion it is more likely that church was constructed by a noble person related to court of second bulgarian empire church of st peter has been protected as a national antiquity since 1927 in 1966 it was listed as an artistic monument of culture of national importance however as of 2011 frescoes have never during church s existence undergone restoration or conservation and remain threatened in lowest parts up to
