west hendford cricket ground was a first class cricket ground in yeovil somerset land for ground was first leased by yeovil cricket club in 1874 and was also used for a range of other sports most significantly hosting yeovil rugby club in 1890s and then again from 1935 until ground was closed significant improvements were made to ground during 1930s including opening of a new pavilion jointly funded by rugby and cricket clubs ground was demolished in 1944 when westland aircraft extended their factory and both yeovil cricket club and rugby club moved to johnson park between 1935 and 1939 ground hosted five annual somerset county cricket club matches in july or august first of which nearly broke a county record for ticket sales on gate somerset won only one of five matches 1936 contest against worcestershire history yeovil and county cricket club was formed in 1865 and was first attempt at setting up a county cricket team for somerset attempt was unsuccessful and club broke up in 1874 club was re formed with lesser remit as yeovil cricket club as part of club s resuscitation committee purchased use of a field in west hendford in yeovil from a local farmer mr brook field part of key farm was leased for ps10 there is record of a match being played on ground following year between two sets of members of yeovil cricket club during late 19th century ground was used for other sports as well as cricket it had a grass athletics track and also hosted yeovil football club who at time played both association and rugby football football club played at west hendford on an irregular basis during late 19th century but returned in 1935 by which time they only played rugby and had changed their name accordingly to yeovil rugby club in 1895 cricket club committee announced that there was provision for a longer lease of five or seven years and that they would make improvements to ground to enable it to host first class cricket ground was also used for field hockey in early 20th century hosting a yeovil hockey club in 1932 ground hosted greyhound racing after it switched from barwick fields racing was organised by salisbury greyhound race club and racing was independent unaffiliated to a governing body somerset county cricket club played their first of five annual first class matches on ground in 1935 match against surrey was a significant event in town and a series of festivities were arranged to run alongside three day contest including a dance and a smoking concert entry for match which took place from 17 to 19 august was one shilling and attracted over 5 000 people raising around ps400 surrey won match by eight wickets takings from this match helped yeovil cricket club make further improvements to ground expanding it and adding further seating following year somerset played worcestershire at ground in what western gazette described as yeovil cricket festival captain of yeovil cricket club richard was included in somerset team which won match by 170 runs takings were slightly lower than previous year due to poor weather but still described as gratifying in 1937 sussex beat somerset at ground in a match that once again drew a crowd of around 5 000 yorkshire evening post described wicket as crumbling towards end of match favouring bowlers in 1938 hampshire visited and report in western daily press lamented state of wicket which meant that game like three first class matches at ground before it was completed in two days rather than scheduled three that winter a new pavilion costing ps550 was erected on ground for shared use of cricket club and rugby club final first class match on ground was played in july 1939 against lancashire but torrential rain limited match to only three hours of play takings for full three days of match were only ps87 and taunton courier estimated that losses for match could be hundreds of pounds despite weather almost 2 000 people attended match and taunton courier report praised alterations that had been made to ground removal of a hedge made ground lighter while ground itself had been well looked after and drained quickly second world war suspended county championship from 1940 to 1945 and during that time westland aircraft took over ground to expand their factory and informed yeovil cricket club that it was no longer available forcing them to search for a new ground in 1946 they eventually relocated to newly opened johnson park in 1948 rugby club also moved to johnson park amalgamating itself into yeovil sports club after a short break somerset county cricket club returned to yeovil playing fourteen fixtures at johnson park between 1951 and 1970 and eight matches at westlands sports ground from 1971 to 1978 records during its limited use as a first class cricket ground only one century was scored on ground by jim parks during 1937 match he scored 140 runs for sussex most wickets taken by a bowler in a match at west hendford was achieved in 1938 when
