battle of tulagi and gavutu tanambogo was a land battle of pacific campaign of world war ii between forces of imperial japanese navy and allied mainly united states u s marine ground forces it took place from 7 9 august 1942 on solomon islands during initial allied landings in guadalcanal campaign u s marines under overall command of u s major general alexander vandegrift captured islands of tulagi gavutu and tanambogo among which japanese navy had constructed a naval and seaplane base landings were fiercely resisted by japanese navy troops who outnumbered and outgunned by allied forces fought and died almost to last man while landings on tulagi and gavutu tanambogo were taking place allied troops were also landing on nearby guadalcanal with objective of capturing an airfield under construction by japanese forces in contrast to intense fighting on tulagi and gavutu landings on guadalcanal were essentially unopposed landings on both tulagi and guadalcanal initiated six month long
