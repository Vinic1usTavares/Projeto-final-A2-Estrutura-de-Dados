battle of gonzales was first military engagement of texas revolution it was fought near gonzales texas on october 2 1835 between rebellious texian settlers and a detachment of mexican army soldiers in 1831 mexican authorities lent settlers of gonzales a small cannon to help protect them from frequent comanche raids over next four years political situation in mexico deteriorated and in 1835 several states revolted as unrest spread colonel domingo de ugartechea commander of all mexican troops in texas felt it unwise to leave residents of gonzales with a weapon and requested return of cannon when initial request was refused ugartechea sent 100 dragoons to retrieve cannon soldiers neared gonzales on september 29 but colonists used a variety of excuses to keep them from town while secretly sending messengers to request assistance from nearby communities within two days up to 140 texians gathered in gonzales all determined not to give up cannon on october 1 settlers voted to initiate a fight mexican soldiers opened fire as texians approached their camp in early hours of october 2 after several hours of desultory firing mexican soldiers withdrew hardin 1994 p 12 although skirmish had little military significance it marked a clear break between colonists and mexican government and is considered to have been start of texas revolution news of skirmish spread throughout united states where it was often referred to as lexington of texas cannon s fate is disputed it may have been buried and rediscovered in 1936 or it may have been seized by mexican troops after battle of alamo
