alfred john shout vc mc 8 august 1882 11 august 1915 was a new zealand born soldier and an australian recipient of victoria cross vc highest decoration for gallantry in face of enemy awarded to members of british and commonwealth armed forces shout was posthumously awarded vc for his actions at lone pine in august 1915 during gallipoli campaign of first world war after ottoman forces had counterattacked and seized a large stretch of australians front line shout gathered a small party of men and charged down one trench throwing bombs he killed eight turkish soldiers and managed to clear others to retake trench in a similar action later that day and supported by another officer he recaptured further ground amid hard fighting in final push forward shout simultaneously lit three bombs to lob at enemy he successfully threw two but third burst just as it was leaving his hand shout was grievously wounded and died two days later born in wellington shout had served in second boer war as a teenager he rose to sergeant and was mentioned in despatches for saving a wounded man before being discharged in 1902 he remained in south africa for next five years serving as an artilleryman in cape colonial forces from 1903 with his australian born wife and their daughter shout immigrated to sydney in 1907 family settled in darlington where shout worked for resch s brewery as a carpenter and joiner he was also active in part time citizens forces being commissioned just prior to outbreak of first world war in august 1914 he joined in australian imperial force aif for active service overseas and was appointed a lieutenant in 1st battalion after training in egypt he took part in anzac landings at gallipoli on 25 april 1915 for his leadership during invasion and its immediate aftermath shout was awarded military cross and later mentioned in despatches shout s three gallantry awards at gallipoli made him most highly decorated member of aif for campaign early life
