yasui v united states 320 u s 115 1943 was a united states supreme court case regarding constitutionality of curfews used during world war ii when they were applied to citizens of united states case arose out of implementation of executive order 9066 by u s military to create zones of exclusion along west coast of united states where japanese americans were subjected to curfews and eventual removal to relocation centers this presidential order followed attack on pearl harbor that brought america into world war ii and inflamed existing anti japanese sentiment in country in their decision supreme court held that application of curfews against citizens is constitutional as a companion case to hirabayashi v united states both decided on june 21 1943 court affirmed conviction of u s born minoru yasui court remanded case to district court for sentencing as lower court had determined curfew was not valid against citizens but yasui had forfeited his citizenship by working for japanese consulate yasui and hirabayashi decisions along with later ex parte endo and korematsu v united states decisions determined legality of curfews and relocations during war in 1980s new information was
