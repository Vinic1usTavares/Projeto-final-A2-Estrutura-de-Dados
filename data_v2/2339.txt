sir francis walsingham 1532 6 april 1590 was principal secretary to queen elizabeth i of england from 20 december 1573 until his death and is popularly remembered as her spymaster born to a well connected family of gentry walsingham attended cambridge university and travelled in continental europe before embarking on a career in law at age of twenty a committed protestant during reign of catholic queen mary i of england he joined other expatriates in exile in switzerland and northern italy until mary s death and accession of her protestant half sister elizabeth walsingham rose from relative obscurity to become one of small coterie who directed elizabethan state overseeing foreign domestic and religious policy he served as english ambassador to france in early 1570s and witnessed st bartholomew s day massacre as principal secretary he supported exploration colonization use of england s maritime strength and plantation of ireland he worked to bring scotland and england together overall his foreign policy demonstrated a new understanding of role of england as a maritime protestant power with intercontinental trading ties he oversaw operations that penetrated spanish military
