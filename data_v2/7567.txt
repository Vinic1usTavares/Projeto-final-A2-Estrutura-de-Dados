north eastern area command was one of several geographically based commands raised by royal australian air force raaf during world war ii for most of its existence it controlled units based in central and northern queensland as well as papua new guinea it was formed in january 1942 from eastern part of former northern area command which had covered all of northern australia and papua headquartered at townsville queensland north eastern area command s responsibilities included air defence aerial reconnaissance and protection of sea lanes within its territory its flying units equipped with fighters reconnaissance bombers dive bombers and transports took part in battles of rabaul port moresby and milne bay in 1942 and landings at hollandia and aitape in 1944 area command continued to operate after war but its assets and staffing were much reduced its responsibilities were subsumed in february 1954 by raaf s new functional commands home operational training and maintenance commands
