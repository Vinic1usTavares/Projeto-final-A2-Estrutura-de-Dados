major general sir thompson capper 20 october 1863 27 september 1915 was a highly decorated and senior british army officer who served with distinction in second boer war and was a divisional commander during first world war at battle of loos in 1915 capper was shot by a sniper as he reconnoitred front line during an assault by his division on german positions he died next day in a casualty clearing station from wounds to both lungs his grave is in nearby lillers communal cemetery capper was an active and vigorous soldier who had been wounded just six months before his death in an accidental grenade detonation shortly before this wound he had been knighted by king george v for his service in command of his division during first battle of ypres field marshal sir john french commented upon his death that he was a most distinguished and capable leader and his death will be severely felt sir john french s ninth despatch long long trail retrieved 9 july 2007 he was also a keen military staff college before being transferred with his unit to india it was in india that capper saw his first action when in 1895 his regiment was attached to a force sent to indian afghan border to relieve a trapped british force in chitral three years later he was again in action as an advisor to an egyptian unit of anglo egyptian army under horatio kitchener which travelled down nile in final campaign
