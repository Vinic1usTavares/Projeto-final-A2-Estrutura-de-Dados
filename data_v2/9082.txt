leslie horvath october 12 1921 november 14 1995 was an american football quarterback and halfback who won heisman trophy while playing for ohio state university in 1944 horvath was first ohio state player to win heisman an award given to best college football player in united states school retired his jersey number 22 in 2001 horvath grew up in parma ohio a suburb of cleveland and became a standout high school athlete despite his small stature he entered ohio state in 1939 on a work scholarship but tried out for and made football team following year he played as a reserve halfback on 1942 team coached by paul brown that won ohio state s first ever national championship horvath graduated that year and moved to ohio state s dental school in 1944 however acting ohio state football coach carroll widdoes asked horvath to rejoin team taking advantage of a world war ii era rule allowing graduate students with remaining eligibility to play horvath agreed and helped lead buckeyes to a 9 0 record and a second place showing in ap poll he won heisman and was named an all american after season horvath graduated from dental school in 1945 and served as a dental officer in u s navy for two years following his discharge he played professionally for los angeles rams in 1947 and 1948 before being released and signing with cleveland browns in 1949 browns won all america football conference championship that year horvath retired from playing in 1950 and moved to los angeles to practice dentistry and lived there for rest of his life horvath was inducted into college football hall of fame in 1966 and ohio state athletics hall of fame in 1977 early life horvath was born to croatian immigrants in south bend indiana in 1921 his family soon moved to parma ohio a suburb of cleveland he attended parma senior high school starting in 1936 and played on track basketball and football teams until 11th grade he decided to switch schools because he felt his basketball teammates were not taking sport seriously horvath s family relocated and in 1938 he enrolled at james ford rhodes high school in cleveland one of parma s rivals playing as a quarterback for rhodes rams horvath guided team to seven straight wins in 1938 but team lost to west technical high school for a chance to be cleveland senate league s western conference representative in city championship he graduated in 1939 college career after graduating horvath attended ohio state university on a work scholarship but managed to make school s football team in 1940 horvath was small for a football player he weighed just 160 pounds but was a quick runner and had a strong arm ohio state s football team was a disappointment in 1940 however finishing season with a 4 4 win loss record under head coach francis schmidt schmidt was fired after season and replaced by paul brown an ohio high school coach who had guided massillon washington to a series of undefeated records and state championships brown simplified ohio state s offense but imposed a level of discipline and organization that had been absent under schmidt horvath was a reserve halfback in buckeyes single wing offense in 1941 when team posted a 6 1 1 win loss tie record and finished second in big ten conference standings he played in many games but senior fullback jack graf and senior halfback tom got most of carries for ohio state despite his small frame brown recognized horvath s potential as a senior in 1942 and made him a regular starter at halfback beside paul sarringhaus and fullback gene fekete while sarringhaus and fekete were ohio state s main offensive weapons horvath averaged eight yards per carry in a victory over pittsburgh and scored two touchdowns and passed for 109 yards in a win over illinois in cleveland ohio state was ranked first in country in ap poll early in season but fell in rankings after a loss to wisconsin in october team won rest of its games however including a 21 7 victory over arch rival michigan at end of season horvath passed to sarringhaus for a 35 yard touchdown and caught another 32 yard touchdown pass from sarringhaus in michigan game ohio state s 9 1 record put it on top of big ten standings and in final ap poll giving school its first ever national championship horvath expected his college football career to be over in 1942 he finished his undergraduate degree that year and enrolled in a graduate program at ohio state university college of dentistry ohio state s football program meanwhile struggled in 1943 after brown and many of its best players entered military during world war ii carroll widdoes an assistant under brown was appointed acting head coach and led team to a 3 6 record following year widdoes asked horvath to return to team taking advantage of a wartime rule that allowed college programs to use graduate students if they had not exhausted their four years of college eligibility widdoes promised horvath a leading role as team s left halfback a level of prominence he had been denied under brown horvath agreed to come back and be a veteran leader for a team that was composed mostly of freshmen because of older players service in war horvath had a breakout season in 1944 gaining 669 rushing yards and 1 200 all purpose yards as buckeyes turned in a 9 0 record and finished second in national polls highlights of horvath s season included scoring winning touchdown in ohio state s annual matchup against michigan calling all of ohio state s offensive plays he was nicknamed playing coach horvath was named a first team all american by sportswriters and most valuable player in big ten after season he was voted by his teammates as ohio state s most valuable player he also won heisman trophy an award given each year to best college football player in country horvath was first ohio state player to win heisman and he remains only heisman winner not to have played football previous season in early 1945 horvath played in annual east west shrine game a college all star game while at ohio state he was a member of delta tau delta fraternity military and professional career after graduating from ohio state s dental school in 1945 horvath signed to play for cleveland rams of national football league horvath however applied for a commission to join u s navy and was sworn in as a lieutenant j g that august he was sent at first to naval station great lakes in illinois for training where he practiced dentistry and acted as an assistant to brown who had entered navy and was coaching base s football team horvath served in hawaii and coached a football team there that won a service national championship he later traveled on assignment as far as china as a naval dental officer before his discharge from navy in 1947 there was speculation that horvath might join cleveland browns a new team coached by brown in all america football conference aafc horvath was still under contract with rams however and joined team which had moved from cleveland to los angeles in 1946 led by quarterback bob waterfield los angeles finished season with a 6 6 record in 1947 horvath rushed for 68 yards and had three receptions horvath had 118 yards of rushing next season when rams finished with a 6 5 1 record and took third place in nfl west division horvath who worked as a dentist in offseason in los angeles was released by rams in 1949 he signed with browns a week later closing his dental office and moving to cleveland to reunite with brown horvath who was used primarily on defense early in season had an 84 yard fumble return for a touchdown in a game against new york yankees and ran for two touchdowns in a november game against chicago hornets cleveland finished season with a 9 1 2 record and won a fourth straight aafc championship aafc disbanded after season and browns were absorbed by nfl but horvath decided to quit football to practice dentistry back in california later life and death horvath married shirley phillips an airline hostess after 1949 season and moved back to los angeles he coached little league football and practiced dentistry in glendale california a major los angeles suburb for rest of his life his wife died in 1973 he remarried two years later to ruby whom he met in hawaii while on vacation in 1974 they were married for 20 years until horvath s death in 1995 of heart failure horvath was inducted into college football hall of fame in 1966 and became a member of ohio state s athletics hall of fame in 1977 ohio state retired his number 22 uniform in 2001 six years after his death he was inducted into parma senior high school athletics hall of fame in 2007 awards and honors 1942 ap national championship with ohio state buckeyes 1944 heisman trophy winner 1944
