british army of wearing a badge on back of its headdress as well as front a tradition that originated with 28th regiment after it fought in two ranks back to back at battle of alexandria in 1801 at its formation regiment comprised two regular two militia and two volunteer battalions and saw its first action during second boer war before first world war regiment s four auxiliary battalions were converted to three territorial force battalions and a special reserve battalion and a further 18 battalions were added to regiment s establishment during war sixteen battalions of regiment saw active
