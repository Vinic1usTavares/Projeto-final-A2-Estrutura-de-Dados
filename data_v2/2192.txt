david watts morgan 18 december 1867 23 february 1933 who later in life hyphenated his name to watts morgan was a welsh trade unionist a labour politician and a member of parliament mp from 1918 to 1933 described as straddling transition in south wales miners politics from lib to socialism but never fully representative of either morgan encouraged rhondda miners to enlist in army in 1914 following outbreak of first world war and was appointed commander of order of british empire for his efforts he initially served in welsh regiment before becoming a lieutenant colonel in labour corps morgan was awarded distinguished service order for bravery at battle of cambrai in 1917 earning him nickname dai alphabet in south wales early life david watts morgan was born in skewen wales in 1867 to thomas and margaret morgan he was educated at skewen elementary school until age of eleven when he began work as a pit boy helping miners with less strenuous work such as manning ventilation doors at age of seventeen he was employed as a coal miner in ynyshir in rhondda in 1880 watts morgan became a at newly opened national colliery in wattstown a responsible position that involved tallying weight of coal extracted by each miner when it reached surface during his time at wattstown he took evening classes to become a mining engineer he never practised once qualified but knowledge he gained was useful to him in his role as leader of several mine rescues and gave him a practical knowledge that informed his later political life trade unionism from watts morgan rose to position of district miners agent in 1898 becoming a member of rhondda labour and liberal association rlla watts morgan followed in political liberalism of william mabon abraham and began speaking at rlla meetings and banquets in 1899 porth and cymmer seat for glamorgan county council gcc fell vacant and watts morgan was seen as a viable candidate from both liberal and labour standpoints but was surprisingly defeated at by election by local timber merchant david jenkins next year jenkins declined to stand for re election but miners district committee refused watts morgan time off from his agent s duties and seat was won uncontested by colleague james baker williams 1996 p 64 in 1902 james baker died at age of 41 leaving an opening in gcc on this occasion watts morgan was permitted to advance as a labour candidate and was returned unopposed williams 1996 p 65 by 1903 he was being talked of as a possible candidate for a south wales parliamentary constituency he joined south wales miners federation at its inception and by 1902 he was pushing for a greater political role for organisation by 1908 federation was making great headway in rhondda and was substantially improving conditions for miners at this time watts morgan was agent and district secretary at no 1 rhondda district under william abraham lewis 1959 p 173 a moderate leader he worked fruitlessly alongside william abraham to resolve 1910 11 cambrian combine dispute after two men were shunned by more radical miners leaders although espousing liberalism and opposing affiliation of miners federation of great britain to labour party watts morgan joined more radical and marxist plebs league and sat on board of governors for central labour college military service on 4 august 1914 watts morgan enlisted as a private in 10th battalion 1st rhondda of welsh regiment one of service battalions formed as part of kitchener s army he was commissioned as a lieutenant in 17th battalion 1st glamorgan on 7 october and was promoted to captain on 16 january 1916 watts morgan was not initially sent to france being used instead as a strong voice in recruitment of men from rhondda into british army he also took part in recruitment campaigns in north wales where his fluency in welsh language was invaluable watts morgan regularly made known his opposition to peace cranks who were insulting boys of whom we are all so proud williams 1996 pg 86 in march 1915 a committee of rhondda figures presented morgan with a cheque for 100 guineas to mark his contribution to recruiting and western mail named him organiser of victory on 15 may 1916 watts morgan transferred to a works battalion of king s liverpool regiment he was promoted to major on 24 november 1916 and went to serve in france works battalions were absorbed by labour corps forerunner of royal pioneer corps in 1917 watts morgan was three times mentioned in despatches and on 4 may 1918 was awarded distinguished service order for leading his pioneer unit in a counter attack against german forces that were breaking through british lines citation for this award was published on 5 july 1918 and read after armistice he commanded a demobilisation station for his work there he received a letter of thanks from king despite oxford dictionary of national biography reporting watts morgan being promoted to lieutenant colonel on 8 this changed to lieutenant colonel watts morgan on 30 march 1920 was appointed commander of order of british empire for services in connection with recruiting in south wales member of parliament in february 1918 watts morgan was selected as labour candidate for newly formed rhondda east constituency as one of patriotic miners leaders lloyd george coalition did not run a candidate against him therefore watts morgan was elected to seat unopposed there was evidence that some wished to run a liberal candidate against watts morgan but nothing came of it williams 1996 pp 84 85 watts morgan made his maiden speech in house of commons in april 1919 on one of his special interests housing he addressed house on state and shortage of housing in welsh coalfields as chief cause of industrial unrest people have been herded together and that is reason why there is much unrest in our district at present williams 1996 p 129 not a regular speaker in house watts morgan busied himself with work on various committees dealing mainly with gas electricity river pollution home office and police council despite his work on private bills and his friendly relationship with prime minister ramsay morgan made it clear that he would defend interests of ex servicemen and those injured in industry he opposed temperance demand for local veto who s who in new parliament ed by t w philip gee 1922 p nearly 13 000 votes watts morgan was a socialist and trade unionist but he was also a strong anti communist and opposed local men of moscow trade unionists who had embraced communism and whom he saw as extremists in 1929 election his main rival was well known local liberal dr r d chalke but biggest interest came from first communist challenger in rhondda arthur horner williams 1996 p 157 although watts morgan had been unwell for first few months of year he roused himself to attend ferndale may day demonstration when he discovered he was sharing platform with communist party of great britain members horner a j cook and dai lloyd davies he left stage and joined crowd so he could heckle horner s speech from floor watts morgan described horner as emissary of blood stained comintern of russia working to break down democratic government and trades union organization of this country he had little cause for concern and was re elected to rhondda east by a majority of almost 9 000 a smaller majority remained substantial williams 1996 p 159 watts morgan s death in 1933 forced a by election which was won by william mainwaring personal life watts morgan was married twice first to elizabeth williams then to blanche amy morgan blanche was herself a strong campaigner for miners rights and was among a group of agents wives who promoted provision of pithead baths bathing areas for miners at surface she was outspoken in her views and once supported a political rival of her husband s party forcing watts morgan to make a public apology watts morgan was a keen sportsman and enjoyed playing golf and bowls he was a member of several organisations including freemasons and foresters friendly societies and was president of rhondda and pontypridd district of british legion a calvinist methodist his local place of worship was bethlehem church in porth watts morgan died at his home in porth on 23 february 1933 while still in office he was survived by his wife two sons and four
