lieutenant general sir reginald george pollard 20 january 1903 9 march 1978 was a senior commander in australian army he served as chief of general staff from 1960 to 1963 born in bathurst new south wales pollard graduated from royal military college duntroon in 1924 a regular officer he served as adjutant quartermaster in several battalions of citizens military forces cmf during 1920s and 1930s in 1938 he was posted to england to undertake staff training which was cut short by outbreak of second world war pollard joined second australian imperial force in 1940 and following year saw action with 7th division in middle east where he was mentioned in despatches promoted to colonel in 1942 he became senior staff officer of 7th division in new guinea and was awarded distinguished service order for his actions he spent much of remainder of war in staff and training positions in australia pollard s early post war roles involved recruit training land air warfare administration and planning in 1953 he was promoted to brigadier and took command of australian army component of british commonwealth forces korea he joined military board as a major general in 1954 and was appointed a commander of order of british empire following year in 1957 he was promoted to lieutenant general and took charge of eastern command in sydney two years later he was appointed a companion of order of
