james ernest newland vc 22 august 1881 19 march 1949 was an australian soldier policeman and a recipient of victoria cross highest decoration for gallantry in face of enemy that can be awarded to members of british and commonwealth armed forces newland was awarded victoria cross following three separate actions in april 1917 during attacks against german forces retreating to hindenburg line while in command of a company newland successfully led his men in several assaults on german positions and repulsed subsequent counter attacks born in victorian town of highton newland joined australian military in 1899 and saw active service during second boer war he continued to serve in australian army s permanent forces on his return to australia and completed several years service in artillery transferring to militia in 1907 newland became a police officer in tasmania before re joining permanent forces in 1910 following outbreak of first world war he was appointed to australian imperial force and was among first wave of men to land at gallipoli in days following landing newland was wounded and evacuated to egypt where he was commissioned as a second lieutenant transferring to western front in 1916 newland was mentioned in despatches for his leadership while commanding a company during an attack at mouquet farm he was wounded twice more during war and medically discharged in march 1918 he returned to service with permanent army newland held various appointments between two world wars and retired a lieutenant colonel in 1941 he died of heart failure in 1949 early life newland was born in geelong suburb of highton victoria on 22 august 1881 to william newland a labourer and his wife louisa jane nee wall in 1899 he enlisted in commonwealth military forces and was assigned to 4th battalion australian commonwealth horse as a private unit later embarked for south africa where newland saw active service in cape town during second boer war returning to australia in 1902 newland re settled in victoria and joined royal australian artillery in july following year he served in artillery for over four years before transferring to militia in september 1907 in 1909 he became a police officer in tasmanian police force where he remained until august 1910 when he re enlisted in permanent army he was posted to australian instructional corps he served with this unit until outbreak of first world war in a ceremony at sheffield tasmania on 27 december 1913 newland married florence may mitchell first world war on 17 august 1914 newland transferred to newly raised australian imperial force following british empire s declaration of war on germany and her allies assigned to 12th battalion he was made its regimental quartermaster sergeant and embarked from hobart aboard hmat geelong on 20 october bound for egypt following a brief stop in western australia troopship arrived at its destination seven weeks later 12th battalion spent following four months training in egyptian desert at commencement of gallipoli campaign 3rd australian brigade of which 12th battalion was part was designated as covering force for anzac landing and as such was first unit ashore on 25 april 1915 at approximately 04 30 newland was wounded in days following landing suffering a gunshot wound to his arm and was evacuated to 1st general hospital while at hospital
