percy shaw jeffrey 14 march 1862 cheltenham england 22 february 1952 was a respected english schoolmaster and author of several books on a range of topics including significant contributions towards teaching of phonetics in schools shaw jeffrey taught at a variety of schools before spending sixteen years as headmaster at colchester royal grammar school with his wife alice he retired first to south africa then to town of whitby north yorkshire in 1916 where he spent his time between numerous trips to countries around world early life percy shaw jeffrey grew up with his parents thomas ashby jeffrey a chemist and mary helen jeffrey nee sparrow in cheltenham gloucestershire and with his younger siblings ada constance russell henry ethel maude and amy louise for three years from 1875 he attended trent college long eaton where he rose to become head of school runner up for duke of devonshire s gold medal with first class honours in cambridge local third in list in latin and distinguished in french early teaching career whilst reading for a maths scholarship at university of marburg shaw jeffrey was offered chance to work at trent college unofficially as first headmaster s private secretary and then as a teacher unfortunately he was unable to win marburg scholarship in 1881 and left unemployed after death of headmaster year after he took up a post as a junior master at emanuel school wandsworth soon after its move to new premises on 30 january 1884 he matriculated into university of oxford as a member of queen s college where he completed a bachelor of arts in 1887 and was granted his ma in 1890 he was disappointed to receive a degree with only third class honours however and was advised to put any ambition of teaching on hold advice which he did not follow after a brief spell teaching at christ s college finchley for six months during 1887 shaw jeffrey became an assistant mathematics and science master at skinners school royal tunbridge wells whilst simultaneously studying for an inter bachelor of science with first class honours whilst at skinners he published words he had written for school song set to music by cuthbert h cronk which persist to present day leopards song sheet music 2nd edition 1927 although it was not his initial intention in 1893 he took up a post as private tutor to a student he had been coaching at skinners school and travelled to florida with him upon his return and owing to a shortage of officers a short diversion from his teaching duties during 1893 saw shaw jeffrey join volunteer battalion of royal west kent regiment unable to afford a new uniform then costing ps80 after a change in regiment apparel to a much more popular red with light blue colour scheme he was forced to resign his commission later that year in 1894 he took up another post this time to become a holiday modern languages tutor a well paid position to son of sir thomas and lady glen coats thomas glen coats later to compete at 1908 olympics putting his schoolmaster plans aside he travelled with them to france and next year to germany where both enrolled at university of marburg once glen coats was safely installed at merton college oxford shaw jeffrey returned to university to study for a phil doc but shortly before completing degree in 1897 he was persuaded by dean of queen s college to take up a position at clifton college bristol headmaster at colchester royal grammar school thumb 300px colchester royal grammar school circa 1908 having been an assistant master at clifton college for three years he was offered a headmaster in 1916 but kept a lasting interest in school during his 16 year stay at crgs student numbers increased from 29 to 180 boys under his guidance particularly through introduction of day pupils shaw jeffrey is also credited with introduction of many changes at school including introduction of purple blazers setting up of old colcestrian society for former boys and quite often honoured former masters a preparatory school a cadet corps an orchestra a bugle band and a series of school entertainments and theatrical performances and acquisition of several buildings on behalf of school he was a national pioneer of teaching of modern european languages through phonetics employed language teachers from germany and france and set up arrangements for foreign study during holidays thumb crgs shaw jeffrey married his wife alice whom he had met on a mediterranean cruise and who stayed at school throughout his time there on his retirement they moved to south africa where he did valuable works on press for allied cause edited by b a b barton with a foreword by hervey w g benham from 1914 onwards they also had possession of whitby s bagdale old hall built in 1530 as a residence and eventually took residence there on his return from south africa though they were often away travelling despite retirement from any sort of career shaw jeffrey maintained that he had no intention of leading an idle life and was variously an inspector and examiner for both university of cambridge and university of london and an assessor for all of cambridge s french papers for five years after introduction of higher certificate he was also an occasional inspector in modern languages for board of education and deputised as professor of french at university of durham while professor himself was incapacitated due to illness he was an assessor of all modern language papers set at durham for five years he continued to fill in positions inspecting in spanish and italian despite only having learned languages during his travels although he spent barely 10 or 12 weeks in any one year in whitby he was still attached to it shortly before his death he published a haunt of ancient peace a history of bagdale old hall with sir d arcy power kbe and also made several large donations ps3000 to whitby parish church ps2000 to museum and ps500 to whitby literary and philosophy society shaw jeffrey died on 22 february 1952 leaving alice as a widow couple had just celebrated their golden wedding anniversary a fact reported in essex county standard along with a short obituary and an overview of latest old colcestrian annual meeting held only a day after his death at which a variety of tributes had been paid article describes him as one of colchester s most famous headmasters and one with tons of personality his death was particularly poignant for members of crgs for it came two days before a world war two war memorial for which he and his wife had contributed majority of money was to be unveiled and dedicated on her death his wife left their house in whitby to whitby literary and philosophy society though efforts to turn it into a museum were ultimately unsuccessful
