padma vibhushan is second highest civilian award of republic of india second only to bharat ratna instituted on 2 january 1954 award is given for exceptional and distinguished service without distinction of race occupation position or sex award criteria include service in any field including service rendered by government servants including doctors and scientists but excluding those working with public sector undertakings award has been bestowed on 307 individuals including twelve posthumous and 20 non citizen recipients during 1 may and 15 september of every year recommendations civilian awards bharat ratna highest civilian award and three tier padma vibhushan classified into pahla varg class i dusra varg class ii and tisra varg class iii which rank below bharat ratna on 15 january 1955 padma vibhushan was reclassified into three different awards padma vibhushan highest of three followed by padma bhushan and padma shri award along with other personal civilian honours was briefly suspended twice in its history for first time in july 1977 when morarji desai was sworn in as fourth prime minister of india for being worthless and politicized suspension was rescinded on 25 january 1980 after indira gandhi became prime minister civilian awards were suspended again in mid 1992 when two public interest litigations were filed in high courts of india one in kerala high court on 13 february 1992 by balaji raghavan and another in madhya pradesh high court indore bench on 24 august 1992 by satya pal anand both petitioners questioned civilian awards being titles per an interpretation of article 18 1 of constitution of india on 25 august 1992 madhya pradesh high court issued a notice temporarily suspending all civilian awards a special division bench of supreme court of india was formed comprising five judges a m ahmadi c j kuldip singh b p jeevan reddy n p singh and s saghir ahmad on 15 december 1995 special division bench restored awards and delivered a judgment that bharat ratna and padma awards are not titles under article 18 of constitution of india regulations award is conferred for exceptional and distinguished service without distinction of race
