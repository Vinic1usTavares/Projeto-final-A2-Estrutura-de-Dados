george julian snowy howell vc mm 19 november 1893 23 december 1964 was an australian recipient of victoria cross highest decoration for gallantry in face of enemy that can be awarded to members of british and commonwealth armed forces howell was decorated with victoria cross following his actions during second battle of bullecourt in which he ran along parapet of a trench bombing german forces attacking his position through use of grenades and thus driving them back born in a suburb of sydney new south wales howell was employed as a builder before enlisting in australian imperial force in june 1915 allotted to force s 1st battalion he served at gallipoli prior to transferring to western front participating in somme offensive of 1916 howell was wounded at pozieres and promoted to corporal in early 1917 during an attack on a german held village he led a rifle bombing section and was awarded military medal for his actions severely wounded in his victoria cross action howell underwent a prolonged hospitalisation period before returning to australia and receiving his discharge on medical grounds settling in coogee he gained employment by working on advertising staff of several newspapers following outbreak of second world war howell served with australian eastern command headquarters but soon sought his discharge and enlisted with united states sea transport service he died in 1964 at age of 71 early life howell was born in sydney suburb of enfield new south wales on 19 november 1893 fourth son of english born carpenter francis john howell and his australian wife martha nee sweeny howell was educated at croydon park and burwood public schools before receiving an apprenticeship as a bricklayer becoming a builder he was employed in his native enfield area of sydney first world war on 3 june 1915 howell enlisted in australian imperial force for service in first world war allotted to 7th reinforcements of 1st battalion as a private he embarked from sydney for egypt on 14 july aboard hmat orsova joining 1st battalion at gallipoli on 1 november howell served on peninsula until allied evacuation following month returning to egypt battalion spent several months training in desert in preparation for service on western front 1st battalion embarked for france in march 1916 and by april unit was entrenched in fromelles sector in third week of july howell
