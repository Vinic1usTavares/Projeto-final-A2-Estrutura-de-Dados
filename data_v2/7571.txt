north western area command was one of several geographically based commands raised by royal australian air force raaf during world war ii its wartime sphere of operations included northern territory adjacent portions of queensland and western australia and dutch east indies command was formed in january 1942 following outbreak of pacific war from western part of northern area command which had covered all of northern australia and papua headquartered at darwin north western area command was initially responsible for air defence aerial reconnaissance and protection of sea lanes within its boundaries in official history of raaf in pacific theatre george odgers described north western area campaign as almost entirely an air war with raid and counter raid odgers air war against japan p 42 from 1943 north western area command s role became increasingly offensive in nature as allies began to advance in new guinea and dutch east indies its combat aircraft ranged from single engined fighters to heavy bombers and were flown by australian british american and dutch squadrons area command continued to operate following end of war but its assets and staffing were much reduced its responsibilities were subsumed in february 1954 by raaf s new functional commands home operational training and maintenance commands area headquarters was disbanded following year history world war ii
