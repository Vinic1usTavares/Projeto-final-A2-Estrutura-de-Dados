air vice marshal george john william mackinolty obe 24 march 1895 24 february 1951 was a senior commander in royal australian air force raaf commencing his service in australian flying corps afc as a mechanic during world war i he rose to become raaf s chief logistics officer for more than twenty years mackinolty was born in victoria and joined afc in 1914 he first saw active duty following year in middle east with no 30 squadron royal flying corps formerly mesopotamian half flight in 1916 he was mentioned in despatches and posted to no 2 squadron afc by end of war he had been commissioned a second lieutenant joining newly formed raaf in august 1921 mackinolty established himself as service s senior logistician between wars first as director of transport and equipment from 1929 to 1935 and thereafter as director of equipment he was appointed an officer of order of british empire in 1937 soon after outbreak of world war ii he became director of supply and was promoted to group captain in june 1942 he was raised to acting air commodore and appointed air member for supply and equipment amse promoted to air vice marshal in 1948 mackinolty continued to serve as amse until his sudden death from cancer in february 1951 aged fifty five early life and world war i born on 24 march 1895 at leongatha victoria mackinolty was son of labourer james mackinolty and his wife mary he
