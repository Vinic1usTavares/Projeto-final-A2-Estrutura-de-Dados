admiral of white sir john balchen 2 february 1670 4 october 1744 sometimes written as balchin was an officer of british royal navy with a long and distinguished career during late 17th and early 18th centuries in course of his service at sea balchen saw action in numerous battles against french and spanish navies across 60 years and three separate wars he was twice captured by french in action both times being exonerated and commended for defence of his ships against overwhelming odds balchen died in shipwreck of 100 gun first rate hms victory off casquets in channel islands during operations to deter french blockading of spanish and portuguese ports during war of austrian succession a capable and efficient officer balchen never found wealth and prestige fellow officers secured in other commissions a fact which remained a source of frustration to him until his elevation to knighthood shortly before his death nine years war balchen was born in february 1670 only surviving child of yeoman gentleman john balchen and his wife ann home educated balchen took a commission in royal navy aged 15 and seven years later gained promotion to lieutenant for most of this period balchen was stationed in west indies and during his service there was lucky in his health west indies command was considered very dangerous during this period mortality rates amongst sailors stationed there being very high due to malaria and yellow fever balchen sir john oxford dictionary of national biography daniel a baugh retrieved 9 november 2007 high death rate led to rapid promotion for those who survived and balchen was made post captain at relatively young age of 27 during nine years war balchen had spent war aboard hms dragon and hms cambridge under admiral john neville who was impressed enough with his subordinate to give him command of prize ship hms virgin safe conduct of which to england earned him step to captain war of spanish succession as with majority of royal navy balchen was placed in reserve at war s conclusion and returned to england to await further deployment whilst there he married susannah daughter of an army colonel marriage produced six children two of whom survived into adulthood frances who later married temple west vice admiral temple west and george who followed his father into navy in 1701 balchen was again at sea commanding small fireships hms firebrand and then hms vulcan with sir george rooke s fleet off spanish coast at outbreak of war of spanish succession he was probably engaged during battle of vigo bay in 1702 where rooke s fleet way of prize money next year he was transferred to 54 gun hms chester with which he was dispatched to west african coast a region considered almost as fatal as west indies surviving once again balchen remained in chester and was attached to convoys bound for portugal and virginia balchen suffered his first defeat on 10 october 1707 leaving safety of portsmouth harbour his convoy was ambushed by a french squadron under forbin and duguay trouin in what became battle at lizard although dozen french warships were larger and stronger than convoy escorts balchen took his ship into battle with other
