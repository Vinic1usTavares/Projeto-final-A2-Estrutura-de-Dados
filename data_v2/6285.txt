brigadier general james creel marshall 15 october 1897 19 july 1977 was a united states army corps of engineers officer who was initially in charge of manhattan project to build an atomic bomb during world war ii a member of june 1918 class of united states military academy at west point that graduated early due to world war i marshall saw service on mexican border between wars he worked on engineering projects in united states and panama canal zone in january 1942 shortly after united states entered world war ii he became district engineer of syracuse district and oversaw construction of rome air depot in june 1942 marshall was placed in charge of manhattan project then known as laboratory development of substitute materials although superseded as head of project by brigadier general leslie r groves jr in september he was manhattan district engineer from 13 august 1942 to 13 august 1943 in november 1943 he became assistant chief of staff g 4 of united states army services of supply usasos in southwest pacific area serving in australia new guinea and philippines marshall left army in 1947 and moved to riverside connecticut where he worked for m w kellogg he later joined koppers building a coal loading facility in turkey and worked on mining projects in africa he was commissioner of highways in minnesota from 1961 to 1965 early life and career james creel marshall was born in plattsburg missouri on 14 october 1897 son of walter scott marshall and his wife cora sutphen nee creel he was appointed to united states military academy at west point in 1915 his classmates included hugh john casey and lucius d clay entire class graduated early on 12 june 1918 due to world war i and marshall who was ranked 24th in class was commissioned as a substantive first lieutenant and temporary captain in united states army corps of engineers he was posted to camp a a humphreys from 8 to 15 july 1918 and then was sent to engineer officers training school at camp lee virginia for additional training while there he married mabel estelle wolff from brooklyn they had two children beryl born in 1919 and robert creel born in 1921 on 24 august 1918 marshall joined 8th engineers at fort bliss texas he returned to camp a a humphreys as a student officer from 10 february 1919 to 12 june 1919 young officers like marshall who had not served overseas during war were sent on battlefield tours from 20 june to 30 august 1919 he toured battlefields of world war i visiting britain france belgium and germany before returning to camp a a humphreys on 10 september 1919 after service at camp a a humphreys with reserve officers training corps marshall was posted to 13th engineers as its adjutant on 10 february 1921 but attended engineer school basic course from 6 june 1921 graduating on 15 august 1921 after which he became an instructor there on 25 june 1922 he became assistant district engineer of 2nd district based in new york city like many of his fellow officers marshall was reduced to his substantive rank of first lieutenant on 18 november 1922 on 4 august 1923 he took charge of engineer office of 3rd new york district located in fort hancock new jersey he then served in panama canal zone as a company commander in 11th engineers from 9 april 1926 to 14 june 1928 he became an instructor in department of engineering at west point on 24 august 1928 he was posted to fort belvoir virginia on 10 august 1932 where he was promoted to captain again on 1 june 1933 there followed duty in office of chief of engineers in washington dc as assistant chief of river and harbor section from 21 january 1937 to 3 september 1939 world war ii marshall attended command and general staff school at fort leavenworth kansas from 11 september 1939 to 3 february 1940 he then became executive officer of 1st engineers with outbreak of world war ii in europe promotion accelerated and he was promoted to major on 1 march 1940 he became district engineer of binghamton district on 25 may 1940 with rank of lieutenant colonel in army of united states from 12 june 1941 on 31 january 1942 he became district engineer of syracuse district which covered new york and part of pennsylvania with rank of colonel from 1 february 1942 at binghamton and syracuse was responsible for a number of major projects including ammunition and explosive plants and construction of rome air depot he also had to attend to flood control measures on upper delaware river on 18 june 1942 marshall was called to washington to take over reorganised atomic bomb project then known as dsm laboratory development of substitute materials marshall read 13 june 1942 report from vannevar bush and james conant and recalled that in a report to colonel leslie r groves jr head of construction branch in office of chief of engineers on 11 august 1942 marshall called for creation a new district without territorial limits to administer dsm project his proposal was approved on 13 august as district engineer of new district marshall reported directly to groves and not chief of engineers he established district headquarters on 18th floor of 270 broadway in new york city with innocuous name of manhattan engineer district following usual practice of naming engineer districts after city in which their headquarters area were located he selected boston firm of stone webster as project s principal contractor marshall and his deputy district engineer kenneth nichols visited tennessee on 30 june 1942 to examine proposed location for production plants in clinch river area but marshall chose to delay actual purchase of land until it was needed nichols felt that marshall s desire for orderly procedures ultimately told against him by september bush was expressing dissatisfaction with slow progress and lack of highest priority for project going to united states secretary of war henry l stimson and then directly to president franklin d roosevelt groves was appointed to head project on 17 september 1942 groves was also a colonel and ranked below marshall on permanent list although groves was promoted to brigadier general before assuming command on 23 september according to nichols groves and marshall disagreed in a major way on how to handle personnel and nichols did witness several confrontations major general wilhelm d styer chief of staff of army service forces decided that marshall would be replaced by nichols marshall would be replaced given an overseas posting he was informed by nichols on june to him it seemed he was getting fired although he had previously expressed a desire to groves for just such an overseas assignment indeed when first assigned to project both nichols and groves had also have expressed a preference for an overseas combat assignment nichols had liked working for him and was happy to have him as a buffer between groves and myself because groves was abrasive and often very critical marshall asked nichols to transfer his secretary virginia olsson to oak ridge when manhattan district headquarters moved there leaving nichols s own secretary anne phillips in new york office this concern for personnel nichols noted was typical of marshall since manhattan district had been officially created on 13 august 1942 marshall chose to formally leave on 13 august 1943 so that he had held job for exactly one year groves s account says that marshall was awarded legion of merit for his service with manhattan district he was posted to camp sutton north carolina as commander of engineer replacement training center there until 26 november 1943 he then had his sought after overseas service in southwest pacific area where he became assistant chief of staff g 4 of united states army services of supply usasos he saw service in australia new guinea and philippines he was promoted to brigadier general on 10 november 1944 and awarded bronze star medal he returned to united states for medical reasons on 12 february 1945 his final command was of boston port of embarkation for which he received army commendation medal on 29 january 1946 he became head of engineer research and development laboratory at fort belvoir he reverted to his permanent rank of colonel on 5 march 1946 and retired from army on a disability on 31 march 1947 on 29 june 1948 he was promoted to brigadier general on retired list later life after leaving army he moved to riverside connecticut where he worked for m w kellogg he eventually tired of commuting to new york city and took a job with koppers building a coal loading facility in turkey he then worked for united nations korean relief at headquarters of united nations in new york he was also involved in mining projects in africa he made his home in skaneateles new york while working on various projects around world in january 1961 he accepted an offer from governor of minnesota elmer l andersen to become that state s commissioner of highways his four year term was marred by a change of government in state in 1963 and his final years saw a series of clashes with new governor karl f rolvaag and his attorney general walter mondale when his term as commissioner of highways ended in 1965 marshall returned to skaneateles where he became an engineering consultant and a professional engineering arbitrator he served as mayor of skaneateles for six years his contributions included addition of an ambulance squad to fire department and construction of an indoor ice skating rink his wife mabel died of cancer in 1976 on 18 july 1977 marshall also died from cancer two are buried together in west point cemetery marshall s son robert creel marshall graduated from west point with class of 1943 and served in europe during world war ii he later served in
