hms ramillies was a royal sovereign class pre dreadnought battleship of royal navy named after battle of ramillies ship was built by j g thompson at clydebank starting with her keel laying in august 1890 she was launched in march 1892 and commissioned into mediterranean fleet as flagship following october she was armed with a main battery of four 13 5 inch guns and a secondary battery of ten 6 inch guns ship had a top speed of 16 5 knots ramillies served as flagship of mediterranean fleet up to 1899 and again from 1900 to 1902 after taking part in manoeuvres off coast of portugal she returned to england for a refit in 1903 upon completion she was commissioned into reserve in 1905 she suffered damage while participating in combined manoeuvres following year and was recommissioned into special service division of home fleet in 1907 becoming parent ship of 4th division of home fleet in 1910 she was relieved of that role a year later before being reduced to material reserve at devonport in august 1911 and stripped and laid up at motherbank for disposal in july 1913 she was sold for scrap in october 1913 and towed to italy to be broken up following month design thumb left right elevation and deck plan as depicted in brassey s naval annual 1905 royal sovereign class battleships were based on admiral class barbette ships but contained several alterations freeboard was raised barbettes armour was extended and an upper belt and secondary armour were added they could also obtain a higher speed but were 4 000 tonnes larger ramillies was long overall and had a beam of 75 ft and a draft of 27 ft 6in she displaced up to 15 580 tonnes at her full combat load her propulsion system consisted of two 3 cylinder triple expansion engines powered by eight coal fired cylindrical boilers with natural draught her engines provided a top speed of 15 5 knots at 9 000 indicated horsepower 16 5 knots at 11 000 indicated horsepower could be obtained with forced draught she had a crew of 712 officers and enlisted men when built ships of royal sovereign class rolled too heavily under certain conditions bilge keels were added to compensate for problem and ships proved to be excellent seaboats quite capable of maintaining high speeds in a seaway gardiner 1979 p 32 ships were well constructed and probably most substantial built for royal navy even if they suffered from excessive weight and fittings burt 1988 p 63 in view of r a burt they were highly successful at that time they were probably unequalled in all round fighting efficiency ramillies was armed with four breech loading 13 5 inch guns on two barbettes with armour ranging from 11 to 17 inches in thickness ship also carried ten quick fire qf 6 inch guns four of which were mounted in casemates on main deck plus sixteen qf 6 pounder guns of an unknown type and a dozen qf 3 pounder hotchkiss guns she was also equipped with seven 18 inch torpedo tubes two of which were submerged between 1899 and 1902 3 pounder guns were removed from upper tops above water torpedo tubes were removed in 1902 1905 burt 1988 pp 78 79 remaining 6 inch guns on upper deck were mounted in 5 inch armoured casemates between 1902 and 1904 all of armour was supplied by builders j g thompson of clydebank waterline belt was 252 ft long by 8 ft 8in deep and its armour varied in thickness between 14 and 18 inches bulkheads were protected by 14 to 16 inches of armour middle deck covering belt was 3 inches thick and lower deck forward and aft of belt was 2 5 inches thick while upper belt between middle and main decks was coated in 3 to 4 inches of armour casemates for 6 inch guns were protected by an equal thickness of armour and conning tower was protected with 14 inch armour on forward side and 3 inches of armour on aft burt 1988 p 62 ship s armoured deck was 2 5 to 3 inches thick service history ramillies was built by j g thompson of clydebank at a cost of 600 plus ps78 295 for guns she was laid down
