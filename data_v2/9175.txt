geoffrey of briel in older literature geoffrey of bruyeres was a french knight and third lord of barony of karytaina in principality of achaea in frankish greece he led a colourful and turbulent life narrated in detail in chronicle of morea accounted finest knight in principality he fought in wars against byzantine greeks was captured in battle of pelagonia in 1259 and was sent back to achaea bearing byzantine terms in 1261 geoffrey was twice deprived of his barony once for rebelling against his uncle prince of achaea william ii of villehardouin and then for abandoning principality without leave in order to spend time with a mistress wife of one of his feudatories in italy he was pardoned both times but henceforth held his title as a gift of prince he died childless in 1275 and barony
