a canticle for leibowitz is a post apocalyptic science fiction novel by american writer walter m miller jr first published in 1959 set in a catholic monastery in desert of southwestern united states after a devastating nuclear war book spans thousands of years as civilization rebuilds itself monks of albertian order of leibowitz preserve surviving remnants of man s scientific knowledge until world is again ready for it novel is a fixup of three short stories miller published in magazine of fantasy science fiction that were inspired by author s participation in bombing of monastery at battle of monte cassino during world war ii book is considered one of classics of science fiction and has never been out of print appealing to mainstream and genre critics and readers alike it won 1961 hugo award for best science fiction novel and its themes of religion recurrence and church versus state have generated a significant body of scholarly research a sequel saint leibowitz and wild horse woman was published posthumously in 1997 publication history development by 1955 walter m miller jr had published over 30 science fiction short stories in such magazines as astounding science
