padma bhushan is third highest civilian award in republic of india preceded by bharat ratna and padma vibhushan and followed by padma shri instituted on 2 january 1954 award is given for distinguished service of a high order without distinction of race occupation position or sex award criteria include service in any field including service rendered by government servants including doctors and scientists but exclude those working with public sector undertakings award has been bestowed on 1254 individuals including twenty one posthumous and ninety six non citizen recipients padma awards committee is constituted every year by prime minister of india and recommendations for award are submitted between 1 may and 15 september recommendations are received from all state and union territory governments as well as from ministries of government of india bharat ratna and padma vibhushan awardees institutes of excellence ministers chief ministers and governors of states members of parliament and private individuals committee later submits their recommendations to prime minister and president of india for further approval award recipients are announced on 26 january republic day padma shri award along with other personal civilian honours was briefly suspended twice in its history first time in july 1977 when morarji desai was sworn in as fourth prime minister of india for being worthless and politicized suspension was rescinded on 25 january 1980 after indira gandhi became prime minister civilian awards were suspended again in mid 1992 when two public interest litigations were filed in high courts of india one in kerala high court on 13 february 1992 by balaji raghavan and another in madhya pradesh high court indore bench on 24 august 1992 by satya pal anand both petitioners questioned civilian awards being titles per an interpretation of article 18 1 of constitution of india on 25 august 1992 madhya pradesh high court issued a notice temporarily suspending all civilian awards a special division bench of supreme court of india was formed comprising five judges a m ahmadi c j kuldip singh b p jeevan reddy n p singh and s saghir ahmad on 15 december 1995 special division bench restored awards and delivered a judgment that bharat ratna and padma awards are not titles under article 18 of constitution of india regulations award is conferred for distinguished service of a high order without distinction of race occupation position or sex criteria include service in any field including service rendered by government servants but exclude
