unita zelma blackwell march 18 1933 may 13 2019 was an american civil rights activist who was first african american woman to be elected mayor in u s state of mississippi blackwell 2006 p 10 blackwell was a project director for student nonviolent coordinating committee sncc and helped organize voter drives for african americans across mississippi she was also a founder of us china peoples friendship association a group dedicated to promoting cultural exchange between united states and china barefootin blackwell s autobiography published in 2006 charts her activism early life blackwell was born u z brown on march 18 1933 in lula mississippi to sharecroppers virda mae and willie brown morrison 1987 p 99 blackwell s uncle gave her name u z which she kept until she was in sixth grade when her teacher told her that she needed a real name not just initials blackwell and her teacher decided on unita zelma blackwell 2006 pp 11 47 48 blackwell and her parents lived in lula her grandfather had been murdered by a white plantation boss in 1936 when she was three years old blackwell s father left plantation on which he worked and fled to memphis tennessee fearing for his life after he confronted his boss about speaking to his wife blackwell 2006 pp 12 13 blackwell and her mother left plantation to live with him soon afterward blackwell s family traveled frequently in search of work haskins 1999 p 15 on june 20 1938 blackwell s parents separated due to religious differences blackwell and her mother went to west helena arkansas to live with blackwell s great aunt so that she had opportunity to receive an education blackwell 2006 p 13 a quality education in mississippi was not an option for blackwell because schools there were centered on cultivation of crops and plantation system black children were allowed to attend school for only two months at a time before they were expected to go back to cotton fields while living in west helena blackwell often visited her father in memphis during summer months she would leave west helena and live with her grandfather and grandmother in lula where she helped plant and harvest cotton blackwell 2006 pp 13 14 blackwell spent a majority of her early years chopping cotton for 3 a day in mississippi arkansas and tennessee as well as peeling tomatoes in florida she was 14 when she finished eighth grade final year of school at westside a school in west helena for black children blackwell 2006 pp 48 49 blackwell had to quit school to earn for her family marriage and move she was 25 when she first met jeremiah blackwell a cook for u s army corps of engineers blackwell 2006 pp 52 53 a few years later they traveled to clarksdale mississippi and were married by a justice of peace morrison 1987 p 101 in january 1957 blackwell became extremely ill and was taken to hospital in west helena where she was pronounced dead she was later found to be alive in her hospital room and claims to have had a near death experience blackwell 2006 pp 56 58 on july 2 1957 couple s only son jeremiah blackwell jr jerry was born blackwell 2006 p 55 in 1960 jeremiah s grandmother miss vashti died a few months later blackwells moved into shotgun house that his grandmother had left to him in mayersville mississippi a town of nearly five hundred people blackwell family eventually was able to build a larger brick discrimination blackwell first got involved in civil rights movement in june 1964 when two activists from student nonviolent coordinating committee came to mayersville and in church she belonged to held meetings concerning rights of african americans to vote blackwell 2006 pp 65 74 following week she and seven others went to courthouse to take a voter registration test so that they could vote blackwell 2006 pp 3 8 75 while they were outside courthouse waiting to take test a group of white farmers from area heard what was happening and tried to scare them off her group stayed there all day
