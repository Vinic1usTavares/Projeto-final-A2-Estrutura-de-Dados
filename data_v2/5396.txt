nursing when her mother died in 1952 she returned to lord howe island to look after her father and two younger siblings brother stan and sister robyn while she had left island to attend secondary family she stitched her own clothes on basis of a catalog she had obtained from sydney she tried these clothes on her younger 12 year old sister robyn as a model she won miss australia title in november 1960 and was very modest about winning crown she then went on a prize winning tour of hong kong tokyo honolulu san francisco and many other places thereafter she worked as a model she holds a bachelor of arts degree in arts law and business and a master s degree ma in commerce career sinclair took up environmental cause of her birthplace lord howe island in 1982 when it was listed as an unesco world heritage site though islanders were pleased with heritage status accorded to island sinclair was unhappy with many of planning and management actions initiated by government of australia to conserve newly accorded heritage status as it affected basic rights of islanders her objections were to cutting of pine trees planted in 19th century she considered these trees though not indigenous as part of island s heritage when trees were being felled she threatened to lie down between trees to stop tree cutters her argument was that trees added to aesthetic beauty of island she also objected to neglect of maintenance of embankments that were built to extend airport on island as embankments were eroding she objected to garbage that was being dumped in lagoon of island as it was causing health hazards management plan proposed by government to preserve island s heritage status envisaged restricting tourist accommodation to about 400 and limiting number of cars on island to 100 which she objected for reason that it was limiting opportunities to islanders who had lived there for many years another of her protests was on creation of an aquatic reserve in a part of island she considered this action as detrimental to fishing rights of local people who had fished in area for several years all her objections resulted in ministry of planning and environment agreeing to address issues she had raised in 1967 she worked in prime minister s department and dealt with public relations and facilitated montreal expo in 1980 sinclair became managing director of australian telecommunications users group atug an ngo dedicated to protecting rights of product users in australia in field of telecommunications awards and recognition sinclair was awarded centenary medal in 2001 for service to children especially child abuse and neglect she was made an officer of order of australia ao in 2002 australia day honours for service as a leading advocate for welfare of abused
