o street market also known as northern market is a historic structure located at 1400 7th street nw in shaw neighborhood of washington d c built in 1881 it is one of three 19th century public market buildings still standing in city along with eastern market and georgetown market market was listed on district of columbia inventory of historic sites in 1968 and national register of historic places in 1995 most distinctive architectural element of gothic revival building is its corner tower on 7th and o streets for decades market was a vital shopping destination for area residents following world war ii market and surrounding neighborhood began to deteriorate building was slightly damaged during 1968 washington d c riots but was restored and reopened in 1980 renovations were to begin in 2003 but in february of that year building s roof collapsed under snow following a historic blizzard a decade later rehabilitated building reopened as part of a 325 million mixed use development city
