m 204 is a state trunkline highway that runs across leelanau peninsula between leland and suttons bay in northwestern lower peninsula of us state of michigan trunkline runs through a rural section of leelanau county connecting two villages with county seat originally a gravel road in 1933 it was later paved within first three years of existence segments were realigned to straighten curves in late 1930s and early 1970s near villages of lake leelanau and suttons bay route description m 204 starts at an intersection with m 22 manitou trail south of leland next to duck lake from there it follows duck lake road along south shore of lake leelanau s northern lobe two lane roadway runs eastward and then southeasterly through a mixture of woods and fields to village of lake leelanau one there m 204 follows phillip street across narrows and turns northeasterly bridge that trunkline uses is eligible for listing on national register of historic places outside of town highway follows duck lake road again past county seat of leelanau county near intersection with horn road m 204 turns southeasterly toward village of suttons bay as trunkline descends a hill into village it passes through vineyards and woods inside town m 204 follows race street and ends downtown at m 22 st joseph avenue like other state highways in michigan m 204 is maintained by michigan department of transportation mdot in 2011 department s traffic surveys showed that on average 3 397 vehicles used highway daily in suttons bay and 2 931 vehicles did so each day near western terminus highest and lowest counts along highway respectively no section of m 204 is listed on national highway system a network of roads important to country s economy defense and mobility history m 204 was first designated on july 12 1933 between leland and suttons bay along what is today duck lake road highway was paved by middle of 1936 road was realigned on july 13 1939 across narrows of lake leelanau in village of same name another realignment near suttons bay was completed on march 26 1956 to smooth out a curve in road a second completed on may 4 1956 straightened a curve by sylt road east of lake leelanau western
