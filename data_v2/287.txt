national coat of arms of singapore is heraldic symbol representing southeast asian island nation of singapore it was adopted in 1959 year singapore became self governing within british empire committee that created it headed by then deputy prime minister toh chin chye was also responsible for national flag and national anthem of singapore at centre of emblem is a red shield bearing a white crescent a new moon representing a rising young nation and five white stars representing various national ideals including multiculturalism supported by a lion and a tiger representing singapore and malaysia respectively below them is a blue ribbon inscribed with majulah singapura in gold malay for onward singapore while use of coat of arms is restricted to government symbol enjoys wide use on national currency and state decorations and appears on cover of national passport history when singapore became self governing in 1959 prime minister lee kuan yew was determined to create for new state a coat of arms among other symbols to replace coat of arms of united kingdom that had been in use until then a committee was formed to design state symbols headed by deputy prime minister toh chin chye who wanted both arms and flag of singapore to represent country s united multi ethnic society coat of arms and flag were created over a span of two months by toh and his committee a law establishing state symbols of singapore was presented to national assembly by minister of culture s rajaratnam and approved in november 1959 on 3 december 1959 state coat of arms flag and anthem were presented to people of singapore for first time toh said in a 1989 interview that when tasked with creation of state symbols apart from anthem we have to produce flag and crest and he felt that new flag of singapore should be flying next to union jack toh further stated symbolism central emblem of coat of arms is a red shield with five white stars resting above a white crescent similar to crescent and stars used on singapore flag and such other national symbols as national ensign for civilian ships red symbolises universal brotherhood and equality of man and white pervading and everlasting purity and virtue crescent represents a new moon which reflects a young nation on ascendant while five pointed stars stand for nation s ideals of democracy peace progress justice and
