douglas albert munro october 11 1919 september 27 1942 was a united states coast guardsman who was posthumously decorated with medal of honor for an act of extraordinary heroism during world war ii he is only person to have received medal for actions performed during service in coast guard munro was born in canada to an american father and a british mother and his family moved to united states when he was a child he was raised in south cle elum washington and attended central washington college of education before volunteering for military service shortly before united states entered world war ii munro and his shipmate raymond evans were known as gold dust twins so called because they were inseparable u s coast guard historian s office gold dust twins during guadalcanal campaign munro was assigned to naval operating base cactus at lunga point where small boat operations were coordinated at second battle of matanikau in september 1942 he led extrication of a force of marines whose position had been overrun by japanese forces he died of a gunshot wound at age 22 while using higgins boat that he was piloting to shield a landing craft filled with marines from japanese fire several
