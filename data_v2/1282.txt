anne rice born howard allen frances o brien october 4 1941 is an american author of gothic fiction christian literature and erotic literature she is perhaps best known for her series of novels vampire chronicles revolving around central character of lestat books from vampire chronicles were subject of two film adaptations interview with vampire in 1994 and queen of damned in 2002 born in new orleans rice spent much of her early life there before moving to texas and later to san francisco she was raised in an observant catholic family but became an agnostic as a young adult she began her professional writing career with publication of interview with vampire in 1976 while living in california and began writing sequels to novel in 1980s in mid 2000s following a publicized return to catholicism rice published novels christ lord out of egypt and christ lord road to cana fictionalized accounts of certain incidents in life of jesus several years later she distanced herself from organized christianity citing disagreement with church s stances on social issues but pledging that faith in god remained central to her life however she now considers herself a secular humanist rice s books have sold nearly 100 million copies placing her among most popular authors in recent american history while reaction to her early works was initially mixed she became more popular with critics and readers in 1980s her writing style and literary content of her works have been deeply analyzed by literary commentators she was married to poet and painter stan rice for 41 years from 1961 until his death from brain cancer in 2002 at age 60 she and stan had two children michele who died of leukemia at age five and christopher who is also an author in addition to her vampire novels rice has authored books such as feast of all saints adapted for television in 2001 and servant of bones which formed basis of a 2011 comic book miniseries several books from vampire chronicles have been adapted as comics and manga by various publishers rice has also authored erotic fiction under pen names anne rampling and a n roquelaure including exit to eden which was later adapted into a 1994 film
