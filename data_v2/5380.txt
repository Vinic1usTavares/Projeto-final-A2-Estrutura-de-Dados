is part of a4 a main road running west from inner london road s name comes from old english strond meaning edge of a river as it historically ran alongside north bank of river thames street was popular with british upper classes between 12th and 17th centuries with many historically important mansions being built between strand and river these included essex house arundel house somerset house savoy palace durham house and cecil house aristocracy moved to west end over 17th century following which strand became well known for coffee shops restaurants and taverns street was a centre
